## Overview
**Vijay** is an Indian film actor who founded the political party Tamilaga Vettrium Kazhagam (TVK). In the 2026 Tamil Nadu assembly elections, TVK secured a majority and Vijay was sworn in as Chief Minister on May 10, 2026.

## Key Facts
- **Role:** President of TVK (Tamilaga Vettrium Kazhagam)
- **Sworn in:** May 10, 2026 as CM of Tamil Nadu
- **Political positioning:** Anti-DMK, anti-Congress, regional Tamil nationalist platform
- **Party founded:** TVK (Tamilaga Vettrium Kazhagam)
- **Assembly majority:** Won sufficient seats to form government

## Analysis (Caveman Style)
The DMK thought they had neutralized Vijay the same way they neutralized all previous film-star politicians — character assassination, CBI/ED raids, and political martyrdom. They were wrong. The 2026 election result proves the DMK's old playbook doesn't work anymore.

Vijay represents something genuinely new in Tamil Nadu politics: a film star who successfully made the transition from popular entertainer to chief minister. Rajinikanth couldn't do it. Kamal Haasan couldn't do it. Vijay actually did it. This changes the political map of Tamil Nadu significantly.

The TVK manifesto was a serious document — it had actual policy proposals, not just cinema-style vague nationalism. It called out the DMK's corruption directly, proposed concrete welfare measures, and positioned itself as the true defender of Tamil identity against both BJP's Hindi imposition AND DMK's family politics.

## BJP vs TVK Dynamic
- TVK and BJP are not allies but also not direct enemies in the traditional sense
- Vijay has been critical of both DMK and Congress while maintaining a complex relationship with the BJP
- The BJP's inability to field a credible CM face in Tamil Nadu meant it couldn't position itself as an alternative to DMK — TVK filled that vacuum
- In some constituencies, anti-DMK votes may have split between TVK and BJP, potentially affecting DMK's performance

## Manifesto Comparison (BJP vs TVK 2026)
The TVK manifesto was discussed as being more concrete and grounded than the BJP's usual welfare nationalism:
- TVK: Specific anti-corruption measures, direct cash transfers, Tamil-first policies
- BJP: Hindutva platform, central government schemes, national security
- Result: Vijay won; BJP remained a junior partner at best even with AIADMK alliance
