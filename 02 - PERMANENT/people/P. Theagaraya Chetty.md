# P. Theagaraya Chetty

## For future Claude
P. Theagaraya Chetty (1852–1925) was the first president and organizational founder of the [[Justice Party]]. A businessman from the Kamma (Telugu Balija Naidu) community, he built the party structure and raised funds. Died in 1925 before Periyar's takeover — his contribution was purely organizational, without the rationalist critique that Periyar later brought. Part of the founding troika with T. M. Nair and C. Natesa Mudaliar.

## Overview
**P. Theagaraya Chetty** was the first president and founding organizer of the [[Justice Party]]. A businessman from the Kamma community (Telugu Balija Naidu), he provided the organizational foundation — party structure, funding, and network building. Died in 1925, before Periyar's radical transformation of the party's ideology.

## Primary Contributions

### Organizational Foundation
Theagaraya Chetty built the Justice Party's organizational structure — recruiting members, raising funds, establishing networks across non-Brahmin communities in Madras Presidency. His business background provided both the capital and the connections needed to sustain an independent political organization.

### First President
As first president, he set the party's initial direction: political representation for non-Brahmins within the constitutional framework of British India, not the radical social revolution that Periyar would later advocate.

## Historical Context
His death in 1925 (before Periyar joined in 1938) means his legacy is purely organizational. The intellectual and ideological transformation — extending anti-Brahminism into a full rationalist critique of Hinduism, women's rights, and Dravidian identity — was Periyar's contribution. Theagaraya Chetty's Justice Party was a caste-association of landed gentry and professionals; Periyar transformed it into a mass movement.
