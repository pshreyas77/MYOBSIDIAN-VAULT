# T. M. Nair

## For future Claude
T. M. Nair (1868–1926) was the co-founder of the [[Justice Party]] who named the party and articulated the case for non-Brahmin political representation in constitutional terms. Medical doctor from the Nair (Thiyya) community. His intellectual contribution — the name "Justice Party" — proved more enduring than his short 8-year tenure (died 1926). Part of the founding troika with P. Theagaraya Chetty and C. Natesa Mudaliar.

---

## Overview
**T. M. Nair** (full name likely Thottathil Mukkur Nair) was a Malayali Nair (Thiyya) medical doctor and co-founder of the [[Justice Party]]. He was instrumental in naming the party — his intellectual contribution that symbolized the demand for social justice. Died in 1926, before Periyar's takeover transformed the party's ideology.

## Primary Contributions

### Naming the Justice Party
Nair's key intellectual contribution was naming the party "Justice Party" — a deliberate symbol of the demand for social justice and political representation for non-Brahmin communities. The name outlasted his life and became the party's lasting identity.

### Constitutional Argument for Communal Representation
Nair articulated the case for non-Brahmin political share in constitutional terms — framing the demand for representation as a matter of justice and fairness within the British constitutional framework, not as radical social revolution. This distinguished the early Justice Party from Periyar's later radical transformation.
