# Dravidar Kazhagam

## For future Claude
Core distillation of [[Dravidar Kazhagam]] (DK, 1944–1949) — the organization Periyar transformed from the [[Justice Party]], explicitly opposing electoral politics and focusing on pure social reform. Split in 1949 to form the [[Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam]] (DMK) under Annadurai, which entered electoral politics and won Madras State in 1967. The DK remains a non-electoral social reform organization to this day.

---

## Overview
**Dravidar Kazhagam** (DK) was formed in 1944 when Periyar reconstituted the [[Justice Party]]. The name change signaled: mass movement, Dravidian identity, social transformation. Periyar explicitly rejected electoral politics, wanting a pure social reform movement. In 1949, the party split — Annadurai and Karunanidhi broke away to form the [[Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam]] (DMK), which entered electoral politics and won Madras State in 1967.

---

## Formation (1944)

### Why the Name Change?
- "Justice Party" had become associated with caste-based politics of elites
- "Dravidar Kazhagam" (DK) signaled: mass movement, Dravidian identity, social transformation
- Periyar explicitly rejected "Justice" as too reformist — wanted revolution

### Transformation from Justice Party
| Justice Party (1916–1944) | Dravidar Kazhagam (1944–) |
|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| Caste-association of landed gentry | Mass social reform movement |
| Electoral politics | No electoral participation |
| Moderate anti-Brahminism | Radical rationalist critique |
| Political representation | Social transformation |

---

## 1944 Party Constitution (DK)
- Organized around **rationalism**, **anti-superstition**, **women's equality**
- Explicitly opposed **varnashrama dharma** (caste hierarchy)
- Demanded **separation of religion from state**
- Advocated **Dravidian federalism** against centralized Hindi-imposing state

---

## Periyar's Vision
Periyar opposed electoral participation entirely — wanted pure social reform without the compromises of political power. He believed:
- Electoral politics would dilute the rationalist mission
- Social transformation must come through changing minds, not winning elections
- Religion and politics must be kept separate from each other

This vision put him in direct conflict with those who wanted to contest elections under the DK banner.

---

## The 1949 Split

### Cause
M. R. Radhakrishnan and others wanted to contest elections under DK banner. Periyar opposed — wanted pure social reform movement. The fundamental disagreement over electoral participation caused the split.

### Outcome
- **Annadurai and Karunanidhi** broke away to form **Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK)**
- DMK entered electoral politics
- DK remained a non-electoral social reform organization

### DMK's Electoral Success
- Won Madras State in **1967** — end of Congress dominance in Tamil Nadu
- DMK and its offshoot AIADMK dominated Tamil Nadu politics for decades
- The Dravidian political tradition Periyar spawned outlasted his anti-electoral stance

---

## Legacy

### DK Today
The original Dravidar Kazhagam continues as a non-electoral social reform organization under Periyar's principles. It maintains the rationalist, anti-superstition, anti-caste stance of Periyar's vision.

### DMK Political Lineage
- DMK (1949–) → AIADMK (1972 split) → continuing Dravidian political tradition
- Anti-Congress stance in Tamil Nadu politics
- Dravidian identity as political force
- Social justice and reservation policies

### Periyar's Paradox
Periyar created an anti-electoral movement that spawned one of India's most successful electoral political traditions. His rationalist, anti-caste, women's rights ideology shaped Tamil Nadu's political culture even as he refused electoral politics himself.
