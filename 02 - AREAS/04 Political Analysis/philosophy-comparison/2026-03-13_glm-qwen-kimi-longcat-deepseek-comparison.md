Now I have solid data on all five models. Let me put together a comprehensive comparison.Here's the full breakdown for research and analysis specifically:

**DeepSeek V3.2 is the strongest overall pick.** It performs comparably to GPT-5 on reasoning benchmarks, and its high-compute variant Speciale surpasses GPT-5, achieving reasoning proficiency on par with Gemini-3.0-Pro — including gold-medal performance in the 2025 IMO and IOI. It also has hybrid thinking + tool-use in one model, which is exactly what you need for research. Quality is comparable to Claude Sonnet 4.6 but at a fraction of the cost — 10–30x cheaper on the API.

**Qwen3.5 is the best runner-up, especially for multilingual work.** The latest Qwen3.5 has 397 billion parameters and native multimodal capabilities enabling it to understand text, images, and video simultaneously — and supports 201 languages and dialects. It outperformed Claude 4.5 Opus on IFBench, a key instruction-following test. The 256K context (extendable to 1M) is excellent for long documents.

**Kimi K2.5 wins on agentic/web research specifically.** It can self-direct an agent swarm with up to 100 sub-agents executing parallel workflows across up to 1,500 tool calls, reducing execution time by up to 4.5x compared to a single-agent setup. On BrowseComp (web research benchmark) it outperformed GPT-5.2 Pro, and on WideSearch it outperformed Claude Opus 4.5. If your research involves synthesizing many sources simultaneously, K2.5 is uniquely powerful.

**GLM-4.7 is primarily coding-optimized**, not research-optimized. It's designed for development environments involving longer task cycles, frequent tool use, and multi-step coding tasks — solid for code-heavy analysis, but Qwen and DeepSeek run deeper on general research tasks.

**LongCat Flash-Thinking** is the dark horse — great for tool-calling tasks and very cheap, with an innovative Re-thinking Mode that simultaneously activates 8 parallel reasoning paths for thorough decision-making. But it lags behind the others on raw reasoning depth.

**Bottom line for you, Sunny**: If you're doing data analysis/ML research work, **DeepSeek V3.2** gives you the best reasoning at the lowest cost. If you're doing multi-source literature synthesis or industry analysis, try **Kimi K2.5**'s agent mode. For any multilingual or document-heavy work, **Qwen3.5** is hard to beat.
