Quick answer
•  Beyonder placed in DC/Vertigo → becomes “just another cosmic” and drops below Lucifer, Michael, Great Evil Beast, Empty Hand, and the Presence.
•  Lucifer placed in Marvel → is still second only to the One-Above-All; he steam-rolls 99 % of the Marvel hierarchy and only stalemates or narrowly loses to Pre-Retcon Beyonder.

1.  Beyonder inside DC / Vertigo
Factor	Effect

Authority hierarchy	Falls under the Source, Monitor-Mind, and Presence.
Power ceiling	High 1-A at best; no longer millions of times larger than a multiverse.
Relative rank	Below Lucifer, Michael, Great Evil Beast, Thought Robot, Empty Hand, and the Presence.

Factor	Effect

Authority hierarchy	Second only to the One-Above-All; above Living Tribunal, Eternity, etc.
Power ceiling	1-A+ to Boundless; can step outside the page and erase abstracts.

Bottom line

### 🤖 Claude

You're presenting an interesting cross-universe scaling argument. Your analysis hinges on how each character's power source and cosmic authority would translate between DC/Vertigo and Marvel cosmologies.

Your key points seem sound within powerscaling frameworks:

**Beyonder in DC/Vertigo**: Without Marvel's "Beyond-Realm" as his native domain, he loses the foundational aspect that made him "millions of times" larger than a multiverse. DC's established hierarchy (Presence, Source, Monitor-Mind) would likely place him below entities like Lucifer and Michael, who have inherent demiurgic authority.

**Lucifer in Marvel**: His power stems from fundamental demiurgic abilities and relationship to the Overvoid/Source, which wouldn't be diminished by Marvel's cosmology. He'd slot in naturally below the One-Above-All but above entities like the Living Tribunal.

The community consensus you're referencing aligns with this - that power sources matter more than raw feats when transplanting characters. Lucifer's authority-based power translates better across cosmologies than Beyonder's realm-dependent supremacy.

One potential counterargument might be that Pre-Retcon Beyonder's reality-warping transcended his specific cosmology, but your point about losing his "beyond the multiverse" status in DC's more structured hierarchy is compelling.

This kind of analysis really highlights how much character power levels depend on their native cosmic frameworks rather than just isolated feats.
