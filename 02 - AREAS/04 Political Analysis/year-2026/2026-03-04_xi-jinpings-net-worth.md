### 🌍 Most Powerful People in the World (2025)

**1. Donald Trump — U.S. President**
Trump holds the top spot on most political power rankings. The U.S. leads global defense spending, accounting for over 60% of total worldwide expenditure, and the presidency gives him decisive influence over global security, trade, and the dollar-based financial system.

**2. Xi Jinping — President of China**
Xi heads the Chinese Communist Party, commands the military as chair of the Central Military Commission, and steers the world's second-largest economy and largest manufacturing base. China's AI and chip strategies give him outsized leverage in both diplomacy and geopolitics.

**3. Elon Musk — Tech mogul & DOGE head**
Musk runs Tesla and SpaceX, is executive chair of X, and shapes industries from space travel to AI. He's currently the richest person in the world, and his proximity to the Trump administration has added a political dimension to his already enormous influence.

**4. Vladimir Putin — President of Russia**
Putin heads a nuclear-armed state with a permanent U.N. Security Council seat. Despite economic sanctions, his decisions — like the war in Ukraine — can destabilize entire regions.

**5. Narendra Modi — Prime Minister of India**
Modi leads the world's most populous democracy and one of the fastest-growing major economies. India's growing defense spending, tech sector, and diplomatic weight in forums like the G20 and the Quad make him a critical global actor.

**6. Mohammed bin Salman (MBS) — Saudi Crown Prince**
As the architect of Saudi economic reforms and custodian of a large share of global oil output, his decisions on oil production and regional security matter enormously to energy markets worldwide.

**7. Jensen Huang — CEO of Nvidia**
The world's most valuable companies and largest economies are competing to buy what Huang sells — AI chips — and they want more than he can produce. Nvidia became the most valuable company on U.S. exchanges, making Huang arguably the most powerful business figure alive.

**8. Jerome Powell — U.S. Federal Reserve Chair**
Powell arguably holds more sway over global finance than any Wall Street CEO. By setting interest rates and steering monetary policy, he affects inflation, employment, and currency values worldwide.

---

### The bottom line
There's no single answer. **Trump** holds the most powerful political office. **Xi** commands the most people and the largest military. **Musk** has the most cross-domain influence spanning business, tech, and now government. **Putin** is the most destabilizing force. The real answer depends on how you define power.
