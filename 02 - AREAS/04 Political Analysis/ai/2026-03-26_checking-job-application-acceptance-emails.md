# Checking job application acceptance emails

> **Conversation Overview**

The person, Sunny, asked Claude to check their Gmail inbox for any job application responses to determine if they had been accepted anywhere. Claude searched Gmail using a broad subject-line query covering terms like "application," "offer," "interview," "hired," "selected," "congratulations," "shortlisted," "rejected," "position," "opportunity," and "recruitment," retrieving up to 30 results. Claude then read the full content of several high-priority emails to assess their actual meaning.

The search revealed that Sunny has applied to multiple Data Analyst roles across platforms including Naukri Campus, Internshala, hirist.tech, LinkedIn, Indeed, Capgemini, and Anblicks. Claude identified and categorized the emails into three groups: application confirmations (Capgemini, Anblicks, hirist.tech, Sajre Edutech via LinkedIn, and several Indeed roles), a status change notification from Naukri Campus requiring direct login to view, and misleading Internshala emails whose subjects implied acceptance but were actually marketing emails for a campus ambassador program. Claude concluded that no confirmed job offer or interview invitation had arrived yet and recommended Sunny log into Naukri Campus to check the status change.

**Tool Knowledge**

For Gmail searches, using a broad `subject:(keyword OR keyword OR ...)` query with `gmail_search_messages` and `maxResults` set to 30 worked well for casting a wide net across job-related emails. Following up with `gmail_read_message` on specific message IDs was necessary to distinguish genuine application responses from misleading marketing emails with deceptive subject lines. One email (Naukri Campus) did not render its body content properly due to a template/rendering issue, meaning the actual status change could not be determined from the email alone — direct platform login was the recommended workaround in such cases.
