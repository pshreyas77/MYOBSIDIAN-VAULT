## Key Notes

**Methodological Challenges:**
- Ancient religions (Egyptian, Sumerian, Greek, Celtic) evolved gradually with no single founder
- Dating depends on: archaeological evidence, written texts, or traditional claims
- Some sources claim Hinduism is 9,000 years old based on spiritual tradition, but scholarly consensus places Vedas at ~1500 BCE 

**Extinct vs. Living:**
- Ancient Egyptian, Greek, Roman, Celtic, and Mesopotamian religions are **extinct** (though some revival movements exist)
- Hinduism, Zoroastrianism, and Judaism are the **three oldest continuously practiced religions** 

**Largest Religions Today:**
1. Christianity (~2.4 billion)
2. Islam (~1.9 billion)
3. Hinduism (~1.2 billion)
4. Buddhism (~520 million)

## Animism & Shamanism: The Original Beliefs

| Practice | Estimated Origin | Nature |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| **Animism** | **~40,000+ BCE** (Paleolithic era) | Belief that spirits/souls inhabit all natural objects (animals, plants, rocks, rivers) |
| **Shamanism** | **~30,000+ BCE** (Upper Paleolithic) | Practice of entering altered states to communicate with spirits; healing and divination |

### Why They Precede All Organized Religions

**Animism** is considered the **original human spirituality**—the belief system of hunter-gatherer societies before agriculture, cities, or written language. Evidence includes:

- **Cave paintings** (~40,000–15,000 BCE): Lascaux, Chauvet, Altamira depicting animals with spiritual significance
- **Burial practices** (~100,000+ BCE): Neanderthal and early human graves with grave goods suggest belief in afterlife
- **Venus figurines** (~28,000–25,000 BCE): Fertility and mother goddess worship
- **Totemism and animal spirits**: Belief that humans have spiritual connections to animals

**Shamanism** emerged as a specialized practice within animistic cultures:

- **Siberian origins**: The word "shaman" comes from Tungusic (Siberian) language
- **Global distribution**: Found in indigenous cultures worldwide (Native American, Siberian, Amazonian, African, Australian Aboriginal, Southeast Asian)
- **Core practice**: The shaman enters trance states (through drumming, dancing, or entheogens) to journey between worlds, heal sickness, and communicate with spirits

### Key Distinctions

| Feature | Animism | Shamanism | Organized Religion |
|---------|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Structure** | Decentralized, universal | Specialized role (shaman) | Institutional, hierarchical |
| **Text** | Oral traditions only | Oral traditions only | Sacred scriptures |
| **Gods** | Spirits in nature; no supreme deity | Spirit world communication | Often supreme deity/deities |
| **Practice** | Daily life integrated | Ritual trance, healing | Formal worship, doctrine |
| **Founder** | None | None | Often specific founder |

### Survival Today

**Animistic/Shamanic practices persist in:**
- Indigenous communities worldwide (Amazon, Siberia, Sub-Saharan Africa, Pacific Islands)
- **Neo-shamanism**: Modern Western revival (Michael Harner's "Core Shamanism," 1980s)
- Syncretic forms: Voodoo (Haiti), Santería (Cuba), Candomblé (Brazil) blend African animism with Christianity
- Folk Buddhism, Hinduism, and Christianity often retain animistic elements (spirit houses, ancestor worship, nature spirits)

## What Is the Oldest Language? (Multiple Answers)

The question has **three different answers** depending on what you mean by "oldest":

| Definition | Answer | Language | Date |
|------------|--------|----------|------|
| **Oldest written language** | **Sumerian** | Cuneiform script | ~3400–3100 BCE  |
| **Oldest complete written sentences** | **Egyptian** | Hieroglyphics | ~2690–2600 BCE  |
| **Oldest language still spoken today** | **Tamil** | Spoken by ~78 million people | ~300 BCE (written); possibly 2500 BCE (Proto-Dravidian)  |

---

## 1. Oldest Written Language: Sumerian (~3400–3100 BCE)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Location** | Mesopotamia (modern southern Iraq) |
| **Script** | Cuneiform (wedge-shaped marks on clay tablets) |
| **Status** | **Extinct** as spoken language (~2000 BCE); used for religious/scholarly purposes until 1st century CE  |
| **Significance** | Language isolate (no known relatives); invented writing system  |

> The Sumerians developed cuneiform to record administrative documents, trade, and epic poems like the *Epic of Gilgamesh* .

---

## 2. Oldest Language Still Spoken Today: Tamil (~300 BCE–2500 BCE)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Speakers** | ~78 million worldwide  |
| **Location** | Tamil Nadu (India), Sri Lanka, Singapore, Malaysia |
| **Written records** | ~300 BCE (Tamil-Brahmi inscriptions)  |
| **Possible origin** | Proto-Dravidian ~2500 BCE (linguistic reconstruction)  |
| **Status** | **Still spoken**; recognized as classical language by UNESCO  |

**Why Tamil wins for "oldest living":**
- Continuous spoken and written tradition for **2,000+ years** 
- Grammar and structure largely unchanged since ancient times 
- Unlike Sanskrit (also ancient), Tamil remained a **living, everyday language** 

> *"Tamil is considered to be the longest-surviving classical language in the world"* 

---

## 3. The Sanskrit vs. Tamil Debate

| Language | Claimed Age | Reality |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Sanskrit** | Often claimed ~5000 BCE | **Vedic Sanskrit: ~1500 BCE** (Rig Veda)  |
| **Tamil** | ~300 BCE (written); ~2500 BCE (proto) | **Earlier written attestation than Sanskrit** |

**The confusion:** Sanskrit was long considered the oldest Indian language due to its religious prestige, but **Tamil has earlier written evidence** and **never died out as a spoken language** .

> *"Recent evidence points to Tamil"* as older than Sanskrit when considering continuous usage
