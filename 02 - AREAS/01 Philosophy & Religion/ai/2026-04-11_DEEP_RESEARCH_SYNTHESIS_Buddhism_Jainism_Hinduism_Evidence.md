## Part III: SAM DAM DAND BHED - Brahmanical Strategy Framework

### The Four-Fold Policy

| Strategy | Sanskrit | Meaning | Application to Buddhism |
|----------|----------|---------|------------------------|
| **Sam** | सम | Conciliation/Assimilation | Buddha made Vishnu avatar; ahimsa, vegetarianism adopted |
| **Dam** | दाम | Bribery/Economic Pressure | Funds diverted from Buddhist to Hindu institutions |
| **Dand** | दंड | Punishment/Violence | Pushyamitra's monastery destruction; later temple conversions |
| **Bhed** | भेद | Division/Divide & Rule | Caste system to fragment Buddhist egalitarian community |

### Historical Implementation

**Sam (Assimilation)**:
- Buddha → 9th avatar of Vishnu
- Buddhist ahimsa → Hindu vegetarianism
- Buddhist monasticism → Hindu sannyasa tradition
- Buddhist critique of sacrifice → Reduced Brahmanical animal sacrifice

**Dam (Economic)**:
- Withdrawal of royal patronage from viharas
- Economic incentives for conversion
- Redirecting temple funds

**Dand (Punishment)**:
- Pushyamitra Shunga (185-149 BCE): Destroyed Buddhist sites
- Adi Shankaracharya (8th c.): Intellectual defeat, temple conversions
- Gahadavala Rajputs (11th-12th c.): Converted Sarnath to Shiva temple

**Bhed (Division)**:
- Hardening caste hierarchy
- Graded inequality to prevent unified Buddhist revolt
- Keeping "untouchables" divided

## Part IV: ASHOKA'S PRAKRIT INSCRIPTIONS

### Language Evidence

| Aspect | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| **Language Used** | Prakrit (Middle Indo-Aryan), specifically Magadhi |
| **Script** | Brahmi (majority), Kharoshthi (northwest) |
| **NOT Used** | Sanskrit |
| **Time Period** | 268-232 BCE |

### Critical Distinction

| Buddhism (Ashoka) | Brahmanism |
|------------------|------------|
| **Prakrit** (people's language) | **Sanskrit** (elite language) |
| Accessible to masses | Restricted to Brahmins |
| Anti-elitist populism | Hierarchical control |
| Written in local dialects | Pan-India "refined" language |

> "Using Prakrit was a conscious populist, anti-elitist choice" — source verification

### Archaeological Proof

- **Major Rock Edicts**: Prakrit in Brahmi script
- **Minor Rock Edicts**: Local vernaculars
- **Northwestern Edicts**: Kharoshthi (not Sanskrit)
- **Western Edicts**: Prakrit (corresponds to Pali)
