# Anatman (No-Self)

**School**: Buddhism (all traditions)  
**Opposite**: [[./Atman]]  
**Contrast**: [[./Jiva]] (Jainism), [[./Brahman]] (Hinduism)  
**Status**: #concept #core-doctrine #buddhist-fundamental

---

## Definition

**Anatman** (Pali: *anatta*) literally means "no-self." It is the central Buddhist doctrine that denies the existence of a permanent, unchanging, independent self or soul.

---

## The Five Aggregates (Skandhas)

Buddha challenged seekers: "Find the self among these five." Each is impermanent (anicca), unsatisfactory (dukkha), and not-self (anatta).

1. **Rupa (Form)**: Physical body, matter
2. **Vedana (Feelings)**: Pleasant, unpleasant, neutral sensations
3. **Samjna (Perception)**: Recognition, labeling
4. **Samskara (Mental Formations)**: Volition, habits, conditioning
5. **Vijnana (Consciousness)**: Awareness, cognizing

**None = self** ✓ All conditioned, all arising/ceasing

---

## Debate Applications

### vs. Atman (Shankara)
**Shankara's Attack**: "Who knows the five skandhas are five? Who registers impermanence? That witness = Atman."

**Buddha's Defense**: "The witness is also the witnessed — consciousness is not-self. Consciousness arises, not a seer separate from seeing."

**Verdict**: Buddha wins on burden of proof — "Show me the self" ≠ found

### vs. Jiva (Mahavira)
**Mahavira**: "Jiva = real, eternal, individual soul. Liberation = soul's release."

**Buddha**: "If soul is permanent, it cannot change (be bound/liberated). If it changes, it is not permanent. Contradiction."

**Verdict**: Buddhism internally consistent. Jainism stuck with eternal-but-changing soul.

---

## Philosophical Implications

### Liberation (Nirvana)
- If no self, then **no self to liberate**
- Liberation = cessation of craving, not "someone becomes free"
- Process, not achievement

### Moral Responsibility
- **Challenge**: "If no self, who acts?"
- **Answer**: Karma operates through dependent origination — causes and effects traverse the stream, no carrier needed

### Contrast with Western Philosophy
- **Hume**: Bundle theory of self — similar to Buddha
- **Descartes**: Cogito = Atman-like
- **Wittgenstein**: Private language argument — no Cartesian theater
