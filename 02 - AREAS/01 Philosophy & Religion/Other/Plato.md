# Plato

**Born**: ~428 BCE, Athens  
**Died**: ~348 BCE  
**Founded**: Academy — first university  
**Status**: #entity #philosopher

---

## Philosophy

### Theory of Forms
- Physical world = shadows of eternal perfect Forms
- Form of Beauty, Justice, Good = more real than physical
- Philosopher-King = ruler who sees Forms

### Cave Allegory
- Prisoners in cave see shadows, think they're real
- Philosopher escapes, sees sun (Forms)
- Returns to help prisoners — they reject him

---

## Comparison: Plato vs Shankara

| Criterion | Plato | Shankara |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Ultimate reality | Forms (eternal, perfect) | Brahman (pure consciousness) |
| Physical world | Shadows/cave | Maya (appearance) |
| Knowledge | Recollection | Recognition |
| Method | Dialectic | Viveka (discrimination) |
| Politics | Philosopher-Kings | Brahmin-Kshatriya |

**Structural identity**: Forms = Brahman. Physical world = Maya. Dialectic = Viveka.

**Key difference**: Plato's Forms = plural. Shankara's Brahman = strictly non-dual.

---

## Criticism

**Whitehead**: "All Western philosophy = footnotes to Plato."

**Reality**: Forms theory destroyed by Aristotle and Nagarjuna. Philosopher-King = dangerous authoritarian idea.

**Nagarjuna's critique**: "Do Forms exist by svabhava?" — destroys Forms in 30 seconds.
