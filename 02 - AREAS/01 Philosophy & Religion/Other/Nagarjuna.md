# Nagarjuna

**Dates**: c. 150–250 CE  
**School**: Madhyamaka (Middle Way) Buddhism  
**Core Doctrine**: Śūnyatā (Emptiness)  
**Status**: #entity #philosopher #rank-1-debater

---

## Biography

Nagarjuna was the founder of the Madhyamaka school of Mahayana Buddhism. His seminal work, *Mūlamadhyamakakārikā* (MMK), systematically destroyed all metaphysical positions using the **Prasanga** (reductio ad absurdum) method.

---

## Core Philosophy

### The Doctrine of Emptiness (Shunyata)
- All phenomena (*dhammas*) lack **svabhava** (inherent self-existence)
- Emptiness does not mean "nothingness" — it means dependent origination
- **Sunyata-sunyata**: Even emptiness itself is empty

### Two Truths Doctrine
- **Samvṛti-satya** (Conventional truth): Provisional, language-based reality
- **Paramārtha-satya** (Ultimate truth): No doctrine — cessation of conceptual proliferation

### Prasanga Method
- Never asserts positive doctrines
- Only destroys opponent positions using their own logic
- Unfalsifiable by design — nothing to attack

### Tetralemma (Catuskoti)
Nagarjuna's fourfold negation applied to any claim:
1. X exists? ❌
2. X does not exist? ❌
3. X both exists and does not exist? ❌
4. X neither exists nor does not exist? ❌

---

## Key Works

- **Mūlamadhyamakakārikā** (MMK): 27 chapters destroying causation, motion, time, self, nirvana
- **Shunyatasaptati**: 70 verses on emptiness
- **Vigrahavyavartani**: Defense against logical attack
- **Ratnavali**: Advice to a king

## Debate Record

**Rank**: #1 (10/10) — Unbeatable in formal debate

### Why Undefeatable
- **Prasanga method**: Never asserts, only destroys
- **Sunyata-sunyata**: Even his own doctrine is empty
- **Zero vulnerability**: Cannot attack what asserts nothing

### Key Victories
- ✅ Destroyed causation (MMK Ch. 1)
- ✅ Destroyed motion (MMK Ch. 2)
- ✅ Destroyed self (MMK Ch. 18)
- ✅ Destroyed time (MMK Ch. 19)
- ✅ Destroyed nirvana (MMK Ch. 25)

## Relationship to Other Thinkers

**Buddha**: "Emptiness" = philosophical culmination of Buddha's silence (avyakata)

**Adi Shankara**: Called Buddhism as "Prachchhanna Bauddha" (crypto-Buddhist). Shankara's Advaita absorbed Madhyamaka while opposing it rhetorically.

**Mahavira**: Anekantavada undermines all absolutes — including Nagarjuna's method

**Modern Parallels**: 
- Wittgenstein (philosophy as therapy, silence)
- Derrida (deconstruction)
- Post-structuralism
