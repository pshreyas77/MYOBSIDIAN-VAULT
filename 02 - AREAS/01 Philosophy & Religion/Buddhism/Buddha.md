# Buddha (Siddhartha Gautama)

**Dates**: c. 563–483 BCE (traditional) | c. 480–400 BCE (modern scholarly)  
**Birth**: Shakya clan, Kapilavastu (Nepal region)  
**Title**: Tathagata, Bhagavan, Shakyamuni  
**Status**: #entity #philosopher #rank-2-debater

---

## Biography

Born as **Siddhartha Gautama** to King Suddhodana and Queen Maya of the Shakya clan. At 29, left palace after encountering the **Four Sights** (old age, sickness, death, ascetic). Practiced extreme asceticism, then discovered the **Middle Path** under the Bodhi tree at Bodh Gaya. Taught for 45 years until Parinirvana at Kushinagar.

**Archaeological Evidence**: Lumbini shrine timber structure (~6th BCE) excavated by Robin Coningham (2013) — oldest Buddhist shrine, confirms period.

## Core Teachings

### Four Noble Truths
1. **Dukkha**: Life contains suffering
2. **Samudaya**: Suffering caused by craving (tanha)
3. **Nirodha**: Suffering can end
4. **Magga**: Path to end suffering = Eightfold Path

### Anatman (No-Self)
- No permanent, unchanging self exists
- Identity = five **skandhas** (aggregates)
  - Form (rupa)
  - Feelings (vedana)
  - Perception (samjna)
  - Mental formations (samskara)
  - Consciousness (vijnana)

### Pratītyasamutpāda (Dependent Origination)
- All phenomena arise dependently
- Nothing exists independently
- Causal chain: ignorance → formations → consciousness → name-form → six senses → contact → feeling → craving → clinging → becoming → birth → suffering

### Middle Path (Majjhima Patipada)
- Avoid extremes: indulgence vs. self-mortification
- Balance = clarity for liberation

### Eightfold Path
1. Right View
2. Right Intention
3. Right Speech
4. Right Action
5. Right Livelihood
6. Right Effort
7. Right Mindfulness
8. Right Concentration

### Avyakata (Unanswered Questions)
Buddha refused to answer 14 questions:
- Is the universe eternal?
- Is it finite or infinite?
- Is the Tathagata after death?
- Is the self identical to the body?

**Reason**: "They don't lead to liberation" — focused on practical ending of suffering, not metaphysical speculation.

## Debate Record

**Rank**: #2 (9/10) — Most empirically rigorous and accessible

### Matches

#### vs. Mahavira
**Winner**: Buddha (7:3)
- Anatman more logically consistent than Jiva
- Pratītyasamutpāda more rigorous than dualistic karma
- **BUT**: Mahavira wins on moral absolutism (ahimsa)

#### vs. Adi Shankara
**Winner**: Buddha
- **Avyakata** = philosophically mature defense
- **Anatman Empirical Challenge**: "Show me the self among skandhas"
- **Arrow Parable**: Practical focus defeats metaphysical speculation
- **Caste Attack**: Exposed Shankara's social contradiction
- **Avidya Ashraya Problem**: Shankara needs ignorant entity but denies it exists

### Key Weapons
1. **Empirical rigor**: "Come and see" (Ehipassiko)
2. **Epistemic humility**: Avyakata questions
3. **Pragmatic focus**: End suffering NOW
4. **Accessibility**: Path open to all — king or beggar

### Defensive Strength
- Never claimed omniscience
- Burden of proof always on opponent
- "Show me" = devastating challenge

## Comparison with Hinduism

### Contrast with Rama/Krishna
| Figure | Birth | Message |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Buddha | Royal | **Left** royalty for liberation |
| Rama | Royal | **Stayed** royal to uphold dharma |
| Krishna | Royal | **Became** royal after being cowherd |

**Key difference**: Hindu avatars born royal and stay royal. Shramana teachers born royal and abandon royalty.

---

## Relationship to Other Thinkers

**Mahavira**: Contemporary, debated directly. Pali texts record Buddha criticizing Jain karma theory.

**Adi Shankara**: 1300 years later. Built career defeating Buddhism. Called "crypto-Buddhist" by critics — ironic given his anti-Buddhist mission.

**Nagarjuna**: Perfected Buddha's silence into Prasanga method.

---

## Modern Assessment

### Why Buddhism Most Respected
1. ✅ Only major religion spread without religious war
2. ✅ Non-theistic = no God-competition
3. ✅ Science-compatible (Dalai Lama + neuroscience)
4. ✅ Empirically verifiable (meditation studies)
5. ✅ Universal ethics (Five Precepts)

### Criticisms
- Stream-of-consciousness needs grounding (Shankara's critique)
- Buddhist atheism rejects all metaphysics (Charvaka criticism: too restrictive)
- Sometimes labeled pessimistic (Dukkha focus)
