# Ancient Indian Philosophical Timeline

## Chronological Order (First to Last)

| Order | Figure | Era | Philosophy/School |
|-------|--------|-----|------------------|
| 1 | Charvaka (Ajita Kesakambali) | ~600-550 BCE | Materialism/Lokayata |
| 2 | Mahavira | ~599-527 BCE | Jainism |
| 3 | Buddha | ~563-483 BCE | Buddhism |
| 4 | Nagarjuna | ~150-250 CE | Madhyamaka Buddhism |
| 5 | Adi Shankara | ~700-788 CE | Advaita Vedanta |

## Gap Analysis

- **Charvaka → Mahavira**: ~1 year (contemporaries)
- **Mahavira → Buddha**: ~36 years (contemporaries in same region)
- **Buddha → Nagarjuna**: ~713 years
- **Nagarjuna → Adi Shankara**: ~638 years

## Key Observations

1. **Axial Age Burst**: All three heterodox founders (Charvaka, Mahavira, Buddha) emerged within ~50 years of each other in the 6th-5th century BCE
2. **Silence Gap**: ~700 years between Buddha and Nagarjuna (systematization of Buddhist philosophy)
3. **Hindu Revival**: ~500-640 years between Nagarjuna and Shankara (Vedantic response to Buddhism)

## Critical Distinctions

### Founders vs Reformers

- **Buddha**: Founded Buddhism. No pre-existing "Buddhism" existed before him.
- **Mahavira**: Systematizer of proto-Jain ascetic current. No organized "Jainism" to "revive."
- Traditional claims: 24 Tirthankaras (Jainism), 28 Buddhas (Buddhism) — theological constructs, not verified history.

### Dating Limitations

- All dates are scholarly ranges
- Ancient India lacked synchronized calendrical records until early centuries CE
- Precision beyond ±25-50 years is academically unjustified
