## Critical Clarifications

### On Founders vs Reformers

- **Buddha**: Historically founded Buddhism. No pre-existing "Buddhism" existed before him.
- **Mahavira**: Systematizer of proto-Jain ascetic current. No fully-formed "Jainism" to "revive."
- Traditional claims of 24 Tirthankaras and 28 Buddhas are theological constructs, not verified history.
- Only Parshvanatha (23rd Tirthankara) has marginal historicity (~8th-7th century BCE).

### On Dating Limitations

All dates are scholarly ranges. Ancient India lacked synchronized calendrical records until early centuries CE. Precision beyond ±25-50 years is academically unjustified for this period.

### Key Insight

The Ramayana and Mahabharata are **mythological/historical fiction**, not verified history:
- No archaeological layer matches Kurukshetra battle
- No foreign contemporary records mention these events
- Texts show later composition layers (post-Buddha/Mahavira)
- Both are oral traditions written down centuries after traditional dates

## Why No Written History from Vedic Period?

### Core Reasons

1. **No Writing System**: Vedic period (~1500-500 BCE) had no writing. Vedas transmitted orally.
2. **Oral Culture Priority**: Brahmanical tradition prioritized memorization over writing. Writing considered corrupting to sacred texts.
3. **No Indigenous Historiography**: Different intellectual priorities. Focused on ritual, philosophy, law — not chronological history.
4. **Religious vs. Secular**: Itihasa (history) was epic/mythology, not critical history.

### Archaeological Reality

- "Hinduism" as recognizable religion crystallized ~200 BCE-500 CE
- Earliest unambiguous Hindu archaeological evidence (temples, icons): post-Mauryan period
- No temples, no images, no inscriptions identifying "Hindu" practice before 2nd century CE
- IVC (2600-1900 BCE) shows no evidence of Vedic/Hindu practices

### Key Points

- IVC (2600-1900 BCE) is pre-Vedic, not Hindu
- Vedic religion (~1500-1200 BCE) dates ~1,500-2,000 years after Sumerian/Egyptian monumental architecture

## What Was Happening When Alexander Started Conquering (334 BCE)

- **Mesopotamia**: Persian satrapy, governed by Mazaeus
- **Egypt**: Persian satrapy, governed by Sabaces (later Mazaces)
- **Persian ruler**: Darius III (r. 336-330 BCE)
- Both regions were restful under Persian control, populations welcomed Alexander as liberator
