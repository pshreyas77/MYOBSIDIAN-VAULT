# Dr. B.R. Ambedkar: Why He Chose Buddhism

## Summary
**Date**: October 14, 1956, Nagpur
**Event**: Largest mass religious conversion in modern history (~600,000 Dalits)
**Death**: December 6, 1956 (6 weeks later)

**Core Thesis**: Ambedkar chose Buddhism not because it's "older" but because it is **rational, egalitarian, Indian, and structurally caste-free**.

---

## Clarifying the "Older Than Hinduism" Question

### Correct Chronology
| Tradition | Dating | Relationship to Buddhism |
|-----------|--------|-------------------------|
| **Vedic Religion** | ~1500 BCE | Older than Buddhism |
| **Upanishads** | ~800–400 BCE | Contemporary/overlap with Buddha |
| **Buddhism** | ~5th century BCE (~563–483 BCE) | Historical, dateable |
| **Modern Brahminic Hinduism** | ~300–800 CE synthesis | **Younger** than Buddhism |

**Verdict**: Vedic tradition is older. But modern temple-and-ritual Hinduism = post-Buddhist synthesis. Buddhism predates the Brahminic system Ambedkar was fighting.

---

## 5 Documented Reasons for Choosing Buddhism

### 1. Caste Rejection (Structural, Not Cosmetic)
- Buddhism denies caste at the **ontological level**
- No Brahmin superiority in Dhamma
- Sangha was historically open to all castes, women, outcasts
- This was structural liberation, not symbolic reform

### 2. Reason Over Ritual
- **Ehipassiko** — "come and see," verify yourself
- No blind faith required
- No priest monopoly on salvation
- Buddhism demands direct verification, not hereditary authority

### 3. Indian Origin
- Ambedkar **explicitly rejected** Christianity and Islam as "foreign"
- Buddhism = Indian soil, Indian founder, Indian texts
- Dalit conversion as **return**, not abandonment

### 4. Built-in Social Ethics
- Equality is **structural**, not metaphorical
- Historical evidence: Shudras and Untouchables as **former Buddhists**
- Degraded by Brahminic counter-revolution post-Gupta period (~320–550 CE)

### 5. The Historical Argument
- *The Buddha and His Dhamma* (1956)
- *Who Were the Shudras?*
- **Core claim**: Dalit conversion = **return to original faith**, not new adoption

---

## Ambedkar's Actual View

> Ambedkar didn't primarily argue "Buddhism is older."

He argued:
- Buddhism = **rational, egalitarian, ethical**
- Brahminism = **hierarchical counter-movement** that:
  1. Absorbed Buddhism (Ashoka's collapse)
  2. Required a "counter-revolution"
  3. Neutralized Buddha by absorbing him as Vishnu avatar (after 800 CE)

### Age Was Secondary
**Ethics and structure were primary.** The age argument, if mentioned, was contextual—not core to his thesis.

## The 1956 Conversion Context

### What Happened
- **October 14, 1956**: Ambedkar took 22 vows with ~600,000 followers
- **Nagpur**: Chosen because historically Buddhist region
- **6 weeks later**: He died
- **Legacy**: Neo-Buddhism (Navayana) born

### 22 Vows Summary
Key elements:
- Rejection of Hindu gods/goddesses
- Rejection of caste-based worship
- Commitment to Buddhist teachings of equality and reason

---

## Bottom Line

**Ambedkar chose Buddhism because:**
- ✅ Rational (no blind faith)
- ✅ Egalitarian (no caste)
- ✅ Indian (not foreign import)
- ✅ Structurally caste-free (not just rhetoric)

**Not because** "it's older"—that framing is your addition, not his core thesis.
