Pegasus Spyware Scandal in India (2021–2023): Accurate, Verified ReportPegasus is a military-grade spyware developed by the Israeli company NSO Group. It infects phones (iOS/Android) via zero-click exploits (no user action required) and grants full access: encrypted messages (WhatsApp, Signal), calls, emails, photos, location, camera, microphone, and keystrokes. NSO claims it sells only to vetted governments for counter-terrorism and serious crime, under Israeli export controls. Misuse has led to blacklisting (including by the US in 2021).The scandal in India centres on allegations that the Indian government (or its agencies) used Pegasus to target critics. No court has conclusively proven government use, but forensic evidence and targeting patterns strongly point to state involvement.Timeline of RevelationsJuly 2021 (Pegasus Project): Forbidden Stories + Amnesty International + 16 media outlets (including The Wire in India) published a global investigation based on a leaked NSO client database of ~50,000 phone numbers. ~300 Indian numbers appeared as potential/select targets. Forensic analysis (Amnesty Security Lab) confirmed Pegasus infections or attempts on multiple Indian phones. 

amnesty.org +2

October 2021: Supreme Court of India appoints independent Technical Committee (overseen by retired Justice R.V. Raveendran) after petitions from journalists and activists.
August 2022: Committee report submitted (partially read in court). Malware found in 5 of 29 phones examined, but not conclusively identified as Pegasus. Court noted government non-cooperation. Reports remain sealed.
