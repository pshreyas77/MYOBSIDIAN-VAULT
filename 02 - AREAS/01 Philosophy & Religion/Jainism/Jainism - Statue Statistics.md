# Jainism: Statue Statistics

## Overview

Jainism has **tens of thousands** of Tirthankara statues - concentrated primarily in India where 99%+ of the world's ~6-12 million Jains live.

---

## Global Estimates

| Region | Estimate |
|--------|----------|
| **India** | Tens of thousands |
| **Overseas** | Few hundred |
| **Total** | ~50,000-100,000 |

**Note**: No global census exists for Jain statues

## Notable Sites

### Gwalior Fort, India
- **1,500+ Jain images** carved into one cliff face
- Multiple Tirthankara statues
- One of the largest collections

### Palitana Temples, Gujarat
- **863 temples** on Shikharji mountain
- Thousands of Tirthankara statues
- Major pilgrimage site

### Shravanabelagola, Karnataka
- **Gommateshwara (Bahubali)**: 57ft monolithic statue
- Built: 983 CE
- **Largest free-standing Jain statue** until 2016

### Mount Shikharji (Parasnath)
- One of the most sacred Jain pilgrimages
- Multiple temples with thousands of statues

---

## Archaeological Evidence

### Oldest Jain Artifact
- **Lohanipur Torso** (Mauryan period)
- **Date**: 3rd century BCE
- **Location**: Near Patna (ancient Pataliputra)
- **Material**: Polished sandstone
- **Pose**: Kayotsarga (standing meditation)
- **Status**: One of the earliest confirmed Jain Tirthankara images

### Other Early Evidence
- **Mathura sculptures**: Hundreds of Jain images from Kushan period (2nd-3rd c. CE)
- **Kankali Tila**: Major Jain archaeological site

## Why Fewer Than Buddhism

| Factor | Jainism | Buddhism |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Population | ~6-12 million | ~500 million |
| Geographic spread | Concentrated in India | Pan-Asia |
| Temple scale | Smaller, focused | Massive complexes |
| Royal patronage | Regional kings | Empire-wide (Ashoka) |
