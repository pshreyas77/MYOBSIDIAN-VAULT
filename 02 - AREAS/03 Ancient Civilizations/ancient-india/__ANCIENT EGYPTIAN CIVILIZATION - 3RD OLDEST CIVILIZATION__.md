
## 📋 ESSENTIAL FACTS

**📍 Location:** Nile River Valley, Egypt  
**📅 Age:** 8,000 years old (6000–30 BCE)  
**DYNASTIC PERIOD:** 3100–332 BCE (2,768 years)  
**🏛️ Type:** Longest-lasting continuous civilization  
**🔬 Dating Method:** Radiocarbon dating, dendrochronology, historical records  
**🏆 UNESCO Status:** Multiple World Heritage Sites

---

## **⚡ KEY FACTS (Minimal)**

✓ **World's longest continuous civilization** (over 3,000 years)  
✓ **Unification by King Narmer/Menes** ~3100 BCE  
✓ **31 dynasties** of pharaohs  
✓ **Great Pyramid of Giza** (2580–2560 BCE) - only surviving wonder  
✓ **Hieroglyphic writing system** (3200 BCE)  
✓ **First solar calendar** (365 days)  
✓ **Mummification** & afterlife beliefs  
✓ **Pyramid Texts** (2400–2300 BCE) - oldest religious texts  
✓ **Advanced medicine, mathematics, engineering**  
✓ **Papyrus** invention (writing material)

---

## **📏 PHYSICAL DETAILS**

- **Territory:** 1,000+ km along Nile River  
- **Peak population:** 3–5 million (New Kingdom)  
- **Major cities:** Memphis, Thebes, Alexandria, Giza  
- **Great Pyramid height:** 146.6 meters (original)  
- **Great Pyramid stones:** 2.3 million blocks  
- **Dynasties:** 31 (Early Dynastic to Ptolemaic)  
- **Writing:** Hieroglyphics, Hieratic, Demotic

## **🔬 SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE**

**Dating Methods:**
- Radiocarbon dating (organic materials)
- Dendrochronology (wooden artifacts)
- Stratigraphy (layer analysis)
- Historical king lists (Manetho, Palermo Stone)
- Astronomical alignments

**Key Dates Verified:**
- **Predynastic Period:** 6000–3100 BCE
- **Early Dynastic:** 3100–2686 BCE
- **Old Kingdom (Pyramid Age):** 2686–2181 BCE
- **Middle Kingdom:** 2055–1650 BCE
- **New Kingdom (Empire):** 1550–1069 BCE
- **Ptolemaic Period:** 332–30 BCE

**Verification:** Multiple international labs, peer-reviewed studies

## **👑 MAJOR PHARAOHS**

**Old Kingdom:**
- Djoser (2686–2649 BCE) - Step Pyramid builder
- Khufu (2589–2566 BCE) - Great Pyramid
- Khafre (2558–2532 BCE) - Sphinx builder

**Middle Kingdom:**
- Mentuhotep II (2055–2004 BCE) - Reunifier
- Senusret III (1878–1839 BCE)

**New Kingdom:**
- Hatshepsut (1479–1458 BCE) - Female pharaoh
- Thutmose III (1479–1425 BCE) - Napoleon of Egypt
- Akhenaten (1353–1336 BCE) - Monotheistic reform
- Tutankhamun (1332–1323 BCE) - Boy king
- Ramses II (1279–1213 BCE) - Greatest builder
- Cleopatra VII (51–30 BCE) - Last pharaoh
