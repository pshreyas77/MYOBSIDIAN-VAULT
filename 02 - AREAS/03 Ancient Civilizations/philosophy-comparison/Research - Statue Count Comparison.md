# Global Statue Count: All Traditions Comparison

## Quick Summary

| Tradition | Estimated Global Statues | Best Single-Site Record |
|-----------|------------------------|------------------------|
| **Buddhism** | Millions | 97,000+ (Longmen Grottoes) |
| **Jainism** | Tens of thousands | 1,500+ (Gwalior cliff) |
| **Daoism** | Tens of thousands | 16,319 (Taiwan temple) |
| **Confucianism** | Dozens to hundreds | 1 (72m tallest) |
| **Shinto** | Near zero | 0 (no kami statues) |

---

## Buddhism: By Far the Most

### Estimated Total
- **Millions worldwide** - no census exists
- Thailand alone: ~40,000+ temples, many with dozens to hundreds each

### Notable Collections
- **Longmen Grottoes, China**: 97,000+ Buddhist statues
- **One temple, China**: 3,000 Buddha statues in single hall
- **Otagi Nenbutsu-ji, Japan**: 1,200 statues
- **Spring Temple Buddha**: 128m tall (world's tallest)

### Why So Many?
- Anthropomorphic tradition - Buddha depicted in human form
- Mass devotion - kings funded massive construction
- Global spread across Asia

---

## Jainism: Significant Production

### Estimated Total
- **Tens of thousands** - concentrated in India
- Most Jains (~6-12 million) live in India

### Notable Sites
- **Gwalior Fort**: 1,500+ Jain images on one cliff face
- **Palitana Temples**: Hundreds of temples, thousands of statues
- **Mount Shikharji**: Major pilgrimage site
- **Gommateshwara**: 57ft monolithic Bahubali statue (Shravanabelagola)

### Key Fact
- 24 Tirthankaras - each temple typically has all 24
- Strict iconographic rules for representation

---

## Daoism: Moderate Production

### Estimated Total
- **Tens of thousands** globally
- ~1,500 temples in China
- Taiwan has significant presence

### Notable Sites
- **Sinying Taizih Temple, Taiwan**: 16,319 statues (Guinness World Record, 2016)
- Major Taoist temples contain hundreds of deity statues

### Key Fact
- Religious Taoism adds deities/rituals to philosophical base
- Laozi statues at major temples

---

## Confucianism: Originally No Images

### Estimated Total
- **Dozens to hundreds** - very few
- Traditional temples use spirit tablets, NOT statues

### Historical Context
- 1530 CE decree: All images replaced with spirit tablets
- Original principle: "Honoring the teaching, not the man"
- Only modern revival temples have statues

### Notable
- **World's tallest**: 72m brass statue in Nishan, China (2016)

### Key Fact
- Confucius specifically rejected statue worship
- Philosophy-first tradition

---

## Shinto: No Kami Statues

### Estimated Total
- **Essentially ZERO** statues of kami in active shrines

### Why No Statues?
- Kami are **invisible energy** (hikari)
- They have no fixed form
- Only symbolic objects: swords, jewels, mirrors

### What You WILL See
- **Komainu**: Guardian lion/dog statues (not kami)
- **Kitsune (Fox)**: 40,000+ at Inari shrines (messengers, not kami)
- **Shinto shrines**: ~80,000-100,000 in Japan

### Key Fact
- Most statue-free major religion in the world
- Ritual purity over imagery

## Why Buddhism Dominates

| Factor | Buddhism | Others |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Image tradition | Yes - human form | Partial/None |
| Devotional scale | Mass practice | Elite/philosophical |
| Historical funding | Kings built massive | Smaller scale |
| Geographic spread | Pan-Asia | Regional |

## Archaeological Evidence (Oldest Physical Traces)

| Tradition | Oldest Dated Evidence | Date |
|-----------|----------------------|------|
| **Buddhism** | Lumbini timber postholes (bodhigara) | ~550 BCE |
| **Jainism** | Lohanipur torso (Mauryan) | 3rd c. BCE |
| **Confucianism** | Guodian bamboo slips | ~300 BCE |
| **Daoism** | Guodian Tao Te Ching fragments | ~300 BCE |
| **Shinto** | Yayoi pillar structures | 300 BCE-300 CE |
