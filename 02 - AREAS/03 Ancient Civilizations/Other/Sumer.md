# Sumer

**Location**: Southern Mesopotamia (modern Iraq)  
**Date**: ~3500–1900 BCE  
**First Cities**: Uruk, Eridu, Ur, Nippur, Kish  
**Writing System**: Cuneiform (world's first)  
**Age**: ~5,500 years old
**Status**: #entity #civilization #first #origin

---

## Claim to Fame

**WORLD'S FIRST CIVILIZATION**

Evidence:
- **First writing** (cuneiform, ~3200 BCE)
- **First cities** (Uruk: ~50,000–80,000 population at peak)
- **First state-level society** (organized by temple not palace)

---

## Timeline

| Period | Date | Features |
|--------|------|----------|
| Ubaid Period | ~6500–3800 BCE | Pre-civilization, proto-temples, irrigation invented |
| Uruk Period | ~4000–3100 BCE | First true cities, pottery wheel, massive walls |
| Early Dynastic | ~2900–2350 BCE | City-states, kingship, laws, cylinder seals |
| Akkadian Empire | ~2350–2100 BCE | World's first empire (Sargon the Great) |
| Ur III | ~2100–2000 BCE | "Sumerian Renaissance," written literature |
| Decline | ~1900 BCE | Taken over by Amorites, Elamites |

---

## Innovations

| Innovation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Cuneiform** | Wedge-shaped script on clay tablets. World's first true writing. |
| **City-states** | Uruk, Ur, Lagash, Umma — independent units |
| **Ziggurats** | Step pyramids as temple platforms |
| **Wheel** | Invented ~3500 BCE — pottery first, transport later |
| **Astronomy** | 360° circle, 60-minute hour, zodiac signs |
| **Law codes** | Ur-Nammu Law Code (~2100 BCE) — world's oldest known law |
| **Literature** | Epic of Gilgamesh, Enûma Elish, Sumerian King List |

## Key Cities

### Uruk
- **Eanna district**: Great ziggurat, Inanna temple
- **Gilgamesh**: Legendary king (possibly Uruk V)
- **Size**: ~6 km² walled area, largest in world at time

### Eridu
- **Claimed oldest city** in Sumerian King List
- **Dedicated to Enki/Ea** (god of wisdom, fresh water)
- **Archaeology**: Founded ~5400 BCE — earlier than Uruk

### Ur
- **Ur III dynasty capital**
- **Ziggurat of Ur** — best preserved (reconstructed by Saddam)
- **Royal Cemetery of Ur** — world's oldest known royal burials with gold
- **Ea-Nasir**: Famous copper merchant (~1750 BCE)

---

## Famous Individuals

### Ea-Nasir (~1750 BCE)
- **Sumerian copper merchant from Ur**
- **Ancient scam-artist**
- **Nanni's complaint tablet**: Worst customer service review in history

**Quote**: "What is one mina of copper? You gave me bad quality!"

**Relevance**: Shows Sumer had consumer culture, contracts, and literacy beyond scribes.

## Sumerian King List

**Claim**: 8 kings ruled for 241,200 years before the Flood.

**First king**: Alulim of Eridu
**Flood**: Deluge narrative precedes Noah by ~2,000 years
**Reality**: Mythology mixed with genealogy, exaggerated reign lengths

## Decline

**Reasons**:
- Soil salinity from irrigation over millennia
- Political fragmentation
- Amorite migration
- Elamite invasion (capture of Ur ~2004 BCE)

**Legacy**: 
- Akkadians, Babylonians, Assyrians all inherited Sumerian culture
- Cuneiform used for 3,000+ years
- Religious concepts spread to entire ancient Near East
