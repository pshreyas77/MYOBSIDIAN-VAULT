
## 📋 ESSENTIAL FACTS

**📍 Location:** Central Anatolia, Turkey (near Çumra, Konya)  
**📅 Age:** 9,500 years old (7500–5700 BCE)  
**🏛️ Type:** Neolithic proto-urban settlement  
**🔬 Dating Method:** Radiocarbon dating (C-14) + Bayesian modeling  
**🏆 UNESCO Status:** World Heritage Site (2012)

---

## **⚡ KEY FACTS (Minimal)**

✓ **World's first large human settlement** (8,000+ population)  
✓ Houses built **wall-to-wall** with **no streets**  
✓ Entry through **rooftops** via wooden ladders  
✓ **18 occupation levels** (continuous settlement)  
✓ Elaborate **wall paintings** & **bull horn installations**  
✓ **Burials under house floors** (ancestor veneration)  
✓ **Mother goddess figurines** & fertility symbols  
✓ **Obsidian mirrors** & advanced tool-making  
✓ **Oldest known map** (wall mural, 6200 BCE)  
✓ Transition from hunting-gathering to farming

---

## **📏 PHYSICAL DETAILS**

- **Site area:** ~13 hectares (32 acres)  
- **Population:** 3,000–8,000 people  
- **Houses:** 1,000+ structures discovered  
- **House size:** 25–50 sq meters  
- **Walls:** Mud-brick, plastered  
- **Height:** Multi-level buildings  
- **Discovery:** 1958 by James Mellaart  
- **Excavation:** 1961–1965 (Mellaart), 1993–present (Ian Hodder)

## **🔬 SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE**

**Dating Method:** Radiocarbon (C-14) dating + Bayesian statistical modeling

**Samples Dated:**
- Charcoal from hearths
- Animal bones
- Plant remains
- Construction timbers

**Results:** 
- **East Mound:** 7100–5700 BCE
- **West Mound:** 6000–5500 BCE
- **Confidence level:** 95% (±50-100 years)

**Verification:** Stanford University, University of Cambridge, multiple labs

---

## **🏠 ARCHITECTURAL FEATURES**

**House Construction:**
- Mud-brick walls (plastered white)
- Wooden support beams
- Flat roofs (living space)
- Internal platforms (sleeping, working)
- Ovens & hearths
- Storage bins

**Urban Planning:**
- No streets (access via roofs)
- Houses share walls
- No public buildings
- No elite residences (egalitarian?)

---

## **🎨 ART & SYMBOLISM**

**Wall Paintings:**
- Geometric patterns
- Hunting scenes
- Animals (leopards, bulls, deer, vultures)
- Volcanic eruption (world's oldest map)
- Abstract designs

**Sculptures & Figurines:**
- Seated woman (Mother Goddess?)
- Animal figurines (cattle, sheep, dogs)
- Clay stamps
- Stone beads

**Installations:**
- Bull horns (bucrania) in walls
- Plastered skulls
- Wall reliefs

---

## **💀 BURIAL PRACTICES**

- **Location:** Under house floors
- **Position:** Fetal (flexed)
- **Grave goods:** Beads, tools, ochre
- **Skull removal:** Some skulls plastered & displayed
- **Ancestor veneration:** Evidence of cult practices
