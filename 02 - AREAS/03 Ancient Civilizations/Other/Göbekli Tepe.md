# Göbekli Tepe

**Location**: Şanlıurfa Province, southeastern Turkey  
**Date**: ~9600 BCE (oldest layer)  
**Discovered**: 1994 by Kurdish shepherd + German archaeologist Klaus Schmidt  
**Age**: ~11,600 years old
**Status**: UNESCO World Heritage Site (2018)  
**Status**: #entity #archaeological-site #oldest #game-changer

---

## Significance

### Revolutionary Discovery

**Destroyed old theory**: Complex society requires agriculture first.

> Hunter-gatherers built massive stone temple complex **BEFORE farming invented.**

**Implications**:
- Religion may have driven civilization, not agriculture
- Hunter-gatherers capable of complex organization
- Fertile Crescent = actual cradle of human settlement

---

## Physical Description

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Pillars** | ~200 T-shaped limestone pillars, up to 5.5m tall, 16 tons |
| **Circles** | ~20 circular/oval enclosures |
| **Art** | Animal carvings: foxes, lions, vultures, scorpions, snakes |
| **No domestic structures** | No houses, no granaries — purely ritual/ceremonial |
| **Construction** | Dry stone walls, no mortar |

**Method**: Pillars quarried from local bedrock, transported ~100-500 meters.

## Timeline

| Phase | Date | Features |
|-------|------|----------|
| Layer III (oldest) | ~9600–8800 BCE | Large circular enclosures, T-pillars, carved animals |
| Layer II | ~8800–8000 BCE | Smaller, rectangular structures |
| Layer I | ~8000 BCE | Settlement debris, used as refuse pit |
| Abandoned | ~8000 BCE | Buried by inhabitants (intentional) |

---

## Mysteries

1. **Who built it?**
   - Pre-pottery Neolithic hunter-gatherers
   - No writing — identity unknown

2. **Why circular?**
   - Possibly astronomical alignment
   - Some structures point to sunset of solstice/equinox

3. **Why buried?**
   - Intentional covering with debris
   - Preserved site for 11,600 years
   - Unknown ritual purpose

4. **No bones?**
   - Minimal human remains found
   - Primary use = ritual, not burial

## Implications for Theory

### Old Theory (Pre-1994)
- Agriculture → Surplus → Permanent villages → Religion → Monuments

### New Theory (Post-Göbekli)
- **Religion/Hunter-gatherer organization → Monuments → ? → Agriculture**

**Evidence**:
- Domesticated wheat appears ~200 km away at same time
- Site may have driven need for settled agriculture

**Klaus Schmidt**: "First temple, then the city"
