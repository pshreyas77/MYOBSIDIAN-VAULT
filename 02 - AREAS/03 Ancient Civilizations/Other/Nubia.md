# Nubia

**Location**: Nile Valley, modern Sudan  
**Date**: ~3800 BCE – 350 CE  
**First Phase**: A-Group culture (~3800 BCE)  
**Age**: ~5,800 years old  
**Status**: #entity #civilization #underrated #african

---

## Significance

**Most Underrated Ancient Civilization**

**Evidence**:
- Contemporaneous with Predynastic Egypt
- African's oldest urban center south of Sahara (Kerma)
- Conquered Egypt as 25th Dynasty ("Black Pharaohs")
- Own script (Meroitic, partially undeciphered)

**Common Misconception**: "Nubia = oldest civilization" (Afrocentric claim)

**Reality**: Ancient but NOT oldest. ~1,700 years AFTER Sumer.

## Timeline

| Period | Date | Features |
|--------|------|----------|
| A-Group | ~3800–2800 BCE | Complex trading society, pottery, graves |
| C-Group | ~2400–1500 BCE | Northern Nubia, cattle pastoralists |
| Kerma Kingdom | ~2500–1500 BCE | **First Nubian state**, walled capital, wealth from trade |
| Egyptian Occupation | ~1500–1000 BCE | Pharaohs of 18th Dynasty conquered, built temples |
| Napatan Period | ~900–270 BCE | **Kushite revival**, regained independence |
| 25th Dynasty | ~747–656 BCE | **Conquered Egypt**, "Black Pharaohs" rule both lands |
| Meroitic Period | ~270 BCE–350 CE | Capital moved to Meroë, own script, iron production |
| Decline | ~350 CE | Axumite (Ethiopian) conquest |

## Key Achievements

| Achievement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Kerma** | Largest settlement in ancient Nubia (~30,000 people), massive mud brick temple (Deffufa) |
| **25th Dynasty** | Piye, Shabaka, Taharqa — restored Egyptian culture during decline |
| **Pyramids** | More pyramids than Egypt (~223 at Meroë, smaller but more numerous) |
| **Iron Production** | Early iron smelting, possibly independently developed |
| **Meroitic Script** | Unique alphabet, still partially undeciphered |
| **Trade Network** | Gold, ivory, incense, exotic animals to Egypt and Mediterranean |

---

## Pyramids of Nubia

**Meroë**:
- ~223 pyramids (more than Egypt)
- Smaller (10-30m base vs Egypt's 230m)
- Steeper angle (~70° vs Egypt's 52°)
- Grave robbers destroyed many

**Significance**: Nubian pyramid tradition continued 800 years AFTER Egyptian collapse.

## Why Underrated?

### Western Academic Bias
- 19th-20th century archaeologists focused on Egypt
- "Merely derivative of Egypt" — incorrect
- Active suppression of Nubian distinctiveness

### Modern Corrections
- **2019 Harvard Museum**: Restored Nubian objects with proper attribution
- **Contemporary scholarship**: Recognizes independent Nubian culture
- **Kushite military**: Defeated Egypt repeatedly — not "subordinate"

## Archaeological Evidence

**Sites**:
- **Kerma**: Capital of Kerma Kingdom, massive Deffufa temple
- **Napata**: Religious capital, pyramid field
- **Meroë**: Last capital, iron production, pyramids
- **Abu Simbel**: Nubian frontier temples (built by Ramesses II)

**Artifacts**:
- Piye's Victory Stela: Defeated Egyptian dynasts, established 25th Dynasty
- Gold jewelry rivaling Egypt
- Distinctive pottery (A-Group, Kerma ware)
