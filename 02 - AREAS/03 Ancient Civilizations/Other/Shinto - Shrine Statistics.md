# Shinto: Statue Statistics

## Overview

**Shinto has essentially ZERO statues of kami** (divine spirits) in active shrines. This makes it unique among major worldviews.

---

## Key Facts

| Metric | Number |
|--------|--------|
| **Shinto shrines (Japan)** | ~80,000-100,000 |
| **Statues of kami** | **~0** in active shrines |
| **Guardian statues (komainu)** | ~100,000+ pairs |
| **Fox (kitsune) statues** | ~40,000+ at Inari alone |

---

## Why No Kami Statues?

### Core Belief
- Kami are **invisible energy** (hikari)
- They have **no fixed form**
- Cannot be depicted in human likeness

### Shinto Principle
> "The divine has no shape - it inhabits all things"

### What Replaces Statues
- **Shintai (God Body)**: Sacred objects in innermost sanctuary
  - Swords
  - Jewels (magatama)
  - Mirrors
- These are vessels, not representations

---

## What You WILL See at Shinto Shrines

### Komainu (Guardian Dogs)
- Pairs of lion-dog statues at shrine entrances
- One with mouth open (A-un), one closed
- **NOT kami** - protectors only
- ~100,000+ pairs across Japan

### Kitsune (Fox Statues)
- At **Inari shrines** specifically
- Fox = **messenger** of Inari, not Inari itself
- **40,000+ kitsune statues** at Inari shrines alone
- Often seen with red bibs, offerings

### Torii Gates
- Gate markers, not statues
- ~100,000+ across Japan

## Archaeological Evidence

### Yayoi Period (~300 BCE - 300 CE)
- **Ikegami-Sone site**: Pillar-supported structures resembling early shrines
- Ritual bronze weapons and mirrors

### Kofun Period (4th c. CE+)
- **Okinoshima island**: Ritual offerings (bronze mirrors, weapons)
- Direct precursors to shrine practices
