# **CARAL-SUPE CIVILIZATION (NORTE CHICO) - 5TH OLDEST CIVILIZATION**
## 📋 ESSENTIAL FACTS

**📍 Location:** Supe Valley, Peru (South America)  
**📅 Age:** 5,500 years old (3500–1800 BCE)  
**🏛️ Type:** Oldest civilization in the Americas  
**🔬 Dating Method:** Radiocarbon dating (146 samples)  
**🏆 UNESCO Status:** World Heritage Site (2009)

---

## **⚡ KEY FACTS (Minimal)**

✓ **Oldest civilization in the Americas**  
✓ **Contemporary with Egyptian pyramids**  
✓ **6 large platform mounds/pyramids**  
✓ **Circular sunken plazas**  
✓ **No warfare evidence** (no weapons, fortifications)  
✓ **Preceramic civilization** (before pottery)  
✓ **Quipu** (knot recording system) - earliest example  
✓ **Irrigation canals** for cotton cultivation  
✓ **Musical instruments** (32 flutes, 37 trumpets found)  
✓ **Independent development** (no Old World contact)

---

## **📏 PHYSICAL DETAILS**

- **Site area:** 66 hectares (Caral)  
- **Pyramid height:** Up to 28 meters (92 ft)  
- **Population:** ~3,000 people (Caral), 20,000+ (entire civilization)  
- **Territory:** 750 sq km across four valleys  
- **Major sites:** Caral, Aspero, Huaricanga, Kotosh  
- **Structures:** 32 pyramids, 300+ houses  
- **Discovery:** 1905 (first survey), 1994-2005 (Ruth Shady excavations)

## **🔬 SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE**

**Dating Methods:**
- **146 radiocarbon dates** from organic materials
- Stratigraphic analysis
- AMS (Accelerator Mass Spectrometry) dating
- Multiple independent labs

**Key Dates Verified:**
- **Caral:** 2627–2000 BCE (peak occupation)
- **Aspero:** 3650–2420 BCE (earliest coastal site)
- **Huaricanga:** 3500–1800 BCE
- **Civilization span:** 3500–1800 BCE

**Verification:** 
- Field Museum (Chicago)
- University of Illinois
- San Marcos University (Lima)
- Peer-reviewed in *Science* (2001)

---

## **🏛️ MAJOR SITES**

**Caral (Capital):**
- 6 pyramid mounds
- 2 circular plazas
- 32 hectares
- 3,000 population

**Aspero (Coastal Port):**
- Maritime trade center
- Fish processing
- 2 platform mounds
- Connected to Caral

**Huaricanga:**
- Oldest inland settlement
- Agricultural center
- 3500 BCE founding

**Kotosh:**
- Temple of the Crossed Hands
- Highland connection
- Religious center

---

## **🌾 ECONOMY & SUBSISTENCE**

**Agriculture:**
- **Cotton** (Gossypium barbadense) - main crop
- Squash, beans, peppers
- Avocados, guava
- Irrigation canals (gravity-fed)

**Marine Resources:**
- Anchovies, sardines
- Mussels, clams
- Trade: fish (coast) ↔ cotton (inland)

**No Cereal Grains:**
- No maize initially (appeared later)
- No wheat, barley
- Unique among ancient civilizations

---

## **🏗️ ARCHITECTURE**

**Pyramid Construction:**
- Stone & earth fill
- Retaining walls
- Shicra bags (mesh containers for stones)
- Ceremonial staircases
- Fire altars on top

**Circular Plazas:**
- Sunken into ground
- Stone-paved floors
- Central fire pits
- Capacity: 200-300 people
- Ritual/ceremonial use

**Residential Areas:**
- Elite houses near pyramids
- Commoner houses on periphery
- Stone foundations
- Reed/cane walls & roofs

---

## **🎵 CULTURAL ACHIEVEMENTS**

**Music:**
- **32 flutes** (condor & llama bones)
- **37 trumpets** (clay)
- Oldest musical instruments in Americas
- Complex musical tradition

**Textiles:**
- Cotton fishing nets
- Clothing, bags
- No pottery (preceramic)
- Advanced weaving

**Quipu:**
- Knot-record keeping system
- **Earliest known example** (2500 BCE)
- Pre-dates Inca by 4,000 years
- Administrative/accounting use

---

## **📜 WRITING SYSTEM**

**Caral Gourd (2005 discovery):**
- Dated to 2500 BCE
- Engraved symbols
- Possible proto-writing
- Debated: writing or decoration?

**Quipu:**
- 3D recording system
- Knots = numbers/information
- Color coding
- Precursor to Inca quipu

---

## **⚔️ NO WARFARE EVIDENCE**

**Unique Characteristics:**
- **No weapons** found (no spears, arrows, clubs)
- **No fortifications** (no walls, moats)
- **No trauma** on skeletal remains
- **No mutilated bodies**
- **No burned structures**

**Interpretation:**
- Peaceful society?
- Trade-based economy
- Religious/ceremonial power
- Cooperative labor organization

---

## **👥 SOCIAL ORGANIZATION**

**Evidence of Hierarchy:**
- Elite residences near pyramids
- Different house sizes
- Specialized labor (builders, musicians, farmers)
- Centralized authority (priests/chiefs)

**Labor Organization:**
- Monumental construction required coordination
- 100,000+ person-days for pyramids
- Feasting evidence (communal meals)
- Corvée labor system?

---

## ** TRADE NETWORKS**

**Regional Trade:**
- Coastal fish ↔ inland cotton
- Spondylus shells (Ecuador)
- Lapis lazuli (Chile)
- Hallucinogenic snuff (Amazon)

**Long-Distance:**
- 1,000+ km trade routes
- Maritime & overland
- No wheeled vehicles (llama caravans?)

---

## **🔍 EXCAVATION HISTORY**

**1905:** Max Uhle first visits site  
**1940s:** Limited surveys  
**1994:** Ruth Shady begins Caral Archaeological Project  
**1996-2005:** Major excavations  
**2001:** Science magazine publication (146 C-14 dates)  
**2009:** UNESCO World Heritage designation  
**Present:** Ongoing research & conservation

**Lead Archaeologist:** Dr. Ruth Shady Solís (San Marcos University, Lima)
