Here's a country-by-country breakdown of potential future leaders over the next 5–10 years across the world's major powers:

---

## 🇺🇸 United States
**Current:** Donald Trump (term ends 2029)
**Next election: 2028**
Likely contenders include younger Republican figures (JD Vance is well-positioned as VP) and a resurgent Democratic field. Names like Gavin Newsom, Gretchen Whitmer, and Pete Buttigieg are frequently discussed on the Democratic side.

---

## 🇨🇳 China
**Current:** Xi Jinping (no formal term limit)
Xi has consolidated power significantly and is expected to remain in control through the early 2030s. Any succession is opaque — the Communist Party selects leadership internally, so no clear successor is publicly visible yet.

---

## 🇷🇺 Russia
**Current:** Vladimir Putin
Putin shows no signs of stepping down. His major offensive in summer 2025 failed militarily, and Russia's casualties continue to mount, which could eventually create internal pressure. Potential successors floated in analyst circles include figures from the security services (FSB/SVR), but succession is highly unpredictable in Russia's system.

---

## 🇮🇳 India
**Current:** Narendra Modi
Modi remains the world's most popular major leader heading into 2026, with 71% approval, though slightly down from earlier highs. He is likely to remain dominant through 2029. The BJP's next generation — figures like Yogi Adityanath and Amit Shah — are considered potential future prime ministers.

---

## 🇬🇧 United Kingdom
**Current:** Keir Starmer (Labour)
Starmer faces challenges from hard-right populists like Reform, with local and Welsh elections potentially dealing further blows to his unpopular leadership. On the Conservative side, figures like Kemi Badenoch (current Tory leader) are positioning themselves for a future run.

---

## 🇩🇪 Germany
**Current:** Friedrich Merz (CDU), elected in 2025
Despite winning what was arguably 2025's most high-profile election, Merz's approval stands at just 31%, against 64% disapproval — making his long-term grip uncertain. The rise of the AfD means German politics could shift significantly by 2029–2030.

---

## 🇫🇷 France
**Current:** Emmanuel Macron (cannot run again in 2027)
Macron ranks last among major leaders in approval, with ratings in the low teens and disapproval near 80%. The 2027 presidential election is wide open — Marine Le Pen (if not legally barred), Jordan Bardella, and centrist figures like Édouard Philippe are key names to watch.

---

## 🇧🇷 Brazil
**Current:** Lula da Silva
2025 marks Lula's final confirmed year, with the 2026 election approaching. Bolsonaro, despite his ineligibility until 2030, plans to run with Trump's backing. Other contenders from both left and right are likely to emerge for 2026.

---

## 🇯🇵 Japan
**Current:** Shigeru Ishiba (LDP)
Japan's LDP has dominated for decades, and leadership rotates frequently within the party. Rising figures within LDP factions are the most likely next PMs — predicting a specific name 5–10 years out is very difficult.

---

## 🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia
**Current leader in waiting:** Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman (MBS)
MBS effectively runs the country already and is expected to formally become king upon King Salman's passing, then likely lead for decades given his age (39).

- **India** is the one major democracy with a clearly dominant leader likely to shape the next decade
