# **GÖBEKLI TEPE - WORLD'S OLDEST CIVILIZATION**
## 📋 ESSENTIAL FACTS

**📍 Location:** Southeastern Anatolia, Turkey  
**📅 Age:** 11,600 years old (9600–8200 BCE)  
**🏛️ Type:** Pre-Pottery Neolithic religious/ritual complex  
**🔬 Dating Method:** Radiocarbon dating (C-14)  
**🏆 UNESCO Status:** World Heritage Site (2018)

---

## **⚡ KEY FACTS (Minimal)**

✓ **World's oldest monumental architecture**  
✓ Built by **hunter-gatherers** (before agriculture)  
✓ **T-shaped limestone pillars** (up to 50 tons each)  
✓ **Animal carvings:** lions, snakes, foxes, birds, spiders  
✓ **20+ circular enclosures** discovered  
✓ Only **5% excavated** so far  
✓ **Deliberately buried** ~8200 BCE  
✓ Predates Stonehenge by **6,000 years**  
✓ Predates Egyptian pyramids by **7,000 years**

---

## **📏 PHYSICAL DETAILS**

- **Pillar height:** 3–6 meters (10–20 ft)  
- **Pillar weight:** 10–50 tons  
- **Material:** Local limestone  
- **Enclosure diameter:** 10–30 meters  
- **Site area:** ~9 hectares  
- **Discovery:** 1963 (surveyed), 1995 (excavation began)  
- **Excavator:** Klaus Schmidt (German Archaeological Institute)

## **🔬 SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE**

**Dating Method:** Radiocarbon (C-14) dating of:
- Organic material in mortar
- Charcoal from construction layers
- Animal bones from fill deposits

**Results:** 9600–8200 BCE (±50-100 years)

**Verification:** Multiple independent labs confirmed dates
