# Checking for job offers

> **Conversation Overview**

Sunny asked Claude to access and review his Gmail inbox for job offers or interview-related communications. Claude searched his inbox and read specific emails to provide a summary of relevant activity. No actual job offers or interview invitations were found — the inbox contained primarily automated application confirmations, recruiter marketing emails, and job recommendation digests.

The most actionable item identified was a hirist.tech confirmation (March 25) indicating Sunny's profile had been shared with a recruiter for a Data Analyst role in the healthcare domain (0–2 years experience). A Naukri Campus email about an application status change could not be read due to a broken email template, so Claude recommended Sunny log into Naukri directly to check. A LinkedIn application confirmation to Sajre Edutech Pvt Ltd and an Internshala email with a misleading subject line (a program pitch, not a job acceptance) were also noted. Job recommendations from Glassdoor, LinkedIn, and Naukri Campus were flagged, including roles at Accenture, Ivanti, WEBBOOST SOLUTION IT SERVICES, Paytm, and Zuntra Digital. An Incedo Technology Solutions AI Innovation Hiring Challenge with an imminent deadline was also highlighted.

**Tool Knowledge**

For Gmail searches, using a broad subject-line OR query combining terms like "offer OR interview OR hiring OR job OR opportunity OR application OR selected OR shortlisted OR congratulations OR joining" with `gmail_search_messages` effectively surfaced relevant emails in one pass. Following up with `gmail_read_message` on specific message IDs was necessary to distinguish genuine application updates from marketing emails with misleading subject lines — the Internshala "application accepted" email being a key example. When an email body fails to render (as with the Naukri Campus status update), the tool returns broken template content rather than an error, so the workaround is to advise the user to check the source platform directly.
