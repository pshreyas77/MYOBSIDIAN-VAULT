This is a great question, and the answer depends on how you define "power." Both men are enormously influential, but in different ways.

**Alex Karp — More Powerful *Inside* Palantir**

Alex Karp is the CEO and has been running day-to-day operations since the company's founding. Thiel largely left the operation of the company in Karp's hands, making Karp the dominant force in Palantir's actual management and strategic direction. He controls who the company works with, sets the company's culture, and is the public face of the business. His net worth has surged to over $18 billion, and Time magazine named him on the Time 100 list of the world's most influential people in 2025.

**Peter Thiel — More Powerful *Outside* Palantir**

Thiel is the original founder and visionary who created Palantir and recruited Karp to lead it. His broader power comes from his political and financial influence. Thiel was a prominent early supporter of Trump, spoke at the 2016 Republican convention, and played a key role in the rise of Vice President JD Vance. His political connections directly benefit Palantir — analysts note that "having political connections and inroads with Peter Thiel and Elon Musk certainly helps them — it makes deals come faster without a lot of negotiation and pressure." Thiel's net worth is also slightly higher at over $25 billion.

**The Bottom Line**

Think of it this way: Thiel is the architect who designed the building, while Karp is the one running it every day. Karp has operational control and public influence over Palantir's direction, but Thiel's political and financial network gives Palantir much of its power in Washington. They are more complementary than competitive — each powerful in different arenas.
