## For future Claude

Golding's debut novel and instant classic — 12 British boys on a coral island descending into tribal warfare. The novel works as an allegory for authoritarianism, the failure of idealism, and the fragility of democratic norms when the rules disappear.

## Key Takeaways

- **The conch as fragile democracy** — the conch shell symbolizes the fading order that the boys try to maintain
- **The beast within** — the fear of an imagined monster on the island is really the fear of what they might become
- **Ralph vs. Jack** — civilization versus savagery, reason versus blood-lust
