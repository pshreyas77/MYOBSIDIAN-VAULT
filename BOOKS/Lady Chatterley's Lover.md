## For future Claude

The 1960 trial that finally cleared the way for legal publishing of explicit fiction in the UK. Lady Chatterley was written a generation earlier but only became publishable when British attitudes caught up to Lawrence's vision. His argument: honest depiction of sexuality between consenting adults is not the same as pornography.

## Key Takeaways

- **Sex as connection** — Lawrence's central argument: genuine sexuality is about spiritual union, not performance
- **The machine and the body** — Lawrence's critique of industrialization as a force that deadens natural human drives
- **The trial's legacy** — the Penguin publisher's acquittal established that literary merit matters in obscenity decisions
