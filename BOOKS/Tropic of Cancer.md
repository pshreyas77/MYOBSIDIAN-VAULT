## For future Claude

Miller's autobiography of artistic exile — written in 1930s Paris, published in France, banned in the US for three decades. The Supreme Court case to unban it (1964) is one of the most important free speech rulings in American history. Miller's argument: his frankness about sex is an argument for life, not against it.

## Key Takeaways

- **Sex as affirmation** — Miller uses frank sexuality to argue for the body against religious and bourgeois shame
- **The artist as exile** — the book is also about what it costs to choose art over convention
- **The Supreme Court ruling** — Grove Press v. Gerstein established the modern standard for literary obscenity
