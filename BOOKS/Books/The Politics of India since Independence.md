The Politics of India since Independence

## Summary
This book provides a comprehensive analysis of Indian politics from 1947 to the present, covering the evolution of political institutions, party systems, and social movements.

## Key Ideas
- The dominance of the Congress system and its breakdown
- Rise of regional parties and coalition politics
- Caste and religion in Indian politics
- Economic liberalization and political change
- Democratic deepening and challenges

## Notes
- Author is a renowned South Asia politics scholar
- Covers period from Nehru to Modi eras
- Includes analysis of state politics
- Comparative perspective with other democracies

## Quotes
> "India's democracy has survived not because of its institutions but despite them."

## Insights
- Identity politics as a central feature of Indian democracy
- Federal accommodation of diversity
- Tension between developmental state and liberal economy
- Role of civil society and social movements
