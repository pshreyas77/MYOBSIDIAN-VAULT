The Making of India's Constitution

## Summary
The classic historical account of how India's constitution was drafted, debated, and adopted by the Constituent Assembly between 1946-1950.

## Key Ideas
- The Constituent Assembly as a microcosm of Indian society
- Key debates on fundamental rights, directive principles, federalism
- Influence of global constitutions and Indian social realities
- Role of key figures: Nehru, Ambedkar, Patel, etc.
- The compromise and consensus-building process

## Notes
- Based on extensive archival research and interviews
- Considered the definitive work on the subject
- Updated with new preface in later editions
- Essential for understanding constitutional intent

## Quotes
> "The Indian Constitution is first and foremost a social document."

## Insights
- Constitution as a tool for social revolution
- Balance between idealism and pragmatism in drafting
- How colonial experience shaped constitutional choices
- The living nature of the document from inception
