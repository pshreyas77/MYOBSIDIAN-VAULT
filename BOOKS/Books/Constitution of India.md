Constitution of India

## Summary
The Constitution of India is the supreme law of India. It lays down the framework defining fundamental political principles, establishes the structure, procedures, powers, and duties of government institutions, and sets out fundamental rights, directive principles, and the duties of citizens.

## Key Ideas
- Sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic
- Fundamental rights and duties
- Directive principles of state policy
- Federal structure with unitary bias
- Judicial review and independence

## Notes
- Came into effect on January 26, 1950
- Longest written constitution of any sovereign nation
- Amended over 100 times
- Establishes parliamentary system of government

## Quotes
> "We, the people of India, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a sovereign socialist secular democratic republic..."

## Insights
- Balance between rigidity and flexibility in amendments
- Basic structure doctrine limits parliamentary power
- Directive principles guide policy-making
- Fundamental rights justiciable, directive principles non-justiciable
