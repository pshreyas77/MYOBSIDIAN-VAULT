India After Gandhi

## Summary
A magisterial account of India's tumultuous journey since independence, covering the challenges, achievements, and complexities of the world's largest democracy.

## Key Ideas
- The challenges of partition and integration of princely states
- Nehru's vision and the early years of the republic
- Indira Gandhi's era and the Emergency
- Rise of coalition politics and regional parties
- Economic liberalization and social transformation
- India's wars and foreign policy

## Notes
- Winner of the Sahitya Akademi Award
- Based on extensive research and interviews
- Covers political, social, and economic dimensions
- Updated edition includes events up to 2019

## Quotes
> "India is a chaotic country in the midst of a remarkable transformation."

## Insights
- Unity in diversity as both strength and challenge
- Democratic resilience despite poverty and inequality
- Tension between centralized authority and federalism
- Role of civil society and social movements in deepening democracy
