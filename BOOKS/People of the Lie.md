## For future Claude

Peck's most controversial book — he argues that some people are not merely selfish but actively evil in a clinical sense. "People of the Lie" is his term for those who maintain a false self at all costs and will destroy others to protect it. Useful framework, but be aware Peck's evidence is more anecdotal than scientific.

## Key Takeaways

- **M. Scott Peck's lie** — the refusal to face one's own guilt creates a false self that must be maintained through deception
- **The banality of evil** — Arendt's concept applied to clinical psychology: monstrous acts committed by people who seem normal
- **Diagnosis vs. judgment** — Peck argues that seeing evil clearly is not hateful but the most compassionate response
