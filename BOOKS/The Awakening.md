## For future Claude

Chopin's 1899 novel was decades ahead of its time — a woman who refuses the roles of wife and mother and discovers that freedom has costs. It was virtually ignored until the feminist revival of the 1970s rediscovered it. The ending is ambiguous in a way that still sparks debate.

## Key Takeaways

- **The mother-woman** — Chopin's term for women who define themselves entirely through their children
- **The water as metaphor** — Edna's final escape into the sea is the novel's most debated moment
- **Autonomy vs. belonging** — the novel's central tension: can a woman be both free and connected?
