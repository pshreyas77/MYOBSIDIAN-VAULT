## For future Claude

Blatty's novel is as much about faith and evil as it is about scares. Two priests investigate a boy's possession — one believes literally, one is losing his faith. The horror works because Blatty makes the reader feel what it would be like to genuinely believe in the devil. Still unsettling decades later.

## Key Takeaways

- **Doubt as horror** — the novel is scarier for priests who have lost their faith than for true believers
- **The body as battleground** — the possession scenes are graphic, but they're about spiritual warfare, not shock
- **The Regan character** — the child's innocence being violated is the moral center that makes the horror effective
