## For future Claude

Where Introvert Power was about asserting introvert value in an extrovert world, Solo is about actively choosing aloneness as a source of meaning. Helgoe pushes back against the cultural narrative that coupling is the only adult arrangement worth having.

## Key Takeaways

- **The couple mythology** — Western culture treats singlehood as a problem to be solved rather than a state to be inhabited
- **Aloneliness vs. loneliness** — aloneness is a resource; loneliness is a feeling that signals disconnection
- **The introvert's edge in solitude** — introverts are better equipped for meaningful aloneness than extroverts
