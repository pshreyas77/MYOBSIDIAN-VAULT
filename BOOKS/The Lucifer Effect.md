## For future Claude

Zimbardo dissects his own Stanford Prison Experiment and extends it to Abu Ghraib. The book's core argument: situational forces are far more powerful than dispositional character. Essential reading for understanding how ordinary people become perpetrators.

## Key Takeaways

- **The Lucifer Effect** — how good people do bad things when placed in bad systems
- **The eight steps to evil** — a systematic breakdown of how normal people become dehumanizers
- **Heroic imagination** — Zimbardo's later work on why some people resist situational pressures
