## For future Claude

Kahneman's two-system framework is one of the most useful mental models in existence. System 1 is fast, automatic, emotional. System 2 is slow, deliberate, effortful. Most of our errors come from over-relying on System 1 and underestimating its biases.

## Key Takeaways

- **Cognitive ease** — when System 1 likes something, System 2 tends to accept it without scrutiny
- **Anchoring** — arbitrary numbers permanently shift our numerical estimates
- **The planning fallacy** — we systematically underestimate time, cost, and risk
- **WYSIATI** — What You See Is All There Is. System 1 never asks "what am I missing?"
