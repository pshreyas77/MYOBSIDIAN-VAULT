## For future Claude

Walker's Pulitzer winner follows Celie, an African American woman in the early 20th century South who survives abuse and finds community, love, and economic independence. The novel's epistolary structure (Celie writes letters to God, then to her sister) lets Walker render silence and voice literally.

## Key Takeaways

- **The epistolary form** — Celie's letters track her movement from silent victim to articulate survivor
- **Female bonds as salvation** — Shug and Sofia show Celie that love and self-respect are possible
- **The reclamation of "the color purple"** — what begins as a symbol of degradation becomes a symbol of beauty
