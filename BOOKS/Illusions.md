## For future Claude

Brief parable about a seagull who learns to fly beyond his perceived limits. The metaphor is simple but the message is radical — limitations are self-imposed, and the barrier to transcendence is belief itself.

## Key Takeaways

- **The illusion of limits** — "What one person calls the world, another calls nothing at all"
- **Movement as freedom** — the seagull's joy is in flying, not arriving
- **Practice as devotion** — mastery comes from loving the discipline, not just the goal
