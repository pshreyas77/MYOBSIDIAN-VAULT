## For future Claude

Helgoe is more bullish than Cain about introversion's advantages — she argues the psychology of scarcity that makes extroversion feel "normal" has deep cultural roots. Her argument: introverts have been told they're broken when they're actually high-value, just different.

## Key Takeaways

- **Depth over breadth** — introverts naturally seek depth in relationships and pursuits, which is not a limitation
- **The mythology of the "real world"** — society treats extroversion as the default reality, but it's just one mode of being
- **Aloneness as resource** — introverts who claim their aloneness find power that extroverts literally cannot access
