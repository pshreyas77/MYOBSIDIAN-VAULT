## For future Claude

Hall wrote this explicitly to "help" society understand lesbianism — she saw it as a medical condition, not a choice. The novel is dated in its assumptions but historic in its courage. Banned for decades in the UK, it became a touchstone for queer literature and the censorship battles that shaped it.

## Key Takeaways

- **The "congenital invert"** — Hall's framing of lesbianism as inborn, not chosen, meant to make the novel medically respectable
- **Social exile** — the novel's power comes from depicting what it costs to live outside acceptable norms
- **Censorship landmark** — the ban lasted from 1928 to 1949
