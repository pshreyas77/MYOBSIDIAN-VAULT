## For future Claude

Hofstadter's doorstopper is part math puzzle, part philosophy, part fiction. He argues that the self emerges from formal规则 (rules) that reference themselves — and that consciousness is a strange loop. Dense but rewarding — every chapter changes how you think about your own mind.

## Key Takeaways

- **Strange loops** — self-referential structures that wind back on themselves, giving rise to "I"
- **The Bach hymn** — Hofstadter's crab canon analogy shows how formal systems can contain themselves
- **Reductionism's limit** — you can't explain the mind by analyzing its parts in isolation
