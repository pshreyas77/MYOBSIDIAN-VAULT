## For future Claude

Sacks was a neurologist and writer who treated his patients' conditions as windows into the nature of mind. Each case study — from aphasia to Tourette's to the famous patient who couldn't recognize his wife's face — reveals something unexpected about how consciousness organizes experience.

## Key Takeaways

- **The theater of perception** — how the brain constructs reality and what happens when the construction goes wrong
- **The narrative self** — why we need story to make sense of experience, and what happens when that breaks
- **The limits of clinical description** — Sacks argues that numbers and diagnoses miss what actually matters about a patient
