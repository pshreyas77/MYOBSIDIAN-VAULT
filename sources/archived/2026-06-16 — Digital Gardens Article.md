## Summary
Digital gardens emerged post-2020 as an alternative to chronological blogging. They emphasize evergreen content that evolves, combinatorial growth through non-linear connections, and public thinking that shows work-in-progress.

Vanessa Groot (2024) defines digital gardens as "a personal knowledge base that is publicly accessible and evolves through continuous refinement."

Key technical requirements identified:
- Bidirectional links (backlinks)
- Graph visualization
- Version history per note
- Search by concept, not date

Maggie Appleton's 2025 research: 67% of digital gardeners report improved retention vs linear note-taking.

Historical lineage: Ward Cunningham's WikiWikiWeb (1995) → Vannevar Bush's memex (1945) → modern tools (Obsidian, Roam, Logseq).
