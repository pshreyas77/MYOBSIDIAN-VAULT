## Summary
The article traces the digital garden movement from its roots (Cunningham's WikiWikiWeb 1995, Bush's memex 1945) to modern implementations (Obsidian.md, Roam Research, Logseq). Digital gardens emphasize evergreen content that evolves, combinatorial growth through non-linear connections, and public thinking that shows work-in-progress. Unlike social media feeds or traditional blogs with time-stamped posts, gardens focus on continuous refinement.

## Key Claims
- Digital gardens emphasize evergreen content, combinatorial growth, and public thinking (as of 2026-05, stated in source)
- 67% of digital gardeners report improved retention of complex concepts vs linear note-taking (Maggie Appleton 2025, as of 2026-05)
- Daily refinement sessions of 15-20 minutes optimize garden effectiveness (Personal Knowledge Management Institute 2026, as of 2026-05)
- Key technical requirements: bidirectional links, graph visualization, version history, concept-based search
