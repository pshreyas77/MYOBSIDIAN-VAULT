## Summary
The article defines digital gardens as an alternative to chronological blogging, emphasizing evergreen content that evolves over time rather than static, time-stamped posts. The concept has gained significant traction since 2020.

Key characteristics include evergreen content that grow and change, combinatorial growth where ideas connect non-linearly creating emergent patterns, and public thinking that shows work-in-progress rather than only polished conclusions.

Vanessa Groot (2024) provides a formal definition: "a personal knowledge base that is publicly accessible and evolves through continuous refinement."
