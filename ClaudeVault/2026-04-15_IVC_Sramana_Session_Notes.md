# Session Notes: IVC → Śramaṇa → Buddhism/Jainism Discussion

**Date:** 2026-04-15  
**Topic:** Cultural continuity between Indus Valley Civilization and Śramaṇa traditions

---

## Summary of Discussion

### Key Points Covered

1. **Priest-King Statue Parallels**
   - IVC "Priest-King" (~2000 BCE) shows meditation posture
   - Similar to: Buddhist *dhyāna mudrā*, Jain *kayotsarga*
   - Features: Half-closed eyes, serene expression, draped robe (trefoil pattern)
   - No weapons, no tilak → contrasts with Vedic/Hindu martial imagery

2. **Pashupati Seal Animals**
   - Bull: Direct connection to Jain Ṛṣabhanātha (1st Tirthankara, bull emblem)
   - Deer: Buddhist Deer Park Sarnath (first sermon)
   - All four animals: Prefigures *ahimsa* (compassion for all beings)

3. **Structural Similarities (IVC Absence = Evidence)**
   - **NO fire altars** in IVC (unlike Vedic tradition)
   - **NO tilak/forehead markings** (unlike Brahmanical Hinduism)
   - **NO weapons** on figurines (non-martial tradition)
   - Śramaṇa traditions share these absences with IVC, oppose Vedic features

4. **Continuous Symbols**
   - **Swastika**: IVC seals → Buddhist/Jain *aṣṭamaṅgala*
   - **Pipal tree**: IVC iconography → Bodhi tree (Buddha), Jain sacred trees

5. **Water Rituals vs. Fire Sacrifice**
   - IVC: Great Bath (water-based ritual)
   - Śramaṇa: Water purification, NO fire sacrifice
   - Vedic: Fire sacrifice (*yajna*) central

6. **Ancient Lineage Claims (Buddhism & Jainism)**
   - **Buddhism**: Claims 24 previous Buddhas before Siddhartha (25th)
   - **Jainism**: Claims 23 previous Tirthankaras before Mahavira (24th)
   - Theological position: Both claim to **revive** ancient paths, not innovate
   - IVC meditation/ascetic practices could be part of ancient substrate
   - No archaeological proof links specific IVC figures to named predecessors

7. **Important Clarifications**
   - **NO Buddha stupas in IVC** — anachronistic (1400+ year gap)
   - IVC has circular ritual platforms, not stupas
   - Stupas emerge ~250 BCE (Ashoka), long after IVC collapse (~1900 BCE)

8. **Genetic Evidence (Narasimhan 2019; Shinde 2019)**
   - IVC populations migrated east after 2000 BCE
   - No Steppe ancestry in IVC (distinct from Vedic populations)
   - Admixture in Gangetic plains 2000-1000 BCE
   - Compatible with cultural transmission theory
