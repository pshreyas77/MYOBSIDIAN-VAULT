### Genetic Evidence (Narasimhan et al., 2019; Shinde et al., 2019)

- After 2000 BCE, IVC-related ancestry shifted eastward toward Gangetic plain
- Admixture between IVC and Steppe populations 2000-1000 BCE in Gangetic plains
- Compatible with cultural transmission model
- Rakhigarhi genome (Shinde et al., 2019) shows no Steppe ancestry

## Core Themes

### 1. Meditation Postures (Moderate-Strong Evidence)
Marshall's (1931) excavation report described the "Priest-King" statue (~2000 BCE) as displaying a "mediative pose." McEvilley (2002) compares to Buddhist *dhyāna mudrā* and Jain *kayotsarga*. Banerjee's (1985) Kalibangan findings extend to pre-IVC levels (~3000 BCE).

### 2. Water Rituals vs. Fire Sacrifice (Strong Evidence)
Great Bath at Mohenjo-daro represents water-based ritual complex. Fire altars conspicuously absent (Lal, 1997; Possehl, 2002).

### 3. Continuous Symbols (Strong Evidence)
- **Swastika:** IVC seals → Buddhist/Jain *aṣṭamaṅgala*
- **Pipal tree:** IVC iconography → Buddhist Bodhi tree

---

## Conclusions

**Documented Parallels:**
Multiple iconographic and structural parallels exist: meditation postures, water-based ritual architecture, symbolic continuities.

**Transmission Hypothesis:**
Partial cultural transmission is supported by specific continuities difficult to explain by coincidence, genetic evidence of eastward population movement, and absence of fire sacrifice in both contexts.

**Synthesis Model:**
The evidence best supports shared cultural substrate and partial transmission rather than direct lineage or complete discontinuity.

**Limitations:**
Definitive proof precluded by chronological gap, undeciphered IVC script, and inability to exclude convergent evolution.
