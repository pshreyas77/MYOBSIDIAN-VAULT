# Corrected Religious Archaeology Evidence Gallery

This gallery presents the oldest verifiable archaeological evidence for major world religions, with corrections based on accurate archaeological dating.

## 1. Mesopotamian Religion — **c. 5000 BCE** (Corrected from 3500 BCE)

**Oldest Evidence:** The **temple complex at Eridu**, with the oldest mud-brick temple levels dating to the **Ubaid period (c. 5000 BCE)**. The Kish Tablet (c. 3500 BCE) was an administrative economic document, not religious evidence.

![Eridu temple remains - oldest Mesopotamian religious site c. 5000 BCE](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/i.natgeofe.com/504be6944cab36467f15482aec1d33bfdc2ebc63.jpg)

![Eridu temple reconstruction - ancient Sumerian religious architecture](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/i.natgeofe.com/903391918752e602530076e283aee592647a436d.jpg)

## 2. Ancient Egyptian Religion — **c. 3400 BCE** (Corrected from 3100 BCE)

**Oldest Evidence:** The **temple remains at Hierakonpolis (Nekhen)**, dedicated to the falcon god Horus. This is the earliest known cult building in Egypt, predating the Karnak complex by millennia.

![Hierakonpolis temple site - oldest Egyptian temple remains c. 3400 BCE](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/madainproject.com/1066536b5cd32e440895b18b545220d60ac32008.jpg)

![Hierakonpolis temple basement revetment - archaeological drawing](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/ancientegyptonline.co.uk/560529682673d94e1cf5c530a75aecf06db2f64e.jpeg)

## 3. Ancient Greek Religion — **c. 1400–1200 BCE** (Verified Correct)

**Oldest Evidence:** **Linear B clay tablets** from Knossos and Pylos recording offerings to Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, Athena, and Dionysos. These prove the Olympian pantheon was worshipped in the Bronze Age.

![Linear B tablet with religious offerings to Greek gods](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/www.worldhistory.org/a84ac553281afb82b82f7d5bf8596addfe064ce7.jpg)

---

## 4. Hinduism — **c. 113 BCE** (Corrected from 1380 BCE)

**Oldest Evidence:** The **Heliodorus Pillar at Vidisha (Besnagar)**, erected by a Greek ambassador to the court of the Shunga king. It is dedicated to **Vasudeva (Krishna/Vishnu)** and features a Garuda capital. The Bogaz Koi inscription (1380 BCE) was a Mitanni treaty in Anatolia, not evidence of Hindu practice in India.

![Heliodorus Pillar capital - Garuda sculpture dedicated to Vasudeva c. 113 BCE](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/kevinstandagephotography.wordpress.com/7876a380c1c5f3a68974749a2fe1b15699544267.jpg)

![Heliodorus Pillar full capital - oldest Hindu archaeological evidence in India](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/kevinstandagephotography.wordpress.com/544ef9d628e3f3fb15732578db959d3f0e7621f8.jpg)

## 5. Zoroastrianism — **c. 530–520 BCE** (Partially Corrected)

**Oldest Evidence:** The **Tomb of Cyrus the Great at Pasargadae** (c. 530 BCE) contains subtle religious symbolism, while the **Behistun Inscription** (c. 520 BCE) is the first indisputable epigraphic evidence naming Ahura Mazda.

![Tomb of Cyrus the Great at Pasargadae - oldest Zoroastrian site c. 530 BCE](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/www.worldhistory.org/2943b0550f0aefd13c9de657a203c81822579202.jpg)

![Behistun Inscription - Darius invoking Ahura Mazda c. 520 BCE](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/www.worldhistory.org/b597aab678bc26f99f9e2704321fc18cc4c2fbdb.jpg)

## 6. Judaism — **c. 600 BCE** (Verified Correct)

**Oldest Evidence:** The **Ketef Hinnom silver amulets**, containing the Priestly Benediction (Numbers 6:24–26). These are the oldest surviving biblical texts, housed at the Israel Museum.

![Ketef Hinnom silver amulets - oldest biblical text c. 600 BCE](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/www.worldhistory.org/5d997d26e06ac7b75106744f40cd3605d9f63047.jpg)

---

## 7. Jainism — **3rd Century BCE** (Corrected from 8th Century BCE)

**Oldest Evidence:** The **Lohanipur torso**, a polished sandstone statue of a Jina (Tirthankara) from Patna, dated to the **Mauryan period (3rd century BCE)**. The Kankali Tila stupa was actually from the **2nd century BCE**, not the 8th century BCE.

![Lohanipur Jain torso - oldest Jain sculpture 3rd century BCE](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/upload.wikimedia.org/95d50e65048bc1c1d37990d2a4cdf3f2e49d0ea6.jpg)

---

## 8. Buddhism — **c. 3rd Century BCE** (Verified Correct)

**Oldest Evidence:** **Ashoka's Lumbini Pillar**, with an inscription identifying the site as the Buddha's birthplace and recording Ashoka's visit.

![Ashoka Pillar at Lumbini - oldest Buddhist archaeological evidence](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/blog-img-dev.s3.ap-south-1.amazonaws.com/53829447f6e2fc2a788be9165ce64464269986f2.jpg)

---

## 9. Christianity — **c. 125–175 CE** (Verified Correct)

**Oldest Evidence:** **Rylands Library Papyrus P52**, the oldest surviving New Testament fragment, containing verses from the Gospel of John.

![Rylands Papyrus P52 - oldest New Testament fragment c. 125-175 CE](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/i0.wp.com/eb7f8472fad09e4ae075fc669dd8446fac6ee39e.jpg)

---

## 10. Islam — **c. 568–645 CE** (Verified Correct)

**Oldest Evidence:** The **Birmingham Quran manuscript**, carbon-dated to 568–645 CE, housed at the University of Birmingham.

![Birmingham Quran manuscript - carbon-dated to 568-645 CE](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/th-thumbnailer.cdn-si-edu.com/0bac90145cb7ab6f3ae1a367eb99637e6ef25cb4.jpg)

---

## 11. Sikhism — **c. 1570s CE** (Corrected from 1599 CE)

**Oldest Evidence:** The **Goindval Pothis**, transcribed by Sahansar Ram (grandson of Guru Amar Das) between **1570–1595 CE**. These contain Gurbani compositions from the Sikh Gurus and Bhagats.

![Goindval Pothi - oldest Sikh manuscript c. 1570-1595 CE](https://kimi-web-img.moonshot.cn/img/c8.alamy.com/4da320b8aadcf2664012507320b5bdf4487d6da5.jpg)

## Key Corrections Made

| Original Claim | Correction | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Kish Tablet (3500 BCE) | **Eridu temple (5000 BCE)** | Kish Tablet was administrative, not religious |
| Karnak Temple (3100 BCE) | **Hierakonpolis (3400 BCE)** | Karnak is New Kingdom (~2000 BCE); Hierakonpolis is oldest known cult building |
| Bogaz Koi (1380 BCE) | **Heliodorus Pillar (113 BCE)** | Bogaz Koi was in Anatolia, not evidence of Hindu practice in India |
| Kankali Tila (8th c. BCE) | **Lohanipur torso (3rd c. BCE)** | Kankali Tila stupa is actually 2nd c. BCE, not 8th c. BCE |
| Adi Granth (1599 CE) | **Goindval Pothis (1570s CE)** | Goindval Pothis predate the 1599 manuscript |
