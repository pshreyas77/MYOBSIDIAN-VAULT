### **CO1 — AutoCAD Basics**

**Q1. What is the importance of AutoCAD?**
> **Ans:** AutoCAD is used for creating accurate 2D and 3D technical drawings. It increases productivity, ensures precision, allows easy editing/modification, and is widely used in mechanical, civil, electrical, and architecture fields.

**Q2. Name any 5 modify commands in AutoCAD.**
> **Ans:** COPY, MOVE, ROTATE, SCALE, MIRROR, ARRAY, OFFSET, TRIM, EXTEND, FILLET, CHAMFER, STRETCH, BREAK, JOIN, ERASE (any 5).

**Q3. What is the use of the TRIM command?**
> **Ans:** TRIM removes unwanted parts of an object that extend beyond a cutting edge.

**Q4. What is the use of the OFFSET command?**
> **Ans:** OFFSET creates a parallel copy of an object at a specified distance.

**Q5. What is the full form of CAD?**
> **Ans:** Computer Aided Design.

**Q6. What is the difference between COPY and OFFSET?**
> **Ans:** COPY duplicates an object to a new location. OFFSET creates a parallel copy at a fixed distance from the original.

### **CO2 — Engineering Curves**

**Q7. Define eccentricity.**
> **Ans:** Eccentricity (e) is the ratio of the distance from the center to the focus (c) to the distance from the center to the vertex (a). Formula: **e = c/a**.
> - Ellipse: e < 1
> - Parabola: e = 1
> - Hyperbola: e > 1

**Q8. Define involute.**
> **Ans:** An involute is the curve traced by the end point of a taut string as it is unwound from a circle or polygon. It is used in gear tooth profiles.

**Q9. Define cycloid.**
> **Ans:** A cycloid is the curve traced by a point on the circumference of a circle as it rolls along a straight line without slipping.

**Q10. Define epicycloid.**
> **Ans:** An epicycloid is the curve traced by a point on the circumference of a circle that rolls on the **outside** of another fixed circle.

**Q11. Define hypocycloid.**
> **Ans:** A hypocycloid is the curve traced by a point on the circumference of a circle that rolls on the **inside** of another fixed circle.

**Q12. Define helix.**
> **Ans:** A helix is a curve generated by a point moving uniformly around a cylinder while simultaneously moving parallel to its axis.

### **CO3 — Projections of Points & Lines**

**Q13. What is the angle between HP and VP in orthographic projection?**
> **Ans:** **90°** (They are mutually perpendicular).

**Q14. Draw the projection of a point lying on HP and 30 mm in front of VP.**
> **Ans:** Front View (a') is **on XY line**. Top View (a) is **30 mm below XY**.

**Q15. Draw the projection of a point lying on VP and 40 mm above HP.**
> **Ans:** Front View (a') is **40 mm above XY**. Top View (a) is **on XY line**.

**Q16. What is the distance between the projectors of two points if they are 30 mm apart?**
> **Ans:** **30 mm** (horizontal distance between their vertical projectors).

**Q17. A point is 20 mm above HP and 30 mm behind VP. Where is its Front View?**
> **Ans:** Front View is **20 mm above XY**.

**Q18. A point is 20 mm above HP and 30 mm behind VP. Where is its Top View?**
> **Ans:** Top View is **30 mm above XY** (behind VP → above XY in First Angle).

**Q19. What is the locus of a point that moves equidistant from a fixed point and a fixed straight line?**
> **Ans:** **Parabola**.

**Q20. What is the locus of a point that moves such that the sum of its distances from two fixed points is constant?**
> **Ans:** **Ellipse**.

### **CO4 — Projections of Planes & Solids**

**Q21. What is the front view of a circular plane when it is perpendicular to HP and parallel to VP?**
> **Ans:** **True circle** (true shape) because it is parallel to VP.

**Q22. What is the top view of a circular plane when it is perpendicular to HP and parallel to VP?**
> **Ans:** **Straight line** (edge view) because it is perpendicular to HP.

**Q23. In first angle projection, the ______ lies between ______ and ______.**
> **Ans:** **(d) Object, projection plane, observer** (Order: Observer → Object → Plane).

**Q24. In third angle projection, the order is ______.**
> **Ans:** Observer → Projection Plane → Object.

**Q25. What is the front view of a square plane when it is parallel to HP?**
> **Ans:** **Straight line** (edge view) parallel to XY.

**Q26. What is the top view of a square plane when it is parallel to HP?**
> **Ans:** **True square** (true shape).

**Q27. What is the trace of a plane?**
> **Ans:** The line of intersection of a plane with the reference plane (HP or VP).

**Q28. What is the difference between first angle and third angle projection?**
> **Ans:** In First Angle, the object is between observer and plane. In Third Angle, the plane is between observer and object.

**Q29. What is the front view of a line parallel to both HP and VP?**
> **Ans:** **True length** (parallel to XY).

**Q30. What is the top view of a line perpendicular to HP?**
> **Ans:** **Point** (the line projects as a point).

> **Ans:** **120°** between each pair.

**Q34. What is the angle between the isometric axis and horizontal?**
> **Ans:** **30°**.

**Q35. What is the ratio of isometric length to true length?**
> **Ans:** **0.815** (or approximately **82%**).

**Q36. What is the reduction factor in isometric projection?**
> **Ans:** **0.815** (or 81.5%).

**Q37. What is an isometric circle?**
> **Ans:** A circle drawn in isometric projection appears as an **ellipse**.

**Q38. How do you toggle between isoplanes in AutoCAD?**
> **Ans:** Press **F5** key.

**Q39. What is the use of SNAP STYLE → Isometric in AutoCAD?**
> **Ans:** It sets the cursor to move along isometric angles (30°, 90°, 150°).

**Q40. What type of line represents the center line in engineering drawing?**
> **Ans:** **Chain line** (long dash — short dash — long dash).
