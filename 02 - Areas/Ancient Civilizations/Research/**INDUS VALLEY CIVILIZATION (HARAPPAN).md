## 📋 ESSENTIAL FACTS

**📍 Location:** Pakistan, Northwest India (Indus-Ghaggar basin)  
**📅 Age:** 5,300 years old (3300–1300 BCE)  
**MATURE PHASE:** 2600–1900 BCE  
**🏛️ Type:** Bronze Age urban civilization  
**🔬 Dating Method:** Radiocarbon dating, stratigraphy, OSL dating  
**🏆 UNESCO Status:** Multiple World Heritage Sites

---

## **🔬 SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE**

**Dating Methods:**
- Radiocarbon dating (C-14)
- Optically Stimulated Luminescence (OSL)
- Thermoluminescence (TL)
- Stratigraphic analysis
- Dendrochronology

**Key Dates Verified:**
- **Early Harappan:** 3300–2600 BCE (regionalization)
- **Mature Harappan:** 2600–1900 BCE (urbanization peak)
- **Late Harappan:** 1900–1300 BCE (deurbanization)
- **Bhirrana (Haryana):** 7500 BCE (early farming phase - OSL dating)

**Verification:**
- Archaeological Survey of India
- Deccan College, Pune
- Physical Research Laboratory, Ahmedabad
- Peer-reviewed in Nature, Science, Journal of Archaeological Science

## **🏛️ MAJOR CITIES**

**Mohenjo-Daro (Sindh, Pakistan):**
- "Mound of the Dead"
- Population: 40,000
- Area: 200 hectares
- Great Bath, Granary, Assembly Hall
- UNESCO World Heritage Site (1980)

**Harappa (Punjab, Pakistan):**
- Population: 23,000
- Area: 150 hectares
- Granaries, working platforms
- First excavated site (1920s)

**Dholavira (Gujarat, India):**
- Area: 100 hectares
- Water management system
- Large signboard with Indus script
- UNESCO World Heritage Site (2021)

**Lothal (Gujarat, India):**
- World's oldest dockyard
- Bead-making factory
- Fire altars
- Trade with Mesopotamia

**Rakhigarhi (Haryana, India):**
- **Largest IVC site** (350 hectares)
- Recent DNA studies (2019)
- 7 skeletons analyzed

## **📜 INDUS SCRIPT**

**Characteristics:**
- **400+ distinct symbols**
- **4,000+ inscriptions** found
- Average length: 5 signs
- Longest inscription: 26 signs
- Written right-to-left
- Found on seals, tablets, pottery

**Status:**
- **UNDECIPHERED** (despite 100+ attempts)
- Not bilingual (no Rosetta Stone equivalent)
- Language unknown (Dravidian? Indo-Aryan? Isolate?)
- Possibly logo-syllabic writing

**Theories:**
- Dravidian language family (majority view)
- Indo-Aryan (minority view)
- Language isolate
- Not writing (symbol system only) - controversial

---

## **🏗️ URBAN PLANNING**

**City Layout:**
- **Grid pattern** streets (north-south, east-west)
- **Citadel** (raised western platform)
- **Lower city** (residential area)
- Standardized brick size (4:2:1 ratio)
- House walls aligned with cardinal directions

**Architecture:**
- Mud-brick & baked brick construction
- Multi-story houses (2-3 floors)
- Central courtyards
- Private bathrooms & toilets
- Wells in houses

**Sanitation:**
- **Covered drainage system** (world's first)
- Household drains → street drains → main sewers
- Manholes for cleaning
- Soak pits for wastewater
- Advanced for Bronze Age

## **🌾 ECONOMY & AGRICULTURE**

**Crops:**
- Wheat, barley
- Peas, sesame, mustard
- Cotton (first civilization to cultivate)
- Rice (later period)
- Millets

**Domesticated Animals:**
- Cattle (zebu/humped)
- Water buffalo
- Sheep, goats
- Chickens (possibly first domesticated)
- **No horses** (debated - late period?)

**Industries:**
- Cotton textile production
- Bead-making (carnelian, steatite)
- Pottery (wheel-made)
- Metallurgy (bronze, copper, lead)
- Shell working
- Seal carving

---

## ** TRADE NETWORKS**

**Internal Trade:**
- Raw materials → cities
- Finished goods → villages
- Standardized weights (binary & decimal)
- Barter system

**External Trade:**
- **Mesopotamia** (Sumer, Akkad, Babylon)
- Mesopotamian texts mention "Meluhha" (Indus region)
- Exports: cotton, timber, ivory, carnelian, lapis lazuli
- Imports: gold, silver, tin

**Trade Evidence:**
- Indus seals found in Mesopotamia
- Mesopotamian cylinder seals in Indus cities
- Dockyard at Lothal
- Warehouse facilities

## **🎨 ART & CRAFTS**

**Seals:**
- Steatite (soapstone)
- Square format (2×2 cm average)
- Animal motifs (unicorn bull, elephant, rhino, tiger)
- Indus script above animals
- Used for trade/administration

**Sculpture:**
- **Dancing Girl** (bronze, 10.5 cm)
- **Bearded Priest** (steatite, 17.5 cm)
- **Bronze buffalo, ram, elephant**
- Terracotta figurines (women, animals)

**Pottery:**
- Wheel-made
- Red clay with black painted designs
- Geometric patterns, animals, plants
- Perforated pottery (strainers)

**Jewelry:**
- Gold, silver, copper
- Carnelian beads (etched)
- Faience (glazed steatite)
- Shell bangles

---

## **💀 BURIAL PRACTICES**

**Cemetery H Culture (Late Harappan):**
- Extended inhumation
- Grave goods (pottery, ornaments)
- Urn burials (cremation?)

**Rakhigarhi Burials:**
- Primary burials
- Flexed position
- Ornaments (bangles, beads)
- No human sacrifice evidence

**DNA Studies (2019):**
- 7 skeletons from Rakhigarhi
- Ancient DNA extracted
- No Steppe ancestry (pre-Aryan migration)
- Ancestral South Indian (ASI) component
- Published in Science (2019)

## **🔍 EXCAVATION HISTORY**

**1829:** Charles Masson first reports Harappa  
**1856:** Bricks used for railway construction  
**1872-75:** Alexander Cunningham excavates Harappa  
**1920s:** Daya Ram Sahni (Harappa), R.D. Banerji (Mohenjo-Daro)  
**1930s:** Mortimer Wheeler (stratigraphy)  
**1947:** Partition (sites split between India & Pakistan)  
**1960s-70s:** George Dales, Walter Fairservis  
**1980s-present:** Archaeological Survey of India, Pakistani Dept. of Archaeology  
**2019:** Rakhigarhi DNA study published

## **📉 DECLINE THEORIES**

**Climate Change (Majority View):**
- **4.2 kiloyear event** (2200 BCE global drought)
- Monsoon weakening
- Ghaggar-Hakra river drying up
- Agricultural collapse

**Tectonic Activity:**
- Earthquakes
- River course changes
- Flooding

**Economic Factors:**
- Trade decline with Mesopotamia
- Resource depletion
- Over-exploitation of land

**NOT Aryan Invasion:**
- **No evidence** of mass violence
- **No burned cities** (except Mohenjo-Daro - debated)
- **No weapons** or battle damage
- **Gradual deurbanization** (not sudden collapse)
- Genetic evidence shows no mass migration ~1900 BCE
