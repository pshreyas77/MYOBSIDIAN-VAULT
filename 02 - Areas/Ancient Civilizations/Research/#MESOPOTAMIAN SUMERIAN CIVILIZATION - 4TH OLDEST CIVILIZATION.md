## 📋 ESSENTIAL FACTS

**📍 Location:** Tigris-Euphrates Valley (Southern Iraq)  
**📅 Age:** 6,500 years old (4500–1750 BCE)  
**URUK PERIOD:** 4000–3100 BCE (first cities)  
**🏛️ Type:** World's first literate civilization  
**🔬 Dating Method:** Radiocarbon dating, stratigraphy, cuneiform records  
**🏆 UNESCO Status:** Uruk, Ur (tentative list)

---

## **⚡ KEY FACTS (Minimal)**

✓ **World's first civilization** with writing  
✓ **Invention of cuneiform** (~3400 BCE)  
✓ **First cities:** Uruk, Ur, Eridu, Lagash, Nippur  
✓ **First written law code:** Code of Ur-Nammu (~2100 BCE)  
✓ **Ziggurat temples** (stepped pyramids)  
✓ **Invention of wheel** (~3500 BCE)  
✓ **First literature:** Epic of Gilgamesh (~2100 BCE)  
✓ **60-based number system** (time measurement)  
✓ **First irrigation systems**  
✓ **Kesh Temple Hymn** (2600 BCE - oldest religious text)

---

## **📏 PHYSICAL DETAILS**

- **Territory:** Southern Mesopotamia (Iraq)  
- **Major cities:** Uruk (largest, 80,000 people), Ur, Eridu, Kish, Lagash  
- **Ziggurat of Ur:** 64m × 46m base, 30m height  
- **Uruk city walls:** 9.5 km circumference  
- **Writing system:** Cuneiform (wedge-shaped)  
- **Materials:** Mud-brick, reed, bitumen  
- **Discovery:** 1840s (first excavations)

## **🔬 SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE**

**Dating Methods:**
- Radiocarbon dating (organic materials)
- Stratigraphy (layer-by-layer excavation)
- Ceramic typology (pottery sequencing)
- Cuneiform king lists
- Dendrochronology (wooden artifacts)

**Key Dates Verified:**
- **Ubaid Period:** 6500–3800 BCE (early settlements)
- **Uruk Period:** 4000–3100 BCE (first cities, writing)
- **Early Dynastic:** 2900–2350 BCE (city-states)
- **Akkadian Empire:** 2334–2154 BCE (first empire)
- **Ur III Dynasty:** 2112–2004 BCE (neo-Sumerian)
- **Old Babylonian:** 1894–1595 BCE (Hammurabi)

**Verification:** University of Chicago, British Museum, Penn Museum, multiple labs

## **🏛️ MAJOR CITIES**

**Uruk (Erech):**
- Population: 40,000–80,000 (largest city in world ~2800 BCE)
- Area: 6 km²
- Famous for: First writing, ziggurat, Epic of Gilgamesh

**Ur:**
- Royal Cemetery (2600–2500 BCE)
- Ziggurat of Ur (best preserved)
- Birthplace of Abraham (biblical)

**Eridu:**
- **Oldest city** in Sumer (5400 BCE)
- First temple (dedicated to Enki)
- 18 occupation levels

**Lagash:**
- Independent city-state
- Vulture Stele (earliest historical document)
- Gudea statues

**Nippur:**
- Religious center (not political)
- Temple of Enlil (chief god)
- Thousands of cuneiform tablets

---

## **📜 CUNEIFORM WRITING**

**Development:**
- **3400 BCE:** Proto-cuneiform (pictographs)
- **3200 BCE:** Early cuneiform (simplified)
- **2600 BCE:** Full syllabic writing
- **2400 BCE:** Literary texts appear

**Materials:**
- Clay tablets (most common)
- Reed stylus (triangular tip)
- Sun-dried or kiln-baked

**Content:**
- Administrative records
- Legal documents
- Literature (Epic of Gilgamesh)
- Religious texts
- Scientific texts (math, astronomy)
- Letters

**Decipherment:**
- 1846: Henry Rawlinson deciphers Behistun Inscription
- Multiple languages: Sumerian, Akkadian, Hittite, Persian

---

## **⚖️ LAW & GOVERNANCE**

**Code of Ur-Nammu (2100–2050 BCE):**
- **Oldest known law code** (300 years before Hammurabi)
- Written in Sumerian
- 57 laws preserved
- Fines instead of retaliation
- Protects widows, orphans, poor

**Code of Lipit-Ishtar (1930 BCE):**
- Predecessor to Hammurabi's Code
- 38 laws preserved

**Governance:**
- City-states (independent)
- Lugal (king) or Ensi (governor)
- Temple economy
- Bureaucracy with scribes

## **🔬 SCIENTIFIC CONTRIBUTIONS**

**Mathematics:**
- Base-60 number system (sexagesimal)
- 60 seconds/minute, 60 minutes/hour
- 360 degrees in circle
- Pythagorean theorem (1000 years before Pythagoras)
- Quadratic equations

**Astronomy:**
- Lunar calendar (354 days)
- Zodiac division (12 signs)
- Planetary observations
- Eclipse predictions

**Technology:**
- Wheel (3500 BCE)
- Plow (agricultural revolution)
- Sailboat (trade)
- Pottery wheel
- Bronze metallurgy

## **📚 LITERATURE**

**Epic of Gilgamesh (~2100 BCE):**
- **World's oldest epic poem**
- King Gilgamesh of Uruk (historical figure ~2700 BCE)
- Flood story (pre-dates Biblical Noah)
- Quest for immortality
- 12 tablets

**Other Works:**
- Kesh Temple Hymn (2600 BCE - oldest religious text)
- Instructions of Shuruppak (wisdom literature)
- Lament for Ur (poetry)
- Love poems

## **💀 RELIGION**

**Major Gods:**
- **Anu** (sky father)
- **Enlil** (air, storms - chief god)
- **Enki** (water, wisdom)
- **Inanna/Ishtar** (love, war)
- **Utu/Shamash** (sun, justice)
- **Nanna/Sin** (moon)

**Beliefs:**
- Polytheistic (many gods)
- Gods control nature & fate
- Afterlife: dark underworld (Irkalla)
- Temple priests as intermediaries
- Divination (reading omens)
