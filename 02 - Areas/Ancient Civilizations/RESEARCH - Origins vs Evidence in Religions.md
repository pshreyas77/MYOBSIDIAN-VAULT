# Origins vs Evidence: Definitive Analysis

## Two Distinct Questions

| Question | Metric | Answer |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Q1: Which tradition has older *historical origins*?** | Linguistic reconstruction, textual philology | Vedic/Brahmanical (~1500–1000 BCE) |
| **Q2: Which tradition has older *physical evidence*?** | Archaeological artifacts, epigraphy | Buddhism (250 BCE) |

---

## Archaeological Evidence (Physical Self-Identifying Artifacts)

| Tradition | First Artifact | Date | Evidence |
|-----------|---------------|------|----------|
| **Buddhism** | Ashokan Major Rock Edicts | 250 BCE | Stone naming Buddha, Dhamma, Sangha |
| **Jainism** | Hathigumpha Inscription | 2nd–1st c. BCE | Stone naming Tirthankaras |
| **Vedic/Hindu** | Heliodorus Pillar | 113 BCE | Stone naming Vasudeva-Krishna |

---

## Historical Origins (Linguistic Dating)

| Tradition | Key Event | Date Range | Method |
|-----------|-----------|------------|--------|
| **Vedic** | Rigveda composition | 1500–1000 BCE | Linguistic philology |
| **Jainism** | Mahavira's lifetime | ~497–425 BCE | Cross-referenced in Buddhist texts |
| **Buddhism** | Buddha's lifetime | ~480–400 BCE | Pali Canon, Ashokan edicts |

---

## Why the Gap Exists

| Tradition | Preservation | Survival |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Vedic** | Oral transmission (shruti); writing ritually impure | ❌ Poor - palm-leaf decays |
| **Buddhist/Jain** | Written + monumental; stone stupas, pillars | ✅ Excellent - stone endures |

---

## Key Scholarly Sources

- **Jamison & Brereton** (2014) - *The Rigveda*
- **Bronkhorst** (2007) - *Greater Magadha*
- **Thapar** (2002) - *Early India*
- **Witzel** (1995) - Early Sanskritization

---

## Bottom Line

> **Buddhism has older proof. Vedic tradition has older roots.**

This is NOT contradictory - they measure different things.
