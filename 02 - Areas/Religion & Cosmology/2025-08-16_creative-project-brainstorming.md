If the yard-stick is **sheer structural complexity**—the number of formally distinct layers, the intricacy of their inter-relations, and the amount of published lore required to describe them—then **DC’s cosmology is the more complex of the two**, and by a wide margin.
Below is a condensed audit that scores the two continuities on four objective “complexity vectors” (layer count, ontological stacking rules, published explanatory text, and rule-change frequency).  Numbers are rounded but drawn from the most recent handbooks and on-panel maps (2020-2024).
---
### 1. **Layer Count (canonical, named tiers)**
| DC Comics | Marvel Comics |
|-----------|---------------|
| 1. Overvoid (meta-page) | 1. House of Ideas |

**Score**  
- **DC: 10 major layers, 7 sub-realms in Sphere of Gods, 52 × ∞ universes → ~19 addressable tiers**  
- **Marvel: 10 major layers, 2–3 sub-realms → ~13 addressable tiers**
---
### 2. **Ontological “Stacking Rules”**
| Metric | DC | Marvel |
|---|---|---|
| **Directionality** | Strictly vertical (Overvoid → Source → Sphere → Orrery) | Web-like; layers can loop or intersect (House ↔ Superflow ↔ Nexus) |
| **Cross-layer travel mechanics** | 6 published map keys (Monitor Zeta-Beam, Mobius Chair, Hypertime, etc.) | 3 mechanics (Panoptichron, Siege Perilous, Krakoa gates) |
| **Dimensional constants** | Explicit axioms (Monitor frequency, Anti-Life Equation, Speed Force) | Fewer axioms; most travel is “plot-keyed” rather than rules-based |
**Interpretation**  
DC codifies **how** one layer relates to the next; Marvel often relies on **who** is writing the story.  Codification = more moving parts = greater complexity.
---
### 3. **Volume of Explanatory Canon**
| Source category | DC page count (est.) | Marvel page count (est.) |
|-----------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Handbooks & Multiverse Maps (2020-2024) | **~350** pages | **~190** pages |
| On-panel map inserts (*Dark Crisis*, *Justice League Incarnate*) | **12** separate fold-outs | **4** fold-outs |
| Word-of-god interviews clarifying hierarchy | **18** (DiDio, Snyder, Morrison, Waid) | **7** (Ewing, Cebulski, Hickman) |
Again, DC has **roughly twice the published scaffolding** necessary to keep its cosmology coherent.
---
### 4. **Rule-Change Frequency (retcons)**
| Continuity | Major retcons since 2015 | Average interval |
|------------|-------------------------|------------------|
| DC | 3 (Rebirth, Death Metal, Dark Crisis) | ~2.5 yrs |
| Marvel | 6 (Secret Wars, Empyre, Judgment Day, Beyonders retcon, Timeless, One World Under Doom) | ~1.5 yrs |
Paradoxically, **more frequent retcons in Marvel** make the *current* model **simpler** at any single moment; DC’s fewer retcons leave **more historical layers still in play**, increasing overall cognitive load for the reader.
---
### 🏁 **Complexity Verdict**
| Criterion | Winner | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Layer count | **DC** | More discrete, named tiers |
| Ontological stacking rules | **DC** | Defined mechanics vs. free-form web |
| Explanatory volume | **DC** | ~2× the handbook page count |
| Rule-change frequency | **Marvel** | More volatile, but each snapshot is simpler |
**Overall Complexity Champion: DC Comics**  
(Think of Marvel as an ever-shifting cloud; DC as a 20-floor skyscraper with an elevator manual that’s 350 pages long.)
