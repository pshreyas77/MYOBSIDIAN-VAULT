# Demographic Transition Lag: India and China Population Boom

## Summary
The population booms in India and China followed similar patterns but differed in timing and government intervention. Both experienced a death rate decline due to post-WWII modern medicine, while birth rates remained high for decades, creating a gap that caused massive population surges. China's boom was halted by the One-Child Policy in 1980, while India's slower cultural/economic transition allowed the boom to persist longer, leading India to overtake China in population in 2023.

## China

| Period       | Growth Driver                                                                 |
|--------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1949–1979    | Mao's pro-natalist policy: "More people = more power." Death rate dropped via basic healthcare. Birth rate stayed high. |
| Peak growth  | ~2.8% annual in 1960s                                                        |
| Brake        | One-Child Policy 1980 → growth collapsed                                     |
| Main reason  | Post-war stability + Maoist ideology + healthcare without birth control      |

## India

| Period       | Growth Driver                                                                 |
|--------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1921–1951    | Slow rise — famines, epidemics still killing many                            |
| 1951–1981    | Fastest phase: ~2.2–2.5% annual                                              |
| Why          | Colonial-era death rate collapsed post-independence via DDT, antibiotics, famine control — but birth rate stayed high for 2–3 more decades (classic demographic lag) |
| Now          | TFR dropped to ~2.0 (2023), but momentum carries population forward          |
| Main reason  | Death rate fell fast, birth rate fell slow = gap = boom. Called "demographic transition lag." |

## Key Difference
- **China**: State-enforced birth suppression stopped boom hard (1980)
- **India**: Cultural/economic transition slower → boom lasted longer → India overtook China in 2023

## Root Cause (Shared)
Colonial/pre-modern era = high death + high birth (balanced). Post-WWII modern medicine = death rate crashes → birth rate takes 30–50 years to follow → massive population surge during that gap.
