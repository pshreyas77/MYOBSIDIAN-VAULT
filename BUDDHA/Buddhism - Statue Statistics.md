# Buddhism: Statue Statistics

## Overview

Buddhism has **by far the most statues** of any philosophical tradition - estimated in the **millions worldwide**.

## Notable Collections

### Longmen Grottoes, China
- **97,000+ Buddhist statues**
- Buddha, Bodhisattvas, Arhats
- UNESCO World Heritage Site

### Single Temple Records
- One temple in China: **3,000 Buddha statues** in one hall
- Wat Pho, Thailand: Reclining Buddha alone is 46m

---

## Why Buddhism Has So Many

1. **Anthropomorphic tradition** - Buddha depicted in human form
2. **Bodhisattva worship** - Many additional figures
3. **Royal patronage** - Kings funded massive construction
4. **Pilgrimage culture** - Merit-making through statue dedication
5. **Mahayana expansion** - Countless Buddha realms and figures

---

## Archaeological Evidence

### Oldest Buddhist Structure
- **Lumbini, Nepal**: Timber postholes and bodhigara remains
- **Date**: ~550 BCE (6th century BCE)
- **Significance**: Earliest confirmed Buddhist worship site
- **Verification**: Radiocarbon and luminescence dating

### Ashokan Evidence
- **250 BCE**: Ashokan edicts - first self-identifying Buddhist artifacts
- Stone inscriptions naming Buddha, Dhamma, Sangha
