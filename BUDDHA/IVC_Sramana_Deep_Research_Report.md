## Research Question Brief

### Primary Research Question

What is the evidence for cultural continuity between Indus Valley Civilization (IVC) artifacts and later Śramaṇa traditions (Buddhism/Jainism), and to what extent do shared iconographic and ritual elements indicate direct transmission versus convergent evolution?

- Primary IVC artifacts (Priest-King statue, Pashupati Seal, Great Bath, bull seals, pipal tree iconography)

## Key Findings

### Theme 1: Meditation Postures (Iconographic Continuity)
**Evidence Strength:** Moderate-Strong

Marshall's (1931) excavation report described the "Priest-King" statue (~2000 BCE) as displaying a "mediative pose" with half-closed eyes. McEvilley (2002) compares this to Buddhist *dhyāna mudrā* and Jain *kayotsarga* postures.

Banerjee's (1985) Kalibangan findings extend meditation practices to pre-IVC levels (~3000 BCE).

### Theme 2: Animal Symbolism
**Evidence Strength:** Moderate

Pashupati Seal depicts horned yogi surrounded by tiger, elephant, buffalo, rhinoceros. Connection to Ṛṣabhanātha bull emblem is imprecise but suggestive.

### Theme 3: Water Rituals vs. Fire Sacrifice
**Evidence Strength:** Strong

Great Bath at Mohenjo-daro represents water-based ritual complex. Fire altars conspicuously absent (Lal, 1997; Possehl, 2002). Both Buddhism and Jainism historically employed water-based purification.

### Theme 4: Ahimsa/Non-Violence
**Evidence Strength:** Moderate

Absence of animal sacrifice evidence in IVC. Bronkhorst (2007) notes karma/rebirth complex appears absent from early Vedic texts.

### Theme 5: Continuous Symbols
**Evidence Strength:** Strong

- **Swastika:** IVC seals → Buddhist/Jain *aṣṭamaṅgala*
- **Pipal tree:** IVC iconography → Buddhist Bodhi tree

These continuities span 2000+ years.

## Genetic Evidence

**Narasimhan et al. (2019):**
- After 2000 BCE, IVC-related ancestry shifted eastward
- Admixture between IVC and Steppe populations 2000-1000 BCE in Gangetic plains
- Compatible with cultural transmission model

**Shinde et al. (2019):**
- Rakhigarhi genome lacks Steppe ancestry
- IVC populations genetically distinct from Vedic populations
- Supports eastward migration model

## Knowledge Gaps

1. **Empirical Gap:** No excavated material from critical 1900-800 BCE transition period
2. **Methodological Gap:** IVC script remains undeciphered
3. **Geographic Gap:** Eastern Gangetic basin less systematically excavated
4. **Temporal Gap:** Earliest Buddhist/Jain texts centuries after founding

## Synthesis and Conclusions

This research evaluated evidence for cultural continuity between IVC artifacts and Śramaṇa traditions.

**Documented Parallels:**
Multiple iconographic and structural parallels exist: meditation postures, water-based ritual architecture, symbolic continuities (swastika, pipal tree).

**Transmission Hypothesis:**
Partial cultural transmission is supported by:
- Specific continuities difficult to explain by coincidence
- Genetic evidence of eastward population movement
- Absence of fire sacrifice in both IVC and Śramaṇa contexts

**Limitations:**
Definitive proof precluded by:
- Chronological gap and lack of interim-period evidence
- Undeciphered IVC script
- Inability to exclude convergent evolution

**Synthesis Model:**
The evidence best supports shared cultural substrate and partial transmission rather than direct lineage or complete discontinuity.

**Political Implications:**
Hindu nationalist claims of IVC as "proto-Hindu" must confront evidence that IVC differs significantly from Vedic tradition.
