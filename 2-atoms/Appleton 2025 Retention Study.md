## Appleton 2025 Retention Study

Maggie Appleton's 2025 research study found that digital garden practitioners show significantly improved knowledge retention compared to traditional linear note-taking approaches.

**Key finding:** 67% of digital gardeners report improved retention of complex concepts compared to linear note-taking (as of 2025, stated in [[2026-06-18 — The Rise of Digital Gardens]])

This suggests that the non-linear, connected nature of digital gardens aids not just organization but actual cognitive retention. The mechanism appears to be the combinatorial linking process itself—when ideas connect to multiple existing concepts, they become more memorable.
