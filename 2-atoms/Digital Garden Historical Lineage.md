This note explains the historical lineage of digital gardens from Vannevar Bush's memex concept to Ward Cunningham's WikiWikiWeb. It traces the intellectual ancestry of modern personal knowledge management systems.

## Digital Garden Historical Lineage

The digital garden movement has two key ancestral concepts:

**Vannevar Bush's Memex (1945):**
Bush envisioned a mechanical device that would store all books, records, and communications, allowing users to follow associative trails through linked information. This concept of associative linking predates the World Wide Web by decades and established the foundational idea that knowledge should be connected non-linearly.

**Ward Cunningham's WikiWikiWeb (1995):**
Cunningham created the first wiki, implementing Bush's vision of associative trails on the web. The WikiWikiWeb allowed users to create and link pages dynamically, establishing bidirectional links as a core feature of networked knowledge systems. This became the direct technical precursor to modern digital garden tools.

**Key claim:** Modern digital gardens (Obsidian.md, Roam Research, Logseq) implement the memex concept through wiki-style bidirectional linking (as of 2026-05, stated in [[2026-06-18 — The Rise of Digital Gardens]])
