## Digital Garden

A digital garden is a personal knowledge base that is publicly accessible and evolves through continuous refinement (Vanessa Groot, 2024). Unlike chronological blogs or social media feeds, digital gardens emphasize:

1. **Evergreen content** — Notes that grow and change over time rather than static, time-stamped posts
2. **Combinatorial growth** — Ideas connect in non-linear ways, creating emergent patterns through linking
3. **Public thinking** — The garden displays work-in-progress, not only polished conclusions

The approach traces its lineage to Vannevar Bush's memex concept (1945) and Ward Cunningham's WikiWikiWeb (1995), but modern implementations leverage tools like Obsidian.md, Roam Research, and Logseq.

**Key claim:** Digital gardens work best when users commit to daily refinement sessions of 15-20 minutes (as of 2026, stated in PKMI study cited in source).
