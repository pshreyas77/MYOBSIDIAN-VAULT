## Definition

A digital garden is a personal knowledge base that is publicly accessible and evolves through continuous refinement. Unlike traditional blogs where posts are time-stamped and rarely updated, digital gardens emphasize:

- **Evergreen content** — notes that grow and change over time rather than static posts
- **Combinatorial growth** — ideas connect in non-linear ways, creating emergent patterns
- **Public thinking** — the garden shows work-in-progress, not just polished conclusions

## Historical Lineage

The digital garden movement traces back to:
- Vannevar Bush's memex concept (1945)
- Ward Cunningham's WikiWikiWeb (1995)

Modern implementations leverage Obsidian.md, Roam Research, and Logseq.
