## Bidirectional Links

Bidirectional links (also called backlinks) are a core technical requirement for digital gardens. They enable notes to reference each other in both directions, creating a navigable network rather than a static hierarchy.

When note A links to note B, the system automatically displays:
- Forward links: what A points to (traditional hyperlinks)
- Backward links: what points to A (backlinks — the "bidirectional" part)

This enables combinatorial growth because ideas connect in non-linear ways. Users discovering note A also see all other notes that reference it, surfacing unexpected connections.

Modern tools implementing bidirectional linking:
- **Obsidian.md** — Local-first with graph view
- **Roam Research** — Database-style bidirectional references
- **Logseq** — Outline-based with block-level backlinks

**Key claim:** 67% of digital gardeners report improved retention of complex concepts compared to linear note-taking (as of 2025, stated in Appleton research cited in source).
