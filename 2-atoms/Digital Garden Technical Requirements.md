## Digital Garden Technical Requirements

Four technical capabilities are necessary for a functional digital garden:

**Bidirectional Links (Backlinks):**
Every note must show what other notes link to it. This enables networked thought—the garden grows not just from new notes, but from discovering unexpected connections between existing notes.

**Graph Visualization:**
Users must be able to see the structure of their knowledge graph visually. This reveals clusters, bridges, and isolated concepts that would be invisible in a list or folder hierarchy.

**Version History:**
Every note must track changes over time. Since gardens are evergreen (not static posts), the ability to see what changed and when is essential for understanding how thinking has evolved.

**Concept-Based Search:**
Search must work by concept, not just by date or keyword. Users need to find ideas by meaning, not chronology. Tagging and full-text search enable this.

**Key claim:** Digital gardens require bidirectional links, graph visualization, version history, and concept-based search (as of 2026-05, stated in [[2026-06-18 — The Rise of Digital Gardens]])
