## Digital Garden Core Characteristics

Digital gardens are defined by three fundamental characteristics:

**Evergreen Content:**
Unlike blog posts that are time-stamped and static, digital garden notes are designed to grow and change over time. A note written today can be updated tomorrow, next month, or next year as understanding evolves. This creates living documents that improve rather than decay.

**Combinatorial Growth:**
Ideas in a digital garden connect in non-linear ways, creating emergent patterns that wouldn't surface in chronological feeds. When notes link bidirectionally, new connections emerge that the author didn't explicitly plan—combinatorial explosion of insight.

**Public Thinking:**
Digital gardens show work-in-progress, not just polished conclusions. This contrasts with social media's highlight reels and traditional publishing's final-draft-only approach. The garden metaphor embraces cultivation over performance.

**Key claim:** Digital gardens are defined by evergreen content, combinatorial growth, and public thinking (as of 2026-05, stated in [[2026-06-18 — The Rise of Digital Gardens]])
