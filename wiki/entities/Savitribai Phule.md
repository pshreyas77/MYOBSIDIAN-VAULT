# [[Savitribai Phule]]

## Overview
Indian social reformer, educationalist, and poet who, along with her husband Jyotirao Phule, played a pivotal role in women's education and anti-caste struggle in 19th century Maharashtra. She is regarded as the first female teacher of India and a pioneering figure in feminism and social reform.

## Key Attributes
- Born: January 3, 1831
- Died: March 10, 1897 (aged 66)
- Nationality: Indian
- Ideology: Feminism, Anti-caste, Social reform, Humanism, Rationalism
- Known for: Pioneer of women's education, First female teacher in India, Anti-caste activism

## Relationships
- Related to: [[Jyotirao Phule]] - Husband and fellow reformer
- Related to: [[B. R. Ambedkar]] - Intellectual heir in anti-caste and educational reform
- Related to: [[Periyar E. V. Ramasamy]] - Fellow social reformer and rationalist
- Related to: [[Vidyasagar]] - Contemporary advocate for women's education
- Part of: [[Satyashodhak Samaj]], [[Women's Education Movement in India]]
- Influenced by: Jyotirao Phule's teachings, experience of caste and gender discrimination, rationalist thought
- Influenced: Women's education movement, Feminist movements in India, Dalit liberation struggle

## Major Works and Contributions
- **"Kavya Phule" (1854)** - Collection of poems
- **"Bavan Kashi Subodh Ratnakar" (1892)** - Collection of poems and speeches
- Numerous speeches, letters, and educational materials
- Poetry in Marathi advocating for education and social justice

## Historical Contributions
- **Women's Education**: Co-founded first girls' school in Pune (1848) with Jyotirao Phule
- **Teacher Training**: Trained as teacher and trained other women as teachers
- **Educational Access**: Worked to provide education to girls and children of lower castes
- **Anti-Caste Work**: Participated in Satyashodhak Samaj activities against caste discrimination
- **Widow Care**: Established care center for widows (1854)
- **Against Infanticide**: Worked to prevent female infanticide
- **Against Child Marriage**: Advocated against child marriage practices
- **Literary Contributions**: Used poetry to spread awareness about social issues

## Philosophical Contributions
- **Feminist Perspective**: Combined gender justice with anti-caste struggle
- **Egalitarian Vision**: Advocated for equal educational opportunities regardless of gender or caste
- **Rationalist Approach**: Applied reason to social problems rather than accepting tradition
- **Humanist Ethics**: Emphasis on human welfare and dignity as basis for social reform
- **Critique of Patriarchy**: Challenged religious and social sanctions of gender inequality

## Legacy
- **Educational Legacy**: Pioneered formal education for women and girls in India
- **Feminist Legacy**: Considered one of the first modern Indian feminists
- **Anti-Caste Legacy**: Worked alongside Jyotirao Phule in anti-caste struggle
- **Social Reform Legacy**: Influenced later women's rights and social reform movements
- **Literary Legacy**: Marathi poetry continues to be read for its social relevance
- **Cultural Impact**: Remembered as pioneering woman educator and social reformer

## Notes
- Born into a Mali (gardener) farming family in Naigaon, Satara (now Maharashtra)
- Illiterate at marriage, Jyotirao educated her at home
- Trained as teacher at Ms. Farar's Institution in Ahmednagar
- Opened first indigenous girls' school in Pune on January 1, 1848
- Faced severe opposition including social ostracism and physical harassment
- Continued teaching despite having cow dung and stones thrown at her
- Adopted a son from a widowed Brahmin, Yashwantrao, who became a doctor
- Worked during plague epidemic in Pune (1897) and contracted the disease
- Died while serving plague patients, considered a martyr to humanitarian cause
- Her birthday (January 3) is celebrated as Balika Din (Girl's Day) in Maharashtra
