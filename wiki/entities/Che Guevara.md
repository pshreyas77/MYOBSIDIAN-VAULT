# [[Che Guevara]]

## Overview
Argentine Marxist revolutionary, physician, author, guerrilla leader, diplomat, and military theorist. A major figure of the Cuban Revolution, his stylized visage has become a ubiquitous countercultural symbol of rebellion and global insignia in popular culture.

## Key Attributes
- Born: June 14, 1928
- Died: October 9, 1967 (aged 39)
- Nationality: Argentine (naturalized Cuban)
- Ideology: Marxism-Leninism, Guevarism, Focoism, Internationalism
- Known for: Cuban Revolution, Guerrilla warfare, "Guerrillero Heroico" photograph, "The Motorcycle Diaries"

## Relationships
- Related to: [[Bhagat Singh]] - Shared Marxist revolutionary ideals and anti-imperialist struggle
- Related to: [[Indian Atheism, Rationalism, and Anti-Caste Struggle]] - Part of broader anti-caste and rationalist tradition
- Part of: [[Cuban Revolution]], [[Latin American Left]]
- Influenced by: Motorcycle journey across South America, 1954 Guatemalan coup
- Influenced: Revolutionary movements worldwide, New Left

## Notes
- Radicalized by witnessing poverty during motorcycle journey across South America
- Cemented revolutionary beliefs after 1954 CIA-backed coup in Guatemala
- Popularized Focoism - theory that small guerrilla vanguard could spark revolution
- Successful in Cuba but failed in Congo and Bolivia due to lack of local support
- Advocated creation of "New Man" driven by moral rather than material incentives
- Viewed religion through materialist lens as potential tool of oppression
- Executed at age 39 in Bolivia, becoming global martyr symbol
- Iconic "Guerrillero Heroico" photograph by Alberto Korda remains among most reproduced images globally
- Visited India in 1959 post-Cuban Revolution to establish diplomatic ties
- Met Nehru but reportedly omitted radical Indian revolutionaries in reports
