# [[M. N. Roy]]

## Overview
Indian revolutionary, radical activist and political theorist, who founded the Communist Party of India (in exile) and later developed the philosophy of Radical Humanism. He was a major figure in the international communist movement and a significant influence on left-wing thought in India.

## Key Attributes
- Born: March 21, 1887
- Died: January 25, 1954 (aged 66)
- Nationality: Indian
- Ideology: Marxism, Radical Humanism, Republicanism, Internationalism
- Known for: Founder of Communist Party of India, Radical Humanism, International communist movement

## Relationships
- Related to: [[B. R. Ambedkar]] - Contemporary social reformer with different approach
- Related to: [[Bhagat Singh]] - Fellow Marxist revolutionary and atheist
- Related to: [[Periyar E. V. Ramasamy]] - Contemporary rationalist and social reformer
- Related to: [[Rammanohar Lohia]] - Socialist contemporary
- Part of: [[Communist Party of India]], [[International Communist Movement]]
- Influenced by: Marxism, Bolshevik Revolution, experience in Mexican and Chinese revolutions
- Influenced: Left-wing politics in India, Radical Humanism movement, democratic socialism

## Major Works and Contributions
- **"India in Transition" (1922)** - Analysis of Indian society and revolutionary prospects
- **"Revolution and Counter-revolution in China" (1930)** - Analysis of Chinese revolution
- **"The Historical Role of Islam" (1939)** - Study of Islam's historical development
- **"Reason, Romanticism and Revolution" (1952)** - Critique of idealism in philosophy
- **"New Humanism" (1940s)** - Development of Radical Humanism philosophy

## Historical Contributions
- **Communist Party of India**: Founded CPI in exile in Tashkent (1920)
- **International Communist Movement**: Active in Comintern, represented India in international forums
- **Indian National Congress**: Early associate, later expelled for dual membership
- **Radical Humanism**: Developed philosophy emphasizing freedom, reason, and ethics
- **Political Organizations**: Founded Radical Democratic Party (1940), League of Radical Congressmen
- **Constitutional Advice**: Advised on constitutional matters during independence period
- **Cultural Work**: Wrote extensively on philosophy, politics, history, and religion

## Philosophical Contributions
- **Radical Humanism**: Philosophy centered on human freedom, reason, and ethical autonomy
- **Critique of Idealism**: Challenge to idealist philosophies in favor of scientific materialism
- **Democratic Socialism**: Advocacy for democracy combined with socialist economics
- **Scientific Temperament**: Emphasis on scientific method and rational inquiry
- **Ethics of Freedom**: Moral philosophy based on individual freedom and social responsibility

## Legacy
- **Political Legacy**: Influenced communist and socialist movements in India
- **Philosophical Legacy**: Radical Humanism continues to influence secular and humanist thought
- **Intellectual Legacy**: Contributions to political theory, philosophy, and history
- **Institutional Legacy**: Organizations founded continue in various forms
- **Cultural Impact**: Remembered as pioneering Indian revolutionary and thinker

## Notes
- Originally associated with Jugantar revolutionary group in Bengal
- Traveled to USA in 1915, joined Indian independence activists in New York
- Went to Mexico in 1917, participated in socialist and anarchist circles
- Lived in Soviet Union during early 1920s, became disillusioned with Bolshevik dictatorship
- Expelled from Comintern in 1929 for deviations from communist line
- Returned to India in 1930, worked to organize communist and socialist forces
- Arrested by British authorities in 1931, imprisoned until 1936
- Developed Radical Humanism during 1940s as alternative to both communism and fascism
- Elected as President of Indian Rationalist Association in 1946
- Advocated for decentralized democracy and economic democracy
- His writings continue to be read by those interested in radical politics and humanist philosophy
