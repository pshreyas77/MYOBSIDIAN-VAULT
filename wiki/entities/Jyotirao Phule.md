# [[Jyotirao Phule]]

## Overview
Indian social activist, thinker, anti-caste reformer and writer from Maharashtra. He, along with his wife Savitribai Phule, pioneered women's education in India and worked for the upliftment of marginalized communities including Dalits and women.

## Key Attributes
- Born: April 11, 1827
- Died: November 28, 1890 (aged 63)
- Nationality: Indian
- Ideology: Anti-caste, Feminism, Rationalism, Humanism, Socialism
- Known for: Satyashodhak Samaj, Women's education, Gulamgiri (Slavery), Critique of Brahmanism

## Relationships
- Related to: [[Savitribai Phule]] - Wife and fellow reformer
- Related to: [[B. R. Ambedkar]] - Intellectual heir in anti-caste struggle
- Related to: [[Periyar E. V. Ramasamy]] - Fellow anti-caste reformer
- Related to: [[Narayana Guru]] - Contemporary social reformer in South India
- Part of: [[Satyashodhak Samaj]], [[Anti-Caste Movement in Maharashtra]]
- Influenced by: Thomas Paine's "Rights of Man", experience of caste discrimination, Christian missionary teachings
- Influenced: Dalit movements, Ambedkar's thought, feminist movements, social reform in Maharashtra

## Major Works and Contributions
- **"Gulamgiri" (1873)** - Comparison of caste system to slavery, critique of Brahmanical hegemony
- **"Shetkaryanche Asud" (1883)** - Cultivator's Whipcord, critique of peasant exploitation
- **"Brahmananche Kasab" (1869)** - Brahmin Priesthood, critique of religious exploitation
- **"Ghulamgiri"** - Marathi work on slavery and caste parallels
- **"Vijlipi"** - Textbook for Satyashodhak Samaj schools

## Historical Contributions
- **Women's Education**: Opened first girls' school in Pune (1848) with Savitribai
- **Anti-Caste Work**: Challenged Brahminical supremacy and untouchability practices
- **Satyashodhak Samaj (1873)**: Founded truth-seeking society for social equality
- **Farmer's Rights**: Advocated for exploited peasant communities
- **Widow Remarriage**: Supported and practiced widow remarriage
- **Egalitarian Schools**: Opened schools for children of all castes and communities
- **Religious Critique**: Challenged caste-based religious practices and texts
- **Social Reform**: Worked against child marriage, female infanticide, and gender discrimination

## Philosophical Contributions
- **Critique of Brahmanism**: Analyzed caste as system of social exploitation
- **Egalitarian Vision**: Society based on equality of opportunity regardless of birth
- **Rationalist Approach**: Applied reason to social problems rather than tradition
- **Humanist Ethics**: Morality based on human welfare and dignity
- **Marxist Influences**: Early analysis of class exploitation in Indian context
- **Feminist Perspective**: Advocacy for women's rights and education

## Legacy
- **Educational Legacy**: Pioneered women's and mass education in India
- **Anti-Caste Legacy**: Foundation for later Dalit movements and Ambedkar's work
- **Social Reform Legacy**: Influenced temple entry movements, inter-caste marriage advocacy
- **Political Legacy**: Inspired Bahujan politics and Dalit assertion movements
- **Cultural Impact**: Statues, memorials, commemorations across Maharashtra
- **Academic Influence**: Sociology, history, political science, gender studies

## Notes
- Born into Mali (gardener) caste, considered Shudra in varna system
- Married Savitribai when she was 9 and he was 21 (common practice at time)
- Educated Savitribai at home, then trained her as teacher
- Faced severe opposition from Brahminical communities for educating girls and lower castes
- Participated in assistance to victims of famines and droughts
- Advocated for inter-dining and inter-marriage across caste lines
- Criticized both British colonialism and Brahminical hegemony as exploitative
- His work laid foundation for Dalit Buddhist movement decades later
- Considered one of the first modern Indian feminists
- Titled "Mahatma" (great soul) by Vithalrao Krishnaji Vandekar in 1888
