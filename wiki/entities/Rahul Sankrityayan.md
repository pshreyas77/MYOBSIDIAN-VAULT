# [[Rahul Sankrityayan]]

## Overview
Indian writer, Buddhist scholar, polymath, Marxist theorist, and prolific translator who is considered the father of Indian travel literature. He was a major figure in spreading Buddhist studies in India and a vocal advocate for social justice and rationalism.

## Key Attributes
- Born: April 9, 1893
- Died: April 14, 1963 (aged 70)
- Nationality: Indian
- Ideology: Marxism, Buddhism, Rationalism, Internationalism, Humanism
- Known for: Buddhist scholarship, Travel writing, Marxist analysis, "Volga Se Ganga"

## Relationships
- Related to: [[B. R. Ambedkar]] - Fellow Buddhist scholar and social reformer
- Related to: [[Periyar E. V. Ramasamy]] - Fellow rationalist and social reformer
- Related to: [[Jyotirao Phule]] - Anti-caste reformer and inspiration
- Related to: [[Bhagat Singh]] - Shared Marxist and rationalist leanings
- Part of: [[Buddhist Revival Movement]], [[Indian Marxist Movement]]
- Influenced by: Buddhism, Marxism, Indian independence movement, extensive travels
- Influenced: Buddhist studies in India, Travel literature, Marxist historiography

## Major Works and Contributions
- **"Volga Se Ganga" (1943)** - Historical fiction spanning from Volga to Ganges
- **"Buddhacharya"** - Life and teachings of Buddha
- **"Darshan Digdarshan"** - Introduction to philosophy
- **"Madhya Asia Ka Itihas"** - History of Central Asia
- Numerous translations from Sanskrit, Pali, Tibetan, Russian, German, French, etc.

## Historical Contributions
- **Buddhist Scholarship**: Major contributor to Buddhist studies in India
- **Travel Literature**: Pioneered travel writing as literary genre in Hindi
- **Marxist Analysis**: Applied historical materialism to Indian and Asian history
- **Nationalism**: Active participant in Indian independence movement
- **Internationalism**: Traveled extensively across Asia and Europe
- **Translation Work**: Made Buddhist texts accessible in Indian languages
- **Social Advocacy**: Used writings to criticize social injustices and superstitions

## Philosophical Contributions
- **Buddhist Rationalism**: Emphasized reason and experience in Buddhist practice
- **Historical Materialism**: Applied Marxist methodology to historical analysis
- **Scientific Temperament**: Promoted scientific thinking and skepticism
- **Humanist Ethics**: Morality based on human welfare and social progress
- **Internationalist Outlook**: Emphasis on global solidarity beyond nationalism

## Legacy
- **Literary Legacy**: Pioneer of Hindi travel literature and historical fiction
- **Scholarly Legacy**: Foundational contributions to Buddhist studies in India
- **Intellectual Legacy**: Bridged Marxism, Buddhism, and Indian rationalism
- **Educational Legacy**: Works used in academic curricula across India
- **Cultural Impact**: Remembered as polymath and fearless truth-seeker

## Notes
- Originally named Ramdarash Sharma, later adopted name Rahul Sankrityayan
- Took sannyas (ascetic vows) and became Buddhist monk (Bhikshu)
- Later returned to householder life and married
- Traveled extensively: Soviet Union, Tibet, China, Japan, Sri Lanka, Europe
- Imprisoned multiple times for anti-British activities
- Taught at University of Leningrad (now St. Petersburg) in 1930s
- Awarded Padma Bhushan in 1963 (posthumously)
- Considered one of the most learned Indians of his time
- His vast personal library formed basis of Sankrityayan Granthagara
