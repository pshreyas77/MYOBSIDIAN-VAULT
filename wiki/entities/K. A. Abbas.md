# [[K. A. Abbas]]

## Overview
Indian film director, novelist, screenwriter, and journalist who was an atheist, Marxist, and vocal advocate for scientific temper and social justice. He used his artistic talents to promote progressive values and challenge social orthodoxies.

## Key Attributes
- Born: June 7, 1914
- Died: June 1, 1987 (aged 72)
- Nationality: Indian
- Ideology: Marxism, Atheism, Scientific Temper, Humanism
- Known for: Filmmaking, Journalism, IPTA, Scientific temper advocacy

## Relationships
- Related to: [[B. R. Ambedkar]] - Contemporary social reformer
- Related to: [[Periyar E. V. Ramasamy]] - Fellow rationalist and atheist
- Related to: [[M. N. Roy]] - Fellow Marxist and radical humanist
- Related to: [[Bhagat Singh]] - Shared atheism and Marxist leanings
- Part of: [[Indian People's Theatre Association]], [[Progressive Writers' Movement]]
- Influenced by: Marxism, Indian independence movement, scientific rationalism
- Influenced: Parallel cinema, socially relevant journalism, scientific temper movement

## Major Works and Contributions
- **"Inquilab" (1942)** - Novel about Indian independence movement
- **"Dharti Ke Lal" (1946)** - Film based on Bengal famine
- **"Pardesi" (1957)** - Award-winning film about African student in India
- **"Saat Hindustani" (1969)** - Film that launched Amitabh Bachchan's career
- Journalistic work in Blitz newspaper and other publications
- Advocacy for scientific temper through writings and speeches

## Historical Contributions
- **IPTA Activism**: Active member of Indian People's Theatre Association
- **Progressive Writers' Movement**: Contributed to literary movement for social change
- **Journalism**: Used journalism to advocate for secularism and scientific thinking
- **Filmmaking**: Created socially relevant parallel cinema
- **Scientific Temper Advocacy**: Wrote and spoke extensively about need for scientific approach
- **Peace Activism**: Involved in nuclear disarmament and peace movements
- **Cultural Exchange**: Promoted Indo-African cultural understanding through films

## Philosophical Contributions
- **Atheist Advocacy**: Openly advocated atheism and criticized religious orthodoxy
- **Scientific Temperament**: Emphasized scientific thinking as essential for progress
- **Humanist Ethics**: Morality based on human welfare and social justice
- **Marxist Analysis**: Applied class analysis to Indian society and culture
- **Secular Worldview**: Advocated for separation of religion from state and public life

## Legacy
- **Cinematic Legacy**: Pioneered socially relevant Indian cinema
- **Journalistic Legacy**: Fearless advocacy for secular and progressive values
- **Scientific Temper Legacy**: Contributed to promotion of scientific thinking in India
- **Cultural Legacy**: Bridged Indian and African cultural understanding
- **Educational Legacy**: Films used in educational contexts for social awareness
- **Inspiration**: Continues to inspire artists and activists committed to social change

## Notes
- Born in Panipat, Punjab (now Haryana) into a Muslim family
- Graduated from University of Delhi with degree in English literature
- Started career as journalist in 1935 with Bombay Chronicle
- Joined Communist Party of India in 1940s
- Founded IPTA unit in Bombay and worked to mobilize artists for social causes
- Wrote extensively in Urdu, Hindi, and English languages
- His film "Saat Hindustani" won National Film Award for Best Feature Film
- Received Padma Shri in 1969 for his contributions to arts
- Remained committed to secular, socialist ideals throughout his life
- His autobiography provides insights into his intellectual journey
- Continued writing and advocating for scientific temper until his death
