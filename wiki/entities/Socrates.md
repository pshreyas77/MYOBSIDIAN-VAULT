# Socrates

**Born**: ~470 BCE, Athens  
**Died**: 399 BCE (hemlock), Athens  
**Influence**: All Western philosophy  
**Status**: #entity #philosopher #rank-6-overall

---

## Philosophy

### Core Method: Elenchus (Cross-examination)
- Question until opponent admits ignorance
- Destroy false confidence in knowledge
- "I know that I know nothing" = beginning of wisdom

### Socratic Paradoxes
- Unexamined life = not worth living
- Better to suffer injustice than commit it
- Virtue = knowledge (if you know good, you do good)

---

## Comparison: Socrates vs Buddha

| Criterion | Socrates | Buddha |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Method | Elenchus | Ehipassiko |
| Core claim | "I know nothing" | "Come and see" |
| Writing | Nothing (oral) | Nothing (oral) |
| Death | Chose death over compromise | Died naturally |
| Social ethics | Accepted Athenian hierarchy | Rejected caste |
| Path | Philosophical dialogue | Eightfold Path |

**Parallel**: Both said "I don't know" was start of wisdom. Both taught through questioning. Both executed/died rather than compromise.

**Difference**: Buddha built complete system. Socrates stayed in doubt.

---

## Comparison: Socrates vs Nagarjuna

**Unexpected parallel**:
- Both destroy positions without asserting own
- Both expose contradictions
- Both claim no positive doctrine

**Difference**: Nagarjuna = systematic (MMK = 450 verses). Socrates = conversational, oral only.

**Verdict**: Nagarjuna = Socrates with logical precision + 600 years sophistication.

---

## Comparison: Socrates vs Chanakya

**Complete opposites**:
- Socrates: "Examined life = only life worth living"
- Chanakya: "Unexamined power = only power worth having"

- Socrates: died for principles
- Chanakya: dying for principles = strategic failure

**Verdict**: Most interesting contrast in philosophy.

---

## Death

399 BCE — charged with corrupting the youth and denying official gods.

Refused exile (offered by jury). Refused escape (planned by friends).

Chose hemlock.

> "The unexamined life is not worth living for a human being."

---

## Legacy

- Shifted philosophy from nature physics to ethics
- Socratic dialogues = literary genre
- Paradox method = foundation of Western philosophical inquiry
- **Highest philosophical integrity**: Only philosopher on this list who chose death over intellectual compromise

**Weakness**: Known only through others (Plato, Xenophon). Cannot separate historical Socrates from Platonic Socrates.

**Rating**: 8/10 — massive influence, highest integrity, but no system built.
