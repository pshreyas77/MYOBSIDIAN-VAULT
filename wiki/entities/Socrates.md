# Socrates

**Type:** #entity
**Category:** Philosopher
**School:** Classical Greek Philosophy
**Dates:** 470-399 BCE
**Created:** 2024-04-17

## Summary
Athenian philosopher who founded Western philosophy. No written works; known through Plato's dialogues and Xenophon's memorabilia. Executed by drinking hemlock in 399 BCE for "corrupting the youth" and "impiety."

## Key Doctrines
- **"Know thyself"** (γνῶθι σεαυτόν)
- **"The unexamined life is not worth living"**
- **Virtue is knowledge** - no one does wrong willingly
- **Socratic method** (elenchus) - question-and-answer to expose contradictions
- **Maieutics** (midwifery) - drawing out ideas through questioning

## Method
The Socratic method involves:
1. Asking clarifying questions
2. Finding contradictions in claims
3. Reaching deeper understanding through elimination

## Death
- Accused by Meletus, Anytus, and Lycon
- Trial described in Plato's *Apology*
- Refused escape (Crito)
- Death scene in *Phaedo*
- Last words about sacrificing a cock to Asclepius

## Legacy
- Shifted philosophy from nature physics to ethics/politics
- Socratic dialogues became literary genre
- Redefined what it means to be a philosopher
- Martyred "gadfly" of Athens
