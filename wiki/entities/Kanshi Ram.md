# [[Kanshi Ram]]

## Overview
Indian politician and social reformer who worked for the empowerment and political mobilization of Dalits, backward classes, and minorities. He founded the Bahujan Samaj Party (BSP) and the Backward and Minority Communities Employees Federation (BAMCEF), dedicating his life to creating political power for marginalized communities.

## Key Attributes
- Born: March 15, 1934
- Died: October 9, 2006 (aged 72)
- Nationality: Indian
- Ideology: Bahujanism, Social justice, Democratic socialism, Ambedkarism
- Known for: Founder of Bahujan Samaj Party, BAMCEF, Dalit political mobilization

## Relationships
- Related to: [[B. R. Ambedkar]] - Intellectual and ideological inspiration
- Related to: [[Periyar E. V. Ramasamy]] - Fellow social reformer with different regional focus
- Related to: [[Mayawati]] - Political successor who led BSP to electoral victories
- Related to: [[Bhagat Singh]] - Shared commitment to anti-caste struggle
- Part of: [[Bahujan Samaj Party]], [[BAMCEF]], [[Dalit Rights Movement]]
- Influenced by: Ambedkar's writings, experience of caste discrimination, socialist movements
- Influenced: Dalit politics, Bahujan mobilization, reservation policy implementation, regional politics

## Major Works and Contributions
- **"The Chamcha Age" (1982)** - Analysis of Dalit collaborators with ruling classes
- **"Dalit Shoshit Samaj Sangharsh Sahayak"** - Organization for Dalit exploited society struggle help
- **"The Untouchable India"** - Analysis of untouchability in India
- Various pamphlets and speeches on Bahujan politics and social transformation

## Historical Contributions
- **BAMCEF (1973)**: Founded to create educated and employed Dalit, OBC, and minority activists
- **DS-4 (Dalit Shoshit Samaj Sangharsh Samiti)**: Revolutionary wing for direct action
- **Bahujan Samaj Party (1984)**: Founded to capture political power for Bahujans (Dalits, tribals, OBCs, minorities)
- **Political Mobilization**: Created first successful Dalit-led political party in North India
- **Electoral Strategy**: Focused on winning elections through Bahujan consolidation
- **Cadre Development**: Trained thousands of activists from marginalized communities
- **Institutional Building**: Created sustainable organizations for long-term struggle

## Philosophical Contributions
- **Bahujan Philosophy**: Political philosophy representing majority against minority rule
- **Political Power Focus**: Emphasis on capturing state power as means of social transformation
- **Critique of Chamchas**: Analysis of collaborators who betray Dalit interests for personal gain
- **Mass Line Approach**: Combining ideological clarity with mass mobilization
- **Electoral Politics**: Using democratic processes to achieve social justice goals

## Legacy
- **Political Legacy**: BSP remains significant force in Uttar Pradesh and national politics
- **Social Legacy**: Empowered millions of Dalits to claim dignity and rights
- **Electoral Legacy**: Demonstrated electoral viability of Dalit-led parties
- **Organizational Legacy**: BAMCEF and DS-4 continue in various forms
- **Ideological Legacy**: Bahujan concept continues to influence social justice discourse
- **Cultural Impact**: Statues, memorials, commemorations across India

## Notes
- Born into a Ramdasia Sikh family considered untouchable in Punjab
- Worked as a scientist in Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)
- Resigned from government job in 1964 to dedicate life to social service
- Traveled extensively across India to understand Dalit conditions
- Inspired by Ambedkar's call to "educate, agitate, organize"
- Initially worked through Republican Party of India and Scheduled Castes Federation
- Founded BAMCEF to create cadre of educated activists from marginalized communities
- Launched Bahujan Samaj Party on Ambedkar's birth anniversary (April 14, 1984)
- First electoral victory in 1989 from Uttar Pradesh
- Served as Member of Parliament multiple times
- Mentored Mayawati who became Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh multiple times
- Continued working until his death in 2006 despite declining health
- His death marked end of an era in Dalit politics but his institutions continue
