# Karl Marx

## For future Claude
German philosopher, economist, and political theorist who developed [[Communist Manifesto]] with Friedrich Engels. Core framework: class struggle drives historical change. Born 1818, died 1883.

## Core Framework
- **Central thesis:** "The history of all society is the history of class struggles"
- **Class struggle progression:**
  - Ancient: master vs slave
  - Feudal: lord vs serf
  - Capitalist: bourgeoisie vs proletariat
- **Historical change** driven by economic conflict between ownership class and working class

## Key Concepts
- [[Class Struggle]] (central to all analysis)
- [[Bourgeoisie]] — factory/company owners, capital owners
- [[Proletariat]] — working class, sells labor
- [[Capitalism]] — creates inequality, produces its own "gravediggers" (workers who will overthrow it)
- "All that is solid melts into air" — capitalism constantly destabilizes traditions

## What He Got Right (scholars agree)
- Globalization (predicted it before it existed)
- Concentration of wealth
- Recurring economic crises
- Corporate power growth
- Worker economic insecurity

## Criticisms / What He Got Wrong
- Capitalism did not collapse as predicted
- Middle class grew in many countries
- Workers gained rights through reform, not revolution
- Communist states often became authoritarian
