# [[Rammanohar Lohia]]

## Overview
Indian socialist leader, thinker, and activist who combined Marxist socialism with anti-caste struggle. He was a prominent figure in the socialist movement in India and advocated for affirmative action policies to address historical injustices.

## Key Attributes
- Born: March 23, 1910
- Died: October 12, 1967 (aged 57)
- Nationality: Indian
- Ideology: Socialism, Marxism, Anti-caste, Democratic socialism, Sarvodaya
- Known for: Socialist leadership, Anti-caste activism, Reservation policy advocacy, "Guilty Men of India's Partition"

## Relationships
- Related to: [[B. R. Ambedkar]] - Contemporary with different approach to caste
- Related to: [[Periyar E. V. Ramasamy]] - Fellow social reformer
- Related to: [[Jyotirao Phule]] - Intellectual inspiration in anti-caste struggle
- Related to: [[Bhagat Singh]] - Shared socialist ideals
- Related to: [[Kanshi Ram]] - Later socialist who continued anti-caste politics
- Part of: [[Socialist Party (India)]], [[Praja Socialist Party]], [[Anti-Caste Movement in India]]
- Influenced by: Marxism, Gandhian socialism, experience of colonialism, nationalist movement
- Influenced: Socialist politics in India, Anti-caste discourse, Reservation policy development

## Major Works and Contributions
- **"Guilty Men of India's Partition" (1948)** - Analysis of responsibility for partition
- **"Marx, Gandhi and Socialism"** - Reconciliation of Marxism and Gandhism
- **"India, China and Russia"** - Geopolitical analysis
- **"Wheel of History"** - Philosophical work on historical materialism
- Numerous speeches and writings on socialism, democracy, and social justice

## Historical Contributions
- **Congress Socialist Party**: Early activist in socialist wing of Congress
- **Quit India Movement**: Active participant in 1942 uprising
- **Post-Independence Politics**: Founded Socialist Party (1948), later Praja Socialist Party
- **Anti-Caste Activism**: Combined class struggle with caste oppression analysis
- **Reservation Advocacy**: Early proponent of reservations for backward classes
- **Farmer's Movements**: Organized peasant movements against zamindari system
- **Civil Liberties**: Championed free speech and democratic rights
- **Foreign Policy**: Advocated non-alignment and peaceful coexistence

## Philosophical Contributions
- **Socialist Feminism**: Combined class analysis with gender oppression
- **Anti-Caste Socialism**: Integrated caste struggle into socialist framework
- **Decentralized Socialism**: Advocated for village-level planning and cooperatives
- **Critical Rationalism**: Applied reason to social and political analysis
- **Pacifist Internationalism**: Combined socialism with peace advocacy

## Legacy
- **Political Legacy**: Influenced socialist and secular politics in India
- **Intellectual Legacy**: Contributions to political theory, philosophy, and history
- **Social Legacy**: Advanced anti-caste discourse and social justice concepts
- **Policy Legacy**: Influenced reservation policies and social welfare programs
- **Cultural Impact**: Remembered as principled socialist and fearless activist

## Notes
- Studied at University of Calcutta and earned doctorate from Berlin University
- Joined Congress Socialist Party in 1934, became general secretary
- Arrested multiple times during freedom struggle and post-independence
- Advocated "Chaturvarna Vyavastha" as alternative to caste system
- Emphasized language reorganization alongside social justice
- Combined parliamentarian politics with mass mobilization
- Criticized Nehruvian socialism for being too moderate on caste issues
- His concept of "private property in land" as root of inequality influenced land reform debates
- Continued activism until his death in 1967
