# [[Bhagat Singh]]

## Overview
Indian revolutionary socialist who was influential in the Indian independence movement against British rule. Born into a family of Punjabi freedom fighters, he became a multilingual intellectual and Marxist revolutionary.

## Key Attributes
- Born: September 28, 1907
- Died: March 23, 1931 (aged 23)
- Nationality: Indian
- Ideology: Marxism, Scientific Socialism, Atheism
- Known for: Revolutionary activities, Lahore Conspiracy Case, Assembly bombing (1929), hunger strike in jail

## Relationships
- Related to: [[Che Guevara]] - Shared Marxist revolutionary ideals and anti-imperialist struggle
- Related to: [[Indian Atheism, Rationalism, and Anti-Caste Struggle]] - Part of broader anti-caste and rationalist tradition
- Part of: [[Indian Independence Movement]]
- Influenced by: Jallianwala Bagh massacre, uncle Ajit Singh
- Influenced: Revolutionary movements in India and globally

## Notes
- Transitioned from Gandhian nationalism to Marxism in late teens
- Used "symbolic violence" (1929 Assembly bombing) to make revolutionary propaganda heard during trial
- Advocated mass mobilization of workers and peasants over individual terrorism
- Believed in global solidarity and world federation of socialist states
- Wrote influential essay "Why I Am an Atheist" defending his materialist worldview
- Executed at age 23, becoming martyr symbol for purity and defiance
