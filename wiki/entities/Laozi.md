# Laozi (Lao Tzu)

**Dates**: ~6th–4th BCE (uncertain)  
**Text**: Tao Te Ching — 5,000 characters  
**Core**: Tao (The Way), Wu Wei (non-action)  
**Followers**: ~400 million Taoists  
**Status**: #entity #philosopher

---

## Philosophy

### Tao (道)
> "The Tao that can be spoken is not the eternal Tao."

- Beyond all concepts and names
- Mother of all things
- Eternal, nameless, source of everything

### Wu Wei (無為)
- Non-action / effortless action
- Flow with Tao, not against it
- Water metaphor: soft overcomes hard

### Te (德)
- Virtue that flows naturally from Tao
- Not forced, not cultivated
- Spontaneous alignment

## Comparison: Laozi vs Nagarjuna

| Criterion | Laozi | Nagarjuna |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Ultimate reality | Tao — beyond concepts | Sunyata — beyond concepts |
| Language | Fails to capture | Fails — concepts = obstacles |
| Method | Paradox/poetry | Prasanga (logical destruction) |
| Liberation | Return to natural Tao | Nirvana = cessation of grasping |

**Structural identity**: Both say ultimate = beyond language. Both use language to point beyond itself. Both say effortless/empty = liberation.

**Difference**: Laozi's Tao = positive (mother, water). Nagarjuna's Sunyata = zero positive content.

**Verdict**: Nagarjuna = Laozi with philosophical precision. Laozi = Nagarjuna with poetic beauty.
