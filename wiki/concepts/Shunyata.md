# Śūnyatā (Emptiness)

**School**: Madhyamaka Buddhism  
**Founder**: [[../entities/Nagarjuna]]  
**Status**: #concept #core-doctrine #undefeatable

---

## Definition

**Śūnyatā** does not mean "nothingness" or nihilism. It means **lack of svabhava** — inherent self-existence, independent existence, essence.

> "Emptiness is not a view; it is a remedy for all views."

---

## Key Formulations

### Svabhava (Self-nature)
Nagarjuna asked of everything: "Does it exist by its own nature?"
- If **yes** → self-caused → impossible (nothing causes itself)
- If **no** → dependent → not independent → not svabhava
- If **both** → contradiction
- If **neither** → doesn't exist

**Result**: All possibilities eliminated → **emptiness**

### Dependent Origination = Emptiness
MMK 24:19: "We declare that dependent origination is emptiness. It is a conceptual designation. It is the Middle Way."

### Sunyata-sunyata
Even emptiness itself is empty. No privileged status.

---

## Two Truths
- **Samvṛti-satya** (Conventional): Provisional, useful for daily life
- **Paramārtha-satya** (Ultimate): No doctrine — silence

**Warning**: "Emptiness" cannot be the ultimate truth — it's just a concept.

---

## Debate Power

### Destroying Brahman (Shankara)
**Nagarjuna**: "Does Brahman exist by svabhava?"

Shankara: Brahman = svayambhu (self-existent), nirvikara (unchanging)

But all four options for svabhava fail. Brahman = svabhava = impossible.

**Verdict**: Shankara's Brahman dissolved by tetralemma.

### Destroying Causation (MMK Ch. 1)
- Nothing causes itself
- Nothing is caused by other (infinite regress)
- Nothing is caused by both
- Nothing is uncaused

**Causation = empty**

### Destroying Motion (MMK Ch. 2)
Same method applied to motion/change.

## Criticisms

### Shankara's Challenge
> "Sunyata of Sunyata = infinite regress. Eventually need ground. That ground = Brahman."

### Bhakti Response
> "Emptiness = despair. Need positive reality to love/devote."

### Scientific Response
> "If all empty, no consistent laws of nature possible."
