# Class Struggle

## For future Claude
Marx's core analytical framework: history moves through conflicts between owning class and working class. Every socio-economic system has内置 contradiction between exploiters and exploited that eventually causes transformation.

## Definition
The conflict between **classes** with opposing interests — primarily between those who own the means of production (bourgeoisie) and those who labor (proletariat). This struggle is the engine of historical change.

## Three Historical Stages

| Era | Ruling Class | Subject Class |
|-----|-------------|---------------|
| Ancient | Patricians / Nobles | Slaves / Plebeians |
| Feudal | Lords / Kings | Serfs / Peasants |
| Capitalist | Bourgeoisie | Proletariat |

## Marx's Argument Flow
1. Feudal society existed
2. Merchants/business owners grew powerful (trade, colonies, industry)
3. Capitalism destroyed feudalism
4. Industry + globalization expanded
5. Workers became exploited (fragmented, repetitive labor)
6. Class conflict intensifies
7. Workers eventually revolt

## Key Claims
- **"The bourgeoisie produces its own gravediggers"** — capitalism creates the working class that will overthrow it
- **"All that is solid melts into air"** — capitalism constantly destabilizes everything; no tradition survives
- **Capitalism creates crises** — overproduction + underconsumption (workers paid too little to buy goods)
- **Globalization forced** — capitalism requires every country into world trade, destroying isolated economies

## Modern Examples
- Gig economy (Uber drivers own nothing, platform owns everything)
- Big Tech monopolies
- Automation replacing labor
- Global supply chains
- Extreme wealth inequality
