# [[Indian Atheism, Rationalism, and Anti-Caste Struggle]]

## Overview
A comprehensive historical overview of the development of atheism, rationalism, and anti-caste movements in India from ancient times to the present day, highlighting key figures, movements, and philosophical developments that challenged religious orthodoxy and the caste system.

## Core Principles
- **Rational Inquiry**: Emphasis on reason, evidence, and critical thinking over blind faith and tradition
- **Social Equality**: Opposition to hierarchical caste system and advocacy for egalitarian society
- **Scientific Temperament**: Promotion of scientific thinking and skepticism toward supernatural claims
- **Human Dignity**: Affirmation of equal worth and rights for all individuals regardless of birth
- **Religious Critique**: Examination of religious institutions and practices that perpetuate inequality

## Historical Development

### Ancient Roots (Pre-6th Century BCE)
- Early skeptical traditions in the Rigveda (Nasadiya Sukta)
- Materialist (Charvaka/Lokayata) philosophy rejecting supernaturalism
- Jain and Buddhist challenges to Vedic authority and caste distinctions
- Ashoka's promotion of dhamma and religious tolerance

### Medieval Period (6th-18th Century)
- Bhakti movement saints challenging caste and ritualism (Kabir, Ravidas, Tukaram)
- Sikhism's egalitarian principles under Guru Nanak
- Sufi mystics emphasizing inner piety over external rituals
- Lingayat movement rejecting caste and advocating social equality

### Colonial Era Reform Movements (19th Century)
- Brahmo Samaj (Rammohun Roy) opposing idol worship and caste rigidity
- Prarthana Samaj advocating inter-caste marriage and widow remarriage
- Arya Samaj (Dayanand Saraswati) rejecting caste by birth and advocating vedic authority
- Aligarh Movement promoting modern education among Muslims
- Wahabi and Faraizi movements advocating Islamic reform

### Pioneers of Anti-Caste Struggle (19th-20th Century)
- **Jyotirao Phule (1827-1890)**: Founded Satyashodhak Samaj, advocated for education of lower castes and women, criticized Brahminical hegemony
- **Savitribai Phule (1831-1897)**: Pioneered women's education, worked alongside Jyotirao in anti-caste struggle
- **Narayana Guru (1856-1928)**: Temple entry movements, "One Caste, One Religion, One God" slogan
- **Iyothee Thass (1845-1914)**: Buddhist revival among Dalits, criticized Hindu caste system
- **E. V. Ramasamy Periyar (1879-1973)**: Self-Respect Movement, rationalist critique of Hinduism, Dravidian movement leadership

### Revolutionary Marxist Trend (Early 20th Century)
- **M. N. Roy (1887-1954)**: Founder of Communist Party of India, radical humanist, criticized religious orthodoxy
- **Bhagat Singh (1907-1931)**: Marxist revolutionary, atheist writer ("Why I Am an Atheist"), anti-colonial and anti-caste fighter
- **Kanshi Ram (1934-2006)**: Founded BAMCEF and Bahujan Samaj Party, mobilized Dalits politically
- **K. A. Abbas (1914-1987)**: Journalist, filmmaker, atheist, advocated scientific temper

### Constitutional and Post-Independence Era
- **B. R. Ambedkar (1891-1956)**: Architect of Indian Constitution, led Dalit Buddhist conversion, annihilation of caste advocate
- **Rammanohar Lohia (1910-1967)**: Socialist leader, anti-caste activist, advocated reservation policies
- **Rahul Sankrityayan (1893-1963)**: Buddhist scholar, rationalist, criticized religious superstition
- **Vidyasagar (1820-1891)**: Promoted widow remarriage, women's education, challenged Brahminical orthodoxy

## Key Movements and Organizations

### Rationalist Movements
- **Rationalist Association (1885)**: Promoted scientific thinking and religious criticism
- **Indian Rationalist Association**: Continued advocacy for rationalism and secularism
- **Periyar's Self-Respect Movement**: Anti-Brahminism, women's rights, rationalist critique
- **Dravida Kazhagam**: Social reform movement rejecting caste and religion-based hierarchy

### Anti-Caste Organizations
- **Satyashodhak Samaj (Phule)**: Truth-seeking society for lower castes
- **Adi Hindu Movement**: Early Dalit assertion of non-Aryan indigenous identity
- **Adi Dravida Movement**: South Dalit movement for self-respect and equality
- **Bahujan Samaj Party (Kanshi Ram)**: Political representation for Dalits, OBCs, and minorities
- **Dalit Panthers (1970s)**: Militant Dalit rights organization inspired by Black Panthers

### Religious Reform Movements
- **Arya Samaj**: Vedic revival, caste reform, shuddhi movement
- **Ramakrishna Mission**: Neo-Vedantic universalism, social service
- **Sri Narayana Dharma Paripalana Yogam**: Ezhava community upliftment
- **Buddhist Revival Movements**: Ambedkarite Buddhism, Dalit Buddhist conversion

## Philosophical Contributions

### Materialist and Atheist Traditions
- **Charvaka/Lokayata**: Ancient Indian materialism rejecting afterlife, karma, and liberation
- **Critique of Religion**: Systematic examination of religious claims through reason
- **Scientific Temperament**: Promotion of evidence-based thinking over faith
- **Humanist Ethics**: Morality based on human welfare rather than divine command

### Anti-Caste Philosophical Frameworks
- **Critique of Brahmanism**: Examination of caste as system of social exploitation
- **Egalitarian Alternatives**: Vision of society based on equality of opportunity
- **Dalit Consciousness**: Awakening of self-respect among oppressed castes
- **Bahujan Ideology**: Political philosophy representing majority (Dalits, tribals, OBCs, minorities)

### Synthesis of Rationalism and Social Justice
- **Ambedkar's Navayana Buddhism**: Rational, egalitarian reinterpretation of Buddhism
- **Periyar's Rationalism**: Scientific approach to social reform, rejection of superstition
- **Marxist Adaptations**: Class analysis combined with caste oppression analysis
- **Secular Humanism**: Non-religious ethical framework emphasizing human dignity

## Contemporary Relevance

### Ongoing Struggles
- **Caste-Based Discrimination**: Persistence of untouchability despite legal abolition
- **Religious Intolerance**: Communal violence and majoritarian politics
- **Rationalist Suppression**: Attacks on rationalists and anti-superstition activists
- **Social Inequality**: Economic disparities along caste and religious lines

### Modern Movements
- **Rationalist International**: Global network promoting scientific skepticism
- **Dalit Rights Movements**: Continued assertion of dignity and equality
- **Women's Movements**: Intersectional struggles against caste patriarchy
- **Environmental Movements**: Critique of religiously sanctioned exploitation of nature
- **LGBTQ+ Rights**: Challenge to religiously sanctioned gender norms

### Digital Age Developments
- **Online Rationalist Communities**: Social media propagation of scientific thinking
- **Ambedkarite Buddhism Online**: Digital dissemination of Ambedkar's writings
- **Periyar Studies**: Academic and activist interest in Self-Respect Movement
- **Anti-Caste Literature**: Growing body of Dalit-Bahujan writings challenging mainstream narratives
