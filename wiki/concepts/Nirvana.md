# Nirvana

**Type:** #concept
**Domain:** Buddhist Philosophy, Soteriology
**Created:** 2024-04-17

## Definition
Nirvana (Sanskrit: निर्वाण; Pali: nibbāna) literally means "blowing out" or "quenching." In Buddhism, it refers to the liberation from the cycle of rebirth (*samsara*) and the cessation of suffering (*dukkha*). It is the ultimate goal of Buddhist practice.

## Types
1. **Sa-upādisesa-nibbāna** - Nirvana with remainder (in life)
2. **An-upādisesa-nibbāna** - Nirvana without remainder (final, at death)

## Related Entities
- [[Siddhartha Gautama (Buddha)]] - attained nirvana
- [[Buddhist Monastic Community]] - practices toward nirvana
- [[Nagarjuna]] - Madhyamaka interpretation (emptiness of nirvana itself)

## Examples
- Buddha's enlightenment under Bodhi tree
- Arhat's liberation
- Bodhisattva's postponement of nirvana for compassion

## Contradictions
>[!contradiction]
>**Positive vs. Negative Definition:** Early Buddhism describes nirvana negatively (cessation of suffering) while Mahayana traditions sometimes discuss positive attributes (bliss, transcendence). See [[Nirvana Sutra]] vs. [[Pali Canon]].

>[!contradiction]
>**Attainability:** Some schools argue nirvana is attainable in this life; others emphasize it's only fully realized at death. See [[Sa-upādisesa debate]].
