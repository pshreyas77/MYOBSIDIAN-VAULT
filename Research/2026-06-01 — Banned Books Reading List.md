## Banned Books — Complete Reading List

*Research compiled via caveman skill web search, 2026-05-31. Tiers assigned by relevance to user's intellectual stack (UCH, religion epistemology, Indian context, dark psychology).*

### Tier S — Foundational

| Book | Author | Banned Where / Why | Why Priority |
|------|--------|-------------------|--------------|
| 1984 | George Orwell | USSR, North Korea, various; totalitarianism critique | Best model of surveillance state + doublethink |
| Brave New World | Aldous Huxley | Banned USA schools, Ireland; "negative activity" | Soma-drugged dystopia more accurate than 1984 for today's world |
| The Satanic Verses | Salman Rushdie | Banned 40+ countries; Iranian fatwa issued | Directly relevant to religion epistemology work |
| Lolita | Vladimir Nabokov | Banned France, UK, NZ, Argentina; pedophilia content | Unreliable narrator done perfectly |
| Ulysses | James Joyce | Banned USA, UK; obscenity | Hardest read, changed literature permanently |
| Lady Chatterley's Lover | D.H. Lawrence | Banned UK until 1960; obscenity | Class + sexuality clash, more relevant than title |
| Mein Kampf | Adolf Hitler | Banned Germany, India (partially); hate speech | Understanding evil requires primary sources |
| The God of Small Things | Arundhati Roy | Banned India (Kerala); obscenity, caste transgression | Dalit/caste dynamics, Kerala politics, stunning prose |
| Animal Farm | Orwell | Banned USSR, China, Cuba, N. Korea | Short, devastating, read in one sitting |
| Fahrenheit 451 | Ray Bradbury | Banned/challenged USA; ironic — book about book burning | Compulsory for media analysis interests |

### Top 3 Priority for UCH Research

1. **The Satanic Verses** — religious epistemology work directly connects
2. **1984** — political analysis lens, Indian parallels strong
3. **The God of Small Things** — Telangana/AP caste dynamics parallel, Indian context

### Caveat on "Banned" Classification

"Banned" = spectrum. Some banned by governments, some challenged in school libraries, some faced prosecution. Lists online conflate these. *Mein Kampf* = ethically charged but historically essential — read critically, not devotionally.
