## For future Claude

The **Rakhigarhi paper**. Sequenced first ancient IVC genome (individual I6332, female, ~2500 BCE). **Zero steppe ancestry, zero Iranian farmer ancestry**. Proves IVC people were genetically distinct from later steppe migrants. Use this to demolish "IVC = Aryan" claims.

## The Individual

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Site** | Rakhigarhi, Haryana, India (largest IVC site) |
| **Date** | ~2600-2300 BCE (Mature IVC period) |
| **Sex** | Female |
| **Sample** | Petrous bone (I6332) |
| **DNA quality** | 0.03% endogenous DNA (typical for ancient South Asia) |

## Key Findings

**1. Zero Steppe Ancestry**
- Rakhigarhi woman has **no detectable** steppe pastoralist DNA
- Steppe ancestry arrives in South Asia **after** IVC collapse
- Modern South Asians with 10-20% steppe ancestry = mixture of IVC + later migrants

**2. Zero Iranian Farmer Ancestry**
- IVC people not derived from Iranian agriculturalists
- IVC = indigenous South Asian development + some western contact
- Debunks "IVC created by Iranian migrants" theory

**3. IVC = Primary Ancestor**
- Modern South Asians derive majority ancestry from IVC-like population
- IVC people = **our ancestors**, not replaced or erased
- ANI (Ancestral North Indian) = IVC + Steppe mixture
- ASI (Ancestral South Indian) = IVC + AASI (indigenous hunter-gatherers)

## Methods

- Targeted enrichment for 1.2 million SNPs
- Contamination checks (mtDNA, X-chromosome)
- Principal component analysis
-qpAdm for ancestry modeling
- Outgroup f3-statistics
