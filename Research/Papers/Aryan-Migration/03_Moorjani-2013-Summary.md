## For future Claude

Established the **ANI-ASI mixture model** and dated the main mixing event. Critical for showing that major population mixture happened **1900-4200 years ago** (roughly 200 BCE - 2200 CE), consistent with post-IVC migration timeline. Use for dating evidence.

## Key Findings

| Finding | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **ANI-ASI mixture dated** | 1900-4200 years ago (~200 BCE - 2200 CE) |
| **All South Asians are mixed** | Every group shows ANI + ASI ancestry |
| **Endogamy began ~70 CE** | After mixture, groups became genetically isolated |
| **ANI proportion varies** | 40-70% in most groups, higher in upper castes |
| **ASI proportion varies** | 30-60% in most groups, higher in tribal groups |

## ANI-ASI Model

```
ANI (Ancestral North Indian) = IVC + Steppe migrant mixture
    ↓
    ├── Mixture with ASI (~1900-4200 years ago)
    │
ASI (Ancestral South Indian) = IVC + AASI (Ancient Ancestral South Indian)
```

**Key insight:** Both ANI and ASI share IVC-related ancestry. The difference: ANI has additional steppe input.

## Dating Method

- **ROLLOFF** algorithm: measures decay of linkage disequilibrium
- Longer LD blocks = more recent mixture
- Calibrated using known admixture events

## Timeline

```
~2500 BCE: Rakhigarhi individual (zero steppe)
    ↓
2000-1000 BCE: Steppe migration begins
    ↓
1900-4200 years ago: Major ANI-ASI mixture event ← THIS PAPER
    ↓
~70 CE: Endogamy begins (bands close)
    ↓
Modern: All groups show mixture, but limited recent gene flow
```
