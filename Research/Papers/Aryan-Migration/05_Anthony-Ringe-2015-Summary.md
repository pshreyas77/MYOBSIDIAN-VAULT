## For future Claude

**The linguistic case for steppe homeland.** Independent of genetics — reconstructed PIE vocabulary matches steppe environment, not South Asia. Use when opponent says "genetics is colonial" — this is 150 years of comparative linguistics.

## Key Linguistic Evidence

### PIE Vocabulary Reconstructed

| PIE Word | Meaning | Significance |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| **\*sneigʷh-** | snow | Cold climate — not tropical India |
| **\*bʰeh₂ǵos** | beech tree | Temperate tree — absent in South Asia |
| **\*kʷekʷlos** | wheel | Wheeled vehicles (~3500 BCE) |
| **\*h₁éḱwos** | horse | Domesticated horse central to PIE culture |

**Critical point:** These words existed in **Proto-Indo-European** (the ancestor language). If PIE spoken in India, these words should describe Indian environment. They don't.

### Archaeological Matches

| Archaeological Feature | Location | Date |
|------------------------|----------|------|
| Earliest wheeled vehicles | Pontic-Caspian steppe | ~3500 BCE |
| Earliest horseback riding | Pontic-Caspian steppe | ~4000 BCE |
| Yamnaya culture expansion | Steppe → Europe + Asia | 3000-2000 BCE |

## The Steppe Hypothesis

**Homeland:** Pontic-Caspian steppe (modern Ukraine, Russia, Kazakhstan)

**Timeline:**
```
4500-3500 BCE: PIE spoken (reconstructed vocabulary matches this ecology)
    ↓
3500 BCE: Wheeled vehicles appear in steppe archaeology
    ↓
3000-2000 BCE: Yamnaya expansion eastward and westward
    ↓
2000-1500 BCE: Indo-Iranian branch reaches South Asia
    ↓
1500-1200 BCE: Rigveda composed in northwest India
```

## Why Out-of-India Fails Linguistically

1. **Wrong ecology:** PIE words don't match Indian flora/fauna/climate
2. **No migration memory:** Vedic texts remember movement **into** India, not out of it
3. **Loanword evidence:** Rigveda has ~4% non-IE loanwords (Dravidian, Munda) — arrived **into** existing linguistic landscape
4. **Branch relationships:** Indo-Iranian is nested within IE family tree — not the root
