## Equipment Constraint — 4kg Dumbbells

**Problem**: 4kg per hand (8kg total) is too light for traditional 2-min rest strength protocols.

**Adjustment logic**:
- **2 min rest**: For heavy loads (80-90% 1RM), CNS recovery, phosphate replenishment
- **45-60 sec rest**: For light loads/high reps (40-60% 1RM), metabolic stress, hypertrophy via time-under-tension

**4kg protocol**:
- **Rest times**: 45-60 sec all exercises (metabolic stress > mechanical tension)
- **Rep ranges**: 12-20+ reps (4kg won't approach failure at 10-12 for most movements)
- **Tempo**: 3-1-1-0 (3 sec eccentric, 1 sec pause, 1 sec concentric) to increase time-under-tension
- **Bodyweight compensation**: Add doorway rows, table rows, pike pushups to supplement 4kg load

## Progression Logic (4kg Constraint)

**Since weight is fixed at 4kg**, progression methods:
1. **Reps**: Add 1-2 reps per session until hitting top of range (20-25), then...
2. **Sets**: Add 1 set per exercise (max 5 sets)
3. **Tempo**: Slow eccentric (4-5 sec instead of 3)
4. **Rest reduction**: Drop from 60s → 45s → 30s between sets
5. **Unilateral**: Convert to single-leg/single-arm variations (e.g., split squats, 1-arm rows)
6. **Bodyweight upgrades**: Elevate feet for pike pushups, single-leg doorway rows

**Deload**: Every 6-8 weeks, reduce volume by 50% for 1 week (active recovery)
