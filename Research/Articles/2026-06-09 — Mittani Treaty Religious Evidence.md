# Mittani Treaty Religious Evidence

**Date:** 2026-06-09
**Type:** Ancient History / Comparative Religion / Textual Evidence
**Status:** verified (cuneiform tablets KBo I 3, KBo I 1)
**Confidence:** high (primary source material)

---

## Textual Hierarchy (Language Priority)

The Mitanni cuneiform documents preserve a precise linguistic hierarchy:
1. **Akkadian** — primary diplomatic language
2. **Sumerian** — literary/ritual prestige
3. **Mitanni Aryan** — Indo-Aryan elite religious vocabulary
4. **Hurrian** — indigenous substrate

This layered structure shows deliberate preservation of each tradition.

---

## Non-Aryan Deities (Hurrian/Akkadian)

These gods appear in the Mitanni tablets but are NOT part of Vedic tradition:
- **Ea** — god of wisdom/waters (Akkadian)
- **Sumuqan** — god of cattle/shepherding
- **Anu** — sky god (Akkadian)
- **Enlil** — chief god of Nippur
- **Ninlil** — Enlil's consort
- **Nabarbi** — war goddess
- **Sauska** — goddess of love/war (Hurrian)
- **Ishara** — treaty goddess
- **Pardahi** — obscure deity

## Indo-Aryan Deities (Mitanni Aryan)

### Indra

| Form | Source | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **In-da-ra** | KBo I 3 | standard phonetic rendering |
| **In-tar** | KBo I 1 | abbreviated form |

### Varuna

| Form | Source | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **A-ru-na** | KBo I 3 | possibly influenced by Hittite "aruna" (sea) |
| **Ú-ru-wa-na** | KBo I 1 | fuller form |

**Note:** "Aruna" appears to be a scribal peculiarity — the Hittite word for "sea" may have contaminated the transliteration.

### Nasatyas (Asvins)

| Form | Source | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Nasattiyanna** | KBo I 3 | with Hurrian plural definite article **-nna** |

The **-nna** suffix is the Hurrian plural marker — showing these deities were understood as a group in the Hurrian linguistic context.

### Mitra

| Form | Source | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Mitrassil** | KBo I 1 | with Hurrian **dual form suffix -ssil** |

The **-ssil** suffix marks the dual number (two entities). This indicates Mitra was conceptualized as a paired deity in the Hurrian rendering — consistent with Mitra's role alongside Varuna as cosmic upholders of order.

## Invocation Formula (KBo I 3, Column 6)

The full invocation line:

```
Hani mi-it-tra-as-si-il Hani u-ru-wa-na-as-si-il ilu **inda-ra** Hani na-sa-at-ti-ya-an-na
```

Breakdown:
- **Hani** — vocative particle ("O")
- **mi-it-tra-as-si-il** — Mitra (dual form)
- **Hani u-ru-wa-na-as-si-il** — Varuna (dual form)
- **ilu** — "god"
- **inda-ra** — Indra
- **Hani na-sa-at-ti-ya-an-na** — Nasatyas (plural)

---

## Significance

1. **Earliest documented Indo-Aryan deity names** — physical cuneiform tablets, not reconstruction
2. **Dual form of Mitra** — indicates paired deity concept already established
3. **Varuna/Mitra as a pair** — matches Vedic tradition of the Asura twins
4. **Hurrian linguistic markers** — -ssil (dual), -nna (plural) show active integration of Indo-Aryan concepts into Hurrian ritual language
5. **The "Aruna" anomaly** — scribal interference from Hittite sea terminology
