# Ancient Religions Physical Artifact Archive

**Date:** 2026-06-09
**Type:** Evidence / Primary Sources / Museum Catalog
**Status:** verified (museum catalogs, digital archives)
**Confidence:** high (catalog numbers, physical locations)

---

## Core Argument

The Mitanni Treaty (~1380 BCE) is the **only physical artifact** on this list from the ancient world. Everything else — Rigveda manuscripts, Avestan manuscripts, Sanskrit inscriptions — is 1,000–2,400 years later. Physical artifact ≠ manuscript tradition.

---

## 4. Sanskrit Inscriptions — Oldest (~1st century BCE)

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Ayodhya Inscription** | ~1st century BCE, Ayodhya ( Uttar Pradesh) |
| **Hathibada Inscription** | ~1st century BCE, Rajasthan |
| **Ghosundi Inscription** | ~1st century BCE, Rajasthan |
| **Publication** | *Epigraphia Indica* (ASI series) |
| **Script** | Brahmi |
| **Language** | Sanskrit (but 500+ years AFTER claimed Vedic composition) |

**Critical note:** These are the OLDEST Sanskrit inscriptions. They are in Brahmi script, dated to ~1st century BCE. This means Sanskrit writing — and therefore Vedic text fixation — did not occur until at least 1,300 years after the claimed composition date of the Rigveda.

---

## 5. Ashoka's Edicts (~250 BCE) — Prakrit NOT Sanskrit

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Edicts** | Ashoka Rock Edicts (c. 250 BCE) |
| **Language** | **Prakrit** (Middle Indo-Aryan), NOT Sanskrit |
| **Script** | Brahmi / Kharosthi |
| **Location** | Multiple sites across India (Dhauli, Jaugada, Girnar, etc.) |
| **Significance** | Earliest writing in India — but in Prakrit, not Sanskrit |

**Why this matters:** If Ashoka (250 BCE) wrote in Prakrit, and Sanskrit inscriptions don't appear until ~1st century BCE, then the claim that the Vedas were composed in Sanskrit (1500-500 BCE) AND fixed to writing around that time is not supported by the archaeological record.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Only one true ancient artifact:** Mitanni Treaty (1380 BCE) — all others are medieval manuscripts
2. **Rigveda gap:** 1040 CE manuscript vs 1500 BCE claim = 2,500 year gap
3. **Avesta gap:** 1287-1324 CE manuscripts vs claimed ancient composition = ~1,500 year gap
4. **Sanskrit inscription gap:** Oldest Sanskrit inscription ~1st century BCE; no Sanskrit writing before ~500 BCE
5. **Ashoka wrote in Prakrit:** Earliest Indian writing is Prakrit, not Sanskrit
6. **Verification sources:** Encyclopedia Iranica, British Museum catalog, UNESCO CDNL/CDLI, ORACC
