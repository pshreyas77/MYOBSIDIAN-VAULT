| Claim Type | Status | Evidence |

## Genetic Evidence (The Decisive Layer)

### Core Papers

**1. Narasimhan et al 2019 — "The Formation of Human Populations in South and Central Asia"**
- *Science* 365(6457), 523 ancient genomes
- Establishes steppe-ancestry timeline in South Asia
- [Free full text (PMC)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6822619/)
- DOI: [10.1126/science.aat7487](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aat7487)

**2. Shinde et al 2019 — "An Ancient Harappan Genome Lacks Ancestry from Steppe Pastoralists or Iranian Farmers"**
- *Cell* 179(3) — The Rakhigarhi paper
- Proves IVC woman had **zero steppe ancestry**
- [Free full text (Harvard/Reich Lab)](https://scholar.harvard.edu/files/vagheesh/files/piis0092867419309675.pdf)
- PubMed: [31495572](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31495572/)
- DOI: [10.1016/j.cell.2019.08.048](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2019.08.048)

**3. Moorjani, Thangaraj, Patterson, Reich et al 2013**
- "Genetic Evidence for Recent Population Mixture in India"
- *AJHG* 93(3):422–438
- Established ANI-ASI mixture model
- Dated main mixing event: ~1900–4200 years ago
- PubMed: [23932107](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23932107/)

**4. Reich et al 2009 — "Reconstructing Indian Population History"**
- *Nature* 461
- Original ANI/ASI two-ancestral-population paper
- [Free full text (PMC)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2842210/)

### Key Genetic Findings

1. **IVC genome = zero steppe**: The sequenced Rakhigarhi individual (IVC, ~2500 BCE) has no steppe pastoralist ancestry. Steppe ancestry arrives **after** IVC decline.

2. **Steppe ancestry proportions**: Modern South Asians carry 10-20% steppe-derived ancestry (varies by caste, region, language group).

3. **Hard chronological anchor**: First detectable steppe ancestry in South Asia appears in **Swat Valley burials dated 1200–800 BCE** — post-IVC, post-Rigveda composition.

4. **Sex-biased migration**: Steppe ancestry shows via Y-DNA (R1a-Z93) far more than mtDNA. Pattern: predominantly male migrants mixing with local women whose maternal lineages trace to South Asian hunter-gatherers (AASI).

5. **ANI-ASI structure**: 
   - ANI (Ancestral North Indian) = IVC + steppe mixture
   - ASI (Ancestral South Indian) = IVC + AASI (Ancient Ancestral South Indian)
   - Both form primary ancestry of modern South Asians
   - IVC people are **real ancestors**, not erased

### Debate Point: Venue Gap

OIT papers (Talageri, Niraj Rai's indigenist readings) circulate in non-genetics journals or self-published volumes. Mainstream genetics papers appear in *Science*, *Cell*, *Nature* — the actual peer-reviewed consensus venues. This gap is itself worth noting.

## Linguistic Evidence (Independent of Genetics)

**Anthony & Ringe 2015** — "The Indo-European Homeland from Linguistic and Archaeological Perspectives"
- *Annual Review of Linguistics* 1:199–219
- Standard linguistic case for steppe homeland
- DOI: [10.1146/annurev-linguist-030514-124812](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-linguist-030514-124812)

### Key Linguistic Findings

1. **PIE vocabulary fits steppe, not tropics**: Proto-Indo-European has words for snow, beech trees, wheeled vehicles — none fit tropical South Asia.

2. **Archaeological match**: Earliest unambiguous evidence for wheeled vehicles + horseback riding = Pontic-Caspian steppe, matching PIE vocabulary origins.

3. **Rigvedic loanwords**: ~4% of Rigvedic vocabulary (~383 words) borrowed from Old Dravidian, Old Munda, other non-Indo-Aryan languages. Vedic speakers arrived **into** an existing linguistic landscape.

### Why OIT Fails Linguistically

Even Talageri (OIT's most serious academic defender) acknowledges Pontic Steppe as secondary homeland for Italic, Celtic, Germanic, Balto-Slavic — already concedes steppe matters. To sustain OIT, must reject comparative linguistics, stratified archaeology, AND ancient DNA simultaneously.

## Pre-Aryan India: What Was Here Before?

"Before Aryans" is not one snapshot — multiple parallel societies at different stages circa 1500 BCE.

### The Deepest Layer: AASI

**AASI (Ancestral Ancient South Indian)** = descendants of Out-of-Africa coastal migration ~65,000 years ago via Southern Dispersal route.

- **Who they are**: Genetically oldest continuous population of India
- **Modern representatives**: Paniya, Irula (southern tribal groups, highest AASI concentration today)
- **Older than**: Dravidian speakers, IVC, everyone

### Religious Life of Pre-Aryan India

| Layer | Time Depth | Evidence | Religious Features |
|-------|------------|----------|-------------------|
| **AASI / Deep Indigenous** | 65,000+ years | Bhimbetka rock shelters (Mesolithic paintings) | Animistic, dances, rituals, nature-spiritual significance |
| **IVC / Harappan** | 2600–1900 BCE | Seals, figurines, Great Bath, burials | Mother goddess cult, bull worship, tree/water reverence, ancestor worship, **no temples** |
| **Peninsular Neolithic → Megalithic** | 1200–300 BCE | Brahmagiri, Navdatoli, Chirand, Mahishadal | Burial rites, afterlife beliefs, Iron Age culture, **Dravidian-speaking, no Aryan demographic input** |

### What We Know (and Don't) About IVC Religion

**Known** (inferred from artifacts, not script — Indus script remains undeciphered):

- **Mother goddess / fertility cult**: Terracotta female figurines across village cultures (parallel to Mediterranean Neolithic)
- **Animal worship / totemism**: Bull figurines common; horned figure on seals ("Pashupati seal" — skeptical of "proto-Shiva" label, this is interpretation not fact)
- **No temples**: No Harappan building identifiable as temple; temple worship is later feature
- **Tree and water reverence**: Pipal-tree imagery on seals; Great Bath at Mohenjo-daro suggests ritual-purity practices
- **Ancestor worship + burial belief**: Consistent across IVC graves + later South Indian megaliths

**Not Present in IVC or Early Vedic**:

Temple worship, image puja, bhakti, vegetarianism, cow veneration, caste rigidity, karma/rebirth/moksha — all **absent** from earliest Vedic religion. These track to non-Vedic indigenous substratum (Dravidian, Munda folk religion), synthesized later (4th–15th centuries CE).

## What "Total India" Looked Like ~1500 BCE

| Region | Culture | Characteristics | Aryan Contact |
|--------|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Northwest** | Late/declining IVC, OCP culture | De-urbanized since ~1900 BCE | Direct contact, migration route |
| **Central India** | Chalcolithic farming cultures | Brahmagiri, Navdatoli, Chirand — regionally distinct | Limited contact |
| **South India (Deccan + Peninsula)** | Independent Neolithic → Megalithic Iron Age | Mixed agro-pastoral, professional potters, Iron Age ~1200–300 BCE | **No demographic Aryan input** — only later cultural/linguistic influence via contact, trade, Sanskritization |

**Critical fact**: South India was never "Aryan-ized" demographically. Megalith builders practiced elaborate burial rites, had afterlife beliefs, built their own traditions parallel to Vedic north.

## Out-of-India Theory: Why It Doesn't Hold

1. **Genetic incompatibility**: OIT requires steppe ancestry to be **in** IVC genomes. It is not. Rakhigarhi = zero steppe. This alone falsifies OIT.

2. **Linguistic convergence**: PIE vocabulary, archaeological correlates (wheeled vehicles, horses), and genetics all independently point to steppe homeland with eastward/westward expansions 3rd–2nd millennium BCE.

3. **Even OIT defenders concede**: Talageri accepts Pontic Steppe for European IE branches — already undermines "India = sole homeland" claim.

4. **Fringe status**: 2022 Indo-Iranian linguistics conference summary: OIT stems mainly from absence of direct invasion evidence (true — invasion is dead), but migration evidence is overwhelming. Most linguists reject OIT.

5. **Venue gap**: OIT papers circulate in non-genetics venues; mainstream consensus in *Science*/*Cell*/*Nature*.
