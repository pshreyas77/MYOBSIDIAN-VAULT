## Sanskrit Origins

**Term**: श्रेयस् (śreyas)
**Root**: From Proto-Indo-Iranian *ćráyas, from PIE *h₂ḱréys "to be superior"
**Etymology**: Comparative form of श्री (śrī) = "prosperity, beauty, auspiciousness, good fortune"
**Grammar**: Neuter substantivized comparative adjective

### Core Meanings
- "Better" or "superior"
- "More excellent" or "more auspicious"
- "The best" or "the most fortunate"
- "That which leads to higher existence / spiritual welfare"

### Famous Vedic Usage
In the Katha Upaniṣad (1.2.1–2), śreyas is contrasted with preya (the pleasant):

> śreyas ca preya ca manuṣyām / etas tau samparītya vivinakti dhīraḥ / śreyo hi dhīro 'bhipreyaso vṛṇīte / preyo mando yogakṣemād vṛṇīte

"The good and the pleasant are both presented to a person. Having examined them, the discerning one tells them apart: the wise chooses the good in preference to the pleasant."

### Cultural Context
- Etymologically related to Goddess Lakṣmī (Hindu goddess of wealth, fortune, prosperity)
- Considered a name that invokes divine blessings and positive energy
- Direct descendant of Proto-Indo-Iranian root shared with Avestan šreyas

## Proto-Indo-Iranian Family Tree

```
Proto-Indo-European
    └── Proto-Indo-Iranian
            ├── Indo-Aryan Branch
            │       ├── Vedic Sanskrit (śreyas)
            │       ├── Classical Sanskrit (śreyas)
            │       ├── Middle Indo-Aryan (Pali: seyyo/seṭṭho)
            │       └── Modern Indo-Aryan (Hindi, Bengali, Marathi, etc.)
            │
            └── Iranian Branch
                    ├── Avestan (šreyas)
                    └── Old Persian (parallel development)
```

**Key insight**: Avestan is NOT an Indo-Aryan language — it belongs to the Iranian branch. Sanskrit and Avestan are sister languages sharing a common Proto-Indo-Iranian ancestor, not a parent-child relationship.

## Avestan (Old Iranian)

**Form**: šreyas (𐬱𐬭𐬀𐬌𐬫𐬱)
**Script**: Avestan alphabet
**Meaning**: Better, superior, excellent; auspicious, prosperous

### Semantic Field
- Comparative excellence — "better than, superior to"
- Spiritual prosperity — that which benefits the soul
- Auspiciousness — bringing good fortune and positive outcomes
- Material welfare — promoting well-being and success

### Cultural Context
In Zoroastrian sacred texts (Avesta), šreyas aligns with the pursuit of Asha (truth, righteousness, cosmic order) — the ultimate good and excellence in existence. The semantic field remains virtually identical to Sanskrit śreyas, reflecting the shared Indo-Iranian heritage.

---

## Middle Indo-Aryan: Pali

**Forms**: सेय्यो (seyyo), सेट्ठो (seṭṭho)
**Meaning**: "Better" (comparative), "Best" (superlative)

### Buddhist Context
In the Pali Canon, these forms represent the same conceptual field as Sanskrit śreyas — the spiritual choice of the higher good over mere pleasantness. The Kaṭhopaniṣad's śreyas/preyas distinction is echoed in Buddhist teachings on right action vs. immediate gratification.

## Dravidian Languages

> **Important**: Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, and Kannada are Dravidian languages, NOT Indo-Aryan. The name Shreyas is borrowed from Sanskrit in these languages and does not have native Dravidian etymological equivalents.

### Tamil (தமிழ்)
**Form**: ஸ்ரேயஸ் (Srēyas)
**Native equivalents**: 
- சிறந்த (Sirantha) — "better, excellent, superior"
- மங்கலான (Mangalana) — "auspicious, fortunate"
- நன்மை (Nanmai) — "goodness, welfare"
**Note**: Direct Sanskrit borrowing; carries same positive connotations in Tamil Hindu context

### Telugu (తెలుగు)
**Form**: శ్రేయస్ (Śrēyas)
**Native equivalents**:
- మేలు (Melu) — "good, welfare"
- మంగలం (Mangalam) — "auspiciousness, good fortune"
- శుభం (Shubham) — "prosperity, benevolence"
**Note**: Sanskrit borrowing; understood as "that which is spiritually or materially beneficial"

### Malayalam (മലയാളം)
**Form**: ശ്രേയസ് (Śrēyas)
**Native equivalents**:
- മേലാനത്തെ (Mēlānatte) — "the better/superior one"
- ശുഭകരമായ (Śubhakaramāya) — "auspicious, fortunate"
- ക്ഷേമം (Kṣēmaṃ) — "well-being, prosperity"
**Note**: Sanskrit borrowing common in Kerala Hindu naming; associated with blessings of Lakṣmī

### Kannada (ಕನ್ನಡ)
**Form**: ಶ್ರೇಯಸ್ (Śrēyas)
**Native equivalents**:
- ಶ್ರೇಷ್ಠ (Śrēṣṭha) — "excellent, superior"
- ಮಂಗಳಕರ (Maṅgạlakara) — "auspicious"
- ಕಲ್ಯಾಣ (Kalyāṇa) — "welfare, prosperity"
**Note**: Sanskrit borrowing; signifies the highest good or spiritual benefit

## Semantic Stability Across Families

### Core Meaning Preservation
The name consistently conveys across all language families:
- Superiority or excellence
- Auspiciousness
- Positive spiritual/moral quality

### Key Variations by Family
| Family | Emphasis |
|---|---|
| Indo-Aryan (Vedic) | "The higher good" — spiritual welfare vs. pleasantness |
| Avestan | "Truth/righteousness (Asha) — cosmic order excellence |
| Pali | "Best" in Buddhist spiritual hierarchy |
| Dravidian | Borrowed prestige — Sanskrit-derived scholarly term |
| Mandarin | Happiness, good fortune, auspiciousness |

### Conclusion
The fundamental meaning of "excellent/best/auspicious" remains remarkably consistent across all language families examined. The conceptual emphasis varies by cultural context — Sanskrit emphasizes comparative spiritual superiority, Chinese emphasizes fortune and happiness — but the positive semantic core is universal.
