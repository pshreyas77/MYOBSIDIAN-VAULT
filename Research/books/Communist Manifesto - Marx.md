# Communist Manifesto — Karl Marx & Friedrich Engels

## For future Claude
Core text of scientific socialism. Published 1848. Argues class struggle is the engine of history, capitalism creates its own downfall, and workers of the world must unite. Required reading for understanding 20th-century politics.

## Summary
**Main thesis:** "The history of all society is the history of class struggles." Economic systems rise and fall through conflict between owning classes and working classes.

## Key Arguments

### 1. Class Struggle Drives History
Every civilization had ruling and subject classes in conflict:
- Ancient Rome: patricians vs slaves
- Feudalism: lords vs serfs
- Capitalism: bourgeoisie vs proletariat

### 2. Rise of the Bourgeoisie
Merchants and business owners became the new ruling class through:
- Trade expansion
- Colonial exploitation
- Industrial machines
- Factories and mass production
- Global markets

Marx actually ADMIRES capitalism's transformative power — it created unprecedented industry, connected the world, advanced science rapidly.

### 3. Capitalism's Destabilizing Nature
**"All that is solid melts into air"** — capitalism constantly destroys old traditions, jobs, and social structures.
- Local shops replaced by malls
- Traditional crafts replaced by assembly lines
- Villages becoming cities
- Nothing stays stable

### 4. Proletarian Exploitation
Factory workers:
- Lose individuality
- Do repetitive, fragmented tasks
- Create wealth but don't own the system
- Modern parallel: gig workers, warehouse scanners, assembly lines

### 5. Capitalist Crises
- Factories produce more goods
- Workers are paid too little to buy
- Economy crashes from overproduction
- Examples: Great Depression, recessions, financial crashes

### 6. Revolution Inevitable
**"The bourgeoisie produces its own gravediggers"** — workers become numerous, organized, and aware of exploitation. This leads to working-class revolution.

## Marx's Historical Flow
```
Feudalism → Merchants gain power → Capitalism destroys feudalism
→ Industry & globalization → Worker exploitation → Class conflict
→ Worker revolution → (predicted) socialism/communism
```

## What Marx Predicted
- Globalization before it existed
- Every country forced into world trade
- Isolated local economies destroyed
- Recurring economic crises from overproduction
- Concentration of wealth in few hands

## Criticisms
- Capitalism didn't collapse — it adapted and reformed
- Middle class grew substantially
- Workers in many countries gained rights through reform, not revolution
- Communist states often became authoritarian bureaucracies

## Famous Quotes
> "The history of all society is the history of class struggles."
> "The bourgeoisie produces its own gravediggers."
> "All that is solid melts into air."
> "Workers of the world, unite!"
