- Nahali-type language isolates, Munda substrate

- Zero steppe ancestry [GENETIC FACT]

- Rakhigarhi skeleton = zero Steppe ancestry

- Sintashta (~2100–1800 BCE) = Proto-Indo-Iranian chariot + fire ritual

- Mitanni treaty 1380 BCE (Syria) — physical cuneiform inscription
- Kikkuli horse training manual ~1345 BCE (Bogazkoy)

- Oldest Rigveda manuscript: 1040 CE

- Rigveda describes pastoral society, ruined cities (armaka) → post-IVC

## Contradictions Resolved

### Sunny's Migration Timing Claim
**Claim**: Aryan migration happened AFTER Buddha (~500 BCE)
**Verdict**: ❌ WRONG on migration timing, ✅ RIGHT on absorption timing

**Resolution**:
- Migration = 2000–1500 BCE (genetic fact, cannot be post-Buddha)
- Absorption/synthesis = 500 BCE–800 CE (Sunny correctly identified this)
- Two separate events conflated into one

### Buddha/Mahavira = Aryan?
**Claim**: Buddha/Mahavira were Aryans (born in north India, spoke Indo-Aryan Prakrit)
**Verdict**: PARTIAL — depends on definition of "Aryan"

| Definition | Buddha = Aryan? |
|------------|-----------------|
| Genetic (steppe ancestry) | PARTIAL — everyone in north India was mixed by 500 BCE |
| Linguistic (speaks Indo-Aryan) | PROBABLY YES — Magadhi Prakrit = Indo-Aryan derived |
| Cultural/Vedic (follows Vedic ritual) | ❌ HARD NO — explicitly rejected Vedas, fire sacrifice |
| Philosophical (substrate origin) | ❌ NO — pre-Aryan indigenous northeast (Zimmer + Bronkhorst) |

**Conclusion**: Being born in post-migration north India ≠ being culturally Aryan. Shramana movement = pre-Aryan indigenous tradition that survived Aryan cultural overlay.
